# US Flagship Models (2025–2026)

## Overview

US AI labs (OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Anthropic, xAI, Meta) dominate the global AI landscape. Most flagship closed-source models **do not disclose parameter counts**, focusing instead on capabilities, API pricing, and benchmark performance.

<Callout type="warning" emoji="📊">
**Key Pattern:** US closed-source labs (OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, xAI) → parameters often undisclosed. US open-weight labs (Meta Llama, Mistral) → parameters typically disclosed.
</Callout>

## Key Observations

### Closed-Source Giants Keep Parameters Secret

- **OpenAI**: Has not officially confirmed GPT-4's ~1.8T parameter count (widely cited from third-party analysis)
- **Google**: Gemini 1.5 Pro's ~540B estimate comes from LLM-Bento and similar databases
- **Anthropic**: Claude series parameters are explicitly "not disclosed" in official documentation
- **xAI**: Grok-1's 314B is an estimate; Grok-2 has no reliable public parameter data

### Open-Weight Models Are Transparent

Meta and Mistral publish parameter counts as a competitive advantage:
- **Llama 3.1 405B**: Largest open-weight dense model
- **Mistral Large 2**: 123B with strong reasoning capabilities

## Trend: From "Bigger is Better" to "More Efficient"

<Callout type="default" emoji="📈">
**2018–2022**: Dense models dominated — GPT-3 (175B), PaLM (540B), GLM-130B (130B)

**2023–present**: MoE architecture allows massive total parameters with controlled activation:
- GPT-4 estimated at ~1.8T total / ~120B active per token
- Enables trillion-parameter class models at reasonable inference cost
</Callout>

## Why Don't US Labs Disclose Parameters?

1. **Competitive secrecy**: Parameter counts reveal architecture scale and capabilities
2. **Focus on capabilities**: Emphasize what models can do, not how big they are
3. **MoE complexity**: Total parameters ≠ active parameters, making single numbers misleading
4. **Regulatory considerations**: Larger models face more scrutiny
