# ChatGPT

> _The moment AI went mainstream_

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Release Date** | November 30, 2022 |
| **Organization** | OpenAI |
| **Based On** | GPT-3.5 (fine-tuned for dialogue) |
| **Access** | Public (free during research preview) |

## Origin Story

ChatGPT wasn't built from scratch — it was a fine-tuned version of GPT-3.5, optimized for conversational interactions. OpenAI gathered human feedback through Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) to make it safer and more helpful.

<Callout type="success" emoji="🎯">
Reached 1 million users in 5 days. 100 million users by January 2023.
</Callout>

## Key Capabilities

- **Conversational AI**: Natural dialogue, follow-up questions, admit mistakes
- **Wide knowledge**: Science, history, programming, creative writing
- **Safety training**: Refused harmful requests, declined to provide dangerous information
- **Accessibility**: Simple web interface, no technical knowledge required

## Impact

ChatGPT's success proved that:
1. **AI could be consumer-friendly** — not just for researchers and engineers
2. **Conversational interfaces** were the killer app for AI
3. **Public adoption** could happen almost overnight

This triggered:
- **Google Code Red** — accelerated Bard development
- **Microsoft's billions** — $10 billion investment in OpenAI
- **Global AI race** — every major tech company entered the fray
