# Theoretical computer science

The mathematical spine: **what can be computed**, in **how much time and space**—often the most “CS-core” department track.

---

## Sub-areas

| Topic | Typical questions |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Algorithms** | Approximation, online, randomized, parallel |
| **Complexity** | P vs NP, circuit lower bounds, communication complexity |
| **Discrete structures** | Graphs, combinatorics, coding |
| **Formal methods** | Model checking, theorem proving, program semantics |
| **Automata & logic** | Verification, database theory, crypto foundations |

---

## Overlap with AI

**Learning theory** (generalization, optimization landscapes) is its own conference ecosystem; classical TCS still anchors **cryptography (incl. post-quantum)**, **quantum computing**, and **verification**. [AI Models](/en/ais/overview) focuses on **releases and capabilities**; this page stays **traditional CS theory** framing.
