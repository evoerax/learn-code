# NOAICS: Mainstream directions in a CS department (beyond an AI-only lens)

**NOAICS** is this site’s label for:

> **Everything that remains a long-running pillar of computer science departments once you park “models / training / agentic software stacks” under [AI Models](/en/ais/overview), [AI Software](/en/software/overview), and [Datasets](/en/datasets/overview).**

It complements **[Hardware](/en/hardware/overview)**: hardware here emphasizes full machines and embedded stacks; **NOAICS** stays closer to the **CS departmental** taxonomy and explicitly discusses overlap with AI.

---

## Why a separate section?

- **AI did not erase the rest of the map.** In widely used taxonomies—[ACM CCS](https://dlccsdev.acm.org/) top-level areas, many national recommendation lists, PhD track pages—**systems, networking, security, data, software engineering, theory, HCI, and graphics** remain first-class.
- **In 2026 the story is often “AI raised the floor for foundations.”** Training, inference, and agents fail first on **systems, data platforms, security, and platform engineering**. Those are still core CS—even when marketing calls them “AI infra.”

## Three compression lenses

### “Classic hard pillars” (excluding AI as its own mega-tab)

Often listed: **systems · networking · databases · security · software engineering · theory · graphics / HCI** (robotics & cross-cuts vary by school).

### Industrial salience

Frequently most visible: **AI · distributed systems · data platforms · security · software/platform engineering · networking**.

### Academic conference flavor

Often emphasized: **theory · systems · security · graphics · HCI · AI** (plus cross-cuts per lab culture).

---

## Four-layer mental model

1. **Theory** — algorithms, complexity, logic & formal methods  
2. **Systems substrate** — architecture, OS, compilers, networking, DB kernels  
3. **Platforms & engineering** — distributed systems, SE, security, cloud & data platforms  
4. **Interaction & applications** — graphics, HCI, robotics, domain computing  

AI threads through all layers; **models & training narratives** live in this site’s AI sections, while **scalable trustworthy infrastructure** mostly layers 2–3.

## A 2026 reality slice (public sources)

- Taxonomies such as **[ACM CCS](https://dlccsdev.acm.org/)** still list **systems, networks, security, information systems, software, theory, human-centered computing**, etc., as peers—not a single AI bucket swallowing everything.  
- **Systems / distributed / storage / cloud:** AI scale pushes bottlenecks back to throughput, scheduling, I/O, and resilience—classic systems.  
- **Software engineering:** often discussed alongside **platform engineering** and sustainable delivery; see Thoughtworks [Looking Glass 2026](https://www.thoughtworks.com/insights/looking-glass).  
- **Security:** cloud & agentic deployments foreground identity, automation, and supply chains; organizations such as [Cloud Security Alliance](https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/) publish ongoing trend commentary.  
- **Data systems:** streaming, lakehouse, governance, and “data as infrastructure” remain central industry narratives.  
- **HCI:** shipping LLM products surfaces interaction, control, and trust problems—not only model scaling.  

For a Chinese-context recommendation map, many readers still consult the **[CCF conference/journal lists](https://ccf.atom.im/)** when aligning papers and venues.

## Non-model directions with long leverage (5–10y intuition)

Heuristic study/job bets (not a formulaic ranking):

- **Systems + AI infrastructure**  
- **Security** (identity, supply chain, cloud & agent governance)  
- **Data systems** (real-time stacks, governance, retrieval)  
- **Platform engineering / software engineering**  
- **Compilers & performance engineering**  
- **Robotics / embedded / CPS**  
- **HCI & multimodal interaction**
