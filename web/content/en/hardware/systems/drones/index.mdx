## Subsystem Chain

The drone subsystem chain runs in both directions — mechanical loads drive motor and battery requirements, while the flight controller closes loops through the ESC to command torque output at the propellers:

**Mechanical path:** Airframe → Brushless motor → ESC → Propeller → Battery (BMS) → Connectors and power distribution

**Control path:** Flight controller → IMU → ESC → Motor → Propeller thrust → Airframe dynamics → IMU (closes loop)

Each link has its own design trade-offs covered in the three-level pages: [Airframe & propulsion](/en/hardware/systems/drones/airframe) covers motor KV, propeller pitch, C-rate, and connector sizing; [Flight control](/en/hardware/systems/drones/flight_control) covers IMU damping, nested loop bandwidth, and log analysis; [Links & safety](/en/hardware/systems/drones/link_safety) covers RC protocol selection, failsafe state machines, and airspace compliance.

---

## Control Hierarchy (conceptual)

- **Inner loop**: Rate / attitude — the flight controller maintains angular stability at hundreds of Hz.
- **Outer loop**: Position / trajectory — waypoint navigation and wind rejection require good state estimation and a tighter IMU budget than inner loop alone.

---

## Safety and Regulations

Arming logic, fail-safe landing, propeller guards, and test-site discipline vary by country. Airspace, weight class, and visual-line-of-sight rules are universal constraints for any outdoor deployment. Production hardware adds certification (CE / FCC / country-specific aviation authority) on top of the engineering baseline.

---

## Learning Path

Kit assembly → log analysis → propeller tuning → frame balancing → custom flight controller firmware. Resist writing your own flight controller before understanding why the existing firmware fails.
