# Automotive

The **Automotive** section (汽车脉络) is the automotive track of the [Whole Systems](/en/hardware/systems) layer. ## How Automotive Differs from Drones / Robotics

- **Electrical environment**: surge tolerance, wide temperature range, and EMC compliance are non-negotiable. The 12V/24V automotive bus sees transients that hobby systems never encounter.
- **Safety**: Functional safety (ISO 26262) and hardware **redundancy** are hard certification thresholds for production vehicles — not optional hardening.
- **Regulatory**: Road homologation, production audits, and type approval far exceed the barriers for hobby or research prototypes.

---

## Hardware Modules (Entry-Level Map)

- **Body domain**: BCM (body control module), lighting, comfort electronics, LIN/CAN peripherals.
- **Powertrain domain**: engine management, transmission control, hybrid / EV battery management system (BMS).
- **Chassis / drive-by-wire**: steer-by-wire, brake-by-wire, drive-by-wire — the interface philosophy between driver intent and actuator control.
- **ADAS sensors**: millimeter-wave radar, forward camera, ultrasonic parking sensors, and the sensor fusion domain controller.

---

## What an Individual Can Do

- **Campus / closed-course prototypes**: drive-by-wire small vehicle, domain-controller test bench, CAN bus logging and replay.
- **Production vehicles**: typically completed within an enterprise V-cycle; this section focuses on **terminology and architecture** aligned with industry practice rather than building a road-legal car from scratch.

---

## Subsystem Chain

The automotive subsystem chain flows in both directions — power and communication infrastructure enable ADAS perception, which in turn commands by-wire actuation to execute longitudinal and lateral control:

**Power & communication path:** 12V/48V power bus → onboard networks (CAN/LIN/Ethernet) → domain controllers → ADAS sensor fusion

**Actuation path:** ADAS perception & planning → by-wire actuators (steering, braking, throttle) → vehicle dynamics → tires → road surface feedback

**Data flow:** Onboard networks carry sensor data (radar, camera, ultrasonic) to the ADAS domain controller, which fuses perception results and emits actuator commands over the same bus infrastructure. By-wire actuators then execute those commands while real-time feedback (wheel speed, yaw rate, steering angle) closes the loop back through onboard networks to the ADAS controller.

Each link has its own design trade-offs covered in the three-level pages: [Onboard networks & power](/en/hardware/systems/automotive/onboard_network) covers 12V/48V配电, CAN/LIN/FlexRay/Ethernet bus architecture, and UDS/OBD-II diagnostics; [Drive-by-wire & actuation](/en/hardware/systems/automotive/by_wire) covers steer-by-wire, brake-by-wire, ISO 26262 ASIL architecture, and fail-operational redundancy; [ADAS sensors](/en/hardware/systems/automotive/adas) covers millimeter-wave radar, forward camera, ultrasonic sensors, sensor fusion, and edge SoC vs domain controller placement.
