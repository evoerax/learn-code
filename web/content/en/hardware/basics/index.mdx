# Foundations

The **Foundations** level is organized into six second-level chapters arranged in a dependency order: first learning to read circuits and power systems, then running real-time peripherals on an MCU, then connecting physical quantities to the system through actuators and sensors, and finally using control laws and buses to link subsystems into a closed loop. When cross-referencing with a **finer-grained skills checklist**, consult the [Full Mastery Roadmap](/en/hardware/progression/roadmap) in the Progression level (layers II–VII roughly correspond to the six chapters below).

---

## Second-Level Chapter Guide

| Order | Page | Problem Solved |
|-------|------|----------------|
| 1 | [Circuits & Electronics](/en/hardware/basics/electronics) | Is energy and signal safe and measurable |
| 2 | [Embedded & MCU](/en/hardware/basics/embedded) | Is computation and timing deterministic |
| 3 | [Actuators & Power](/en/hardware/basics/actuation) | How commands become force and motion |
| 4 | [Sensors & Perception](/en/hardware/basics/sensing) | Where state observations come from |
| 5 | [Control & Estimation](/en/hardware/basics/control) | How feedback stabilizes the loop |
| 6 | [Buses & Communication](/en/hardware/basics/communication) | How modules synchronize and achieve redundancy |
