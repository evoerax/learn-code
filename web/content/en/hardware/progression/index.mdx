# Progression track

The **Progression** section sits between **Foundations** and **Systems**: where Foundations builds the six core chapters (circuits, embedded, actuation, sensing, control, communication), Systems dives into drones, automotive, and robotics integration. Progression fills the **wider curriculum** that does not fit neatly into a single chapter—math foundations, mechanical design, AI on edge, engineering practice, and the interactive **[Mastery roadmap](/en/hardware/progression/roadmap)**.

The roadmap maps eleven knowledge layers with 200+ topics. It shares the same checklist with the Chinese site (labels differ by language), and your progress is saved in the browser's localStorage.

## Suggested reading order

1. **Foundations first.** Complete most of the [six Foundations chapters](/en/hardware/basics) before using the roadmap; self-auditing against a structured checklist is far more effective once you have hands-on context.
2. **Check gaps before Systems.** Before deep-diving into [drones](/en/hardware/systems/drones), [automotive](/en/hardware/systems/automotive), or [robotics](/en/hardware/systems/robotics), review roadmap layers **VIII (Mechanical design)**, **IX (AI & algorithms)**, and **XI (Engineering practice)** to identify gaps.
3. **Layers I and IX are portable.** Math foundations (calculus, probability, signals & systems) and AI/algorithms pair well with external textbooks. Study these in parallel with the site using [Programming languages](/en/timeline), [AI models](/en/ais/overview), and [Datasets](/en/datasets/overview) as anchors.
