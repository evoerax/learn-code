# Shopify

Shopify Inc. (TSX: SHOP, Nasdaq: SHOP) is a Canadian multinational e-commerce company headquartered in Ottawa, Ontario, founded in **2006** by **Tobias Lütke**, **Daniel Weinand**, and **Scott Lake**. What began as a solution to the founders' own frustration with existing e-commerce tools — Lütke built the platform originally to sell snowboarding equipment online under the name Snowdevil in 2004 — has become the dominant SaaS commerce platform for small and medium-sized businesses worldwide. Shopify powers over **5 million merchants** and facilitated **$292.3 billion in Gross Merchandise Value (GMV)** in 2024, serving customers including Tesla, LVMH, Nestlé, PepsiCo, and Whole Foods. With **$11.6 billion in revenue** and **$1.23 billion in net income** in FY2025, and 7,600 employees globally, Shopify is Canada's most valuable technology company and one of the world's leading commerce platform ecosystems.

## Timeline

### 2004–2009: From Snowdevil to Platform

- **2004**: **Tobias Lütke**, a German-Canadian computer programmer, launches **Snowdevil** — an online store selling snowboarding equipment and apparel. Dissatisfied with existing e-commerce solutions, Lütke builds his own platform using **Ruby on Rails**. The store sells snowboarding-related merchandise and gives Lütke the foundational experience to build the Shopify platform.
- **2006 (June)**: Lütke, along with **Daniel Weinand** (also a developer and snowboarder) and **Scott Lake**, formally incorporates **Shopify** in Ottawa. The initial product is a hosted shopping cart platform that allows anyone to set up an online store quickly. The platform introduces **Liquid** — a custom template language written in Ruby — which remains Shopify's core templating engine to this day. The name "Shopify" reflects the mission: to make commerce easier ("shop" made simple with "ify").
- **2007**: Shopify launches its first theme gallery, establishing the precedent of separating design from functionality — merchants can change storefront appearance without touching underlying code.
- **2008**: Shopify opens its **App Store** (initially called "Plugins"), allowing third-party developers to extend platform functionality. This seeds the developer ecosystem that would become a major competitive moat.
- **2009 (June)**: Launches **Shopify API platform** and App Store, enabling external developers to build applications that integrate with Shopify stores. This was remarkably early for SaaS API ecosystems — predating the broader API economy by several years.

### 2010–2015: Growth, IPO, and the Plus Tier

- **2010 (January)**: Launches **Build-A-Business competition** — a global contest encouraging entrepreneurs to build businesses on Shopify, with winners receiving mentorship and cash prizes. This seeding strategy built an entire generation of Shopify evangelists.
- **2010 (December)**: Raises **$7 million Series A** funding at a $20 million pre-money valuation, led by Bessemer Venture Partners.
- **2011 (October)**: Raises **$15 million Series B** funding. The platform reaches 30,000 active merchants.
- **2013 (August)**: Launches **Shopify Payments** — an integrated payment processing solution that eliminates the need for third-party payment gateways. Also launches **Shopify Point-of-Sale (POS)** system, extending the platform into physical retail.
- **2013 (December)**: Raises **$100 million Series C** funding at a $1 billion valuation, making Shopify Canada's first unicorn tech company.
- **2014 (February)**: Launches **Shopify Plus** — an enterprise-tier platform designed for high-volume merchants and brands, competing directly with platforms like Magento Enterprise. Target customers include large brands migrating from legacy e-commerce systems.
- **2015 (May 21)**: **Goes public on NYSE** via IPO, raising over **$131 million**. Shares priced at $17, close at $31 on day one. The IPO marks Shopify as one of Canada's most successful tech listings. Ticker: SHOP on NYSE, later SHOP on TSX as dual listing.

### 2016–2020: Capital, Logistics, and Remote Work

- **2016 (April)**: Launches **Shopify Capital** — a cash advance product providing merchants with working capital based on their sales history. This diversifies revenue beyond subscriptions and transaction fees, extending into financial services.
- **2016 (October)**: Acquires **Boltmade** — a Canadian software consultancy, strengthening internal product development capabilities.
- **2017 (January)**: Integrates **Amazon selling** directly into Shopify, allowing merchants to list on Amazon from their Shopify dashboard — a strategic move to position Shopify as platform-agnostic rather than competitive with Amazon.
- **2017 (April)**: Rebrands Shopify Pay as **Shop Pay** — a one-click checkout solution that saves customer information for faster future purchases. By 2024, Shop Pay has over **150 million users** worldwide.
- **2018 (October)**: Opens first **flagship retail store in Los Angeles** — demonstrating Shopify's commitment to the omnichannel commerce vision and providing a physical showcase for the platform's capabilities.
- **2019 (January)**: Launches **Shopify Studios** — an in-house creative agency to help brands build content and marketing campaigns on the platform.
- **2019**: Acquires **Handshake** — a B2B e-commerce platform for wholesale sales, expanding Shopify beyond purely D2C (direct-to-consumer) into B2B commerce.
- **2019**: Acquires **6 River Systems** for **$450 million** — a warehouse robotics and fulfillment automation company. This was Shopify's largest acquisition at the time and signaled serious ambition in logistics and fulfillment.
- **2020 (May)**: Declares **permanent remote work** policy for all employees — becoming one of the first major tech companies to formalize post-pandemic remote work permanently, eliminating costly office real estate.
- **2020 (February)**: Joins the **Diem Association** (formerly Libra Association) — Facebook's controversial stablecoin project. Shopify later exits this initiative.

### 2021–2023: Logistics Spinoff and Workforce Reductions

- **2021 (June)**: Removes **20% revenue share for app developers earning under $1 million** annually — a major concession to the developer ecosystem that encourages app ecosystem growth and innovation.
- **2021**: Acquires **Primer** — an augmented reality (AR) app for e-commerce, strengthening Shopify's visual commerce capabilities.
- **2022 (January)**: Announces **strategic partnership with JD.com**, China's largest retailer, to enable Chinese merchants to sell globally via Shopify's platform.
- **2022 (July)**: **Lays off 10% of workforce** (~1,000 employees) — citing overhiring during the COVID-19 e-commerce boom. This follows a broader tech sector correction as pandemic-driven growth normalizes.
- **2022 (August)**: Makes **$100 million strategic investment in Klaviyo** — a customer relationship management (CRM) and email marketing platform for e-commerce, deepening the partnership ecosystem.
- **2022 (September)**: Acquires **Remix** — the open-source React-based web framework. Shopify had already been using Remix internally for its Oxygen storefront infrastructure and chooses to acquire and fund the core team directly.
- **2022**: Acquires **Dovetale** — an influencer marketing platform for e-commerce brands.
- **2022**: Acquires **Deliverr** for **$2.1 billion** — a fulfillment network and inventory management platform designed to compete with Amazon's FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) by offering fast delivery guarantees for Shopify merchants.
- **2023 (May)**: **Sells Shopify Logistics (including Deliverr and 6 River Systems) to Flexport** for a stake in Flexport. This represents a significant strategic pivot — having built up logistics and fulfillment capabilities, Shopify decides to divest this capital-intensive business to focus on its core platform and instead partner with Flexport. The deal includes taking a ~13% equity stake in Flexport.
- **2023 (May)**: **Lays off 20% of workforce** (~2,000 employees) in coordination with the Flexport deal, as the logistics business is separated. This second round of layoffs within a year signals significant restructuring.

### 2024–2026: Nasdaq-100, AI, and Universal Commerce

- **2024**: Shopify's App Store grows to over **16,000 apps**, providing extensive ecosystem optionality for merchants across marketing, inventory, shipping, accounting, and customer service.
- **2024**: **Shopify Editions** — a new product announcement format where Shopify reveals multiple new features twice yearly, akin to Apple's WWDC keynotes. The Spring 2024 edition introduced Sidekick (AI commerce assistant), Shopify Credit (business credit card), and expanded fulfillment network integrations.
- **2024**: **Shopify Collabs** — a creator commerce platform enabling influencers to earn commissions by promoting merchant products directly through the platform.
- **2025 (March)**: Transfers **US listing from NYSE to Nasdaq**, aligning with the tech sector's primary exchange.
- **2025 (May)**: Joins the **Nasdaq-100** index — a significant milestone reflecting Shopify's maturation from growth stock to established tech blue chip. The company also becomes a component of the S&P/TSX 60 in Canada.
- **2025 (September)**: Becomes a component of the **Nasdaq-100** — marking formal recognition as one of the world's most valuable technology companies.
- **2025**: Announces **$2 billion share buyback program** — signaling confidence in long-term value creation and returning capital to shareholders.
- **2026 (January)**: Announces **Universal Commerce Protocol** with **Google** — a cross-platform standard allowing merchants to seamlessly sell across multiple online marketplaces and platforms from a single Shopify dashboard. This positions Shopify as the operating system for multi-platform commerce rather than just a standalone storefront.
- **2026**: Launches **Shopify Bundles** — a native product bundling tool, and expands **Shopify Magic** (AI features) across the merchant dashboard, including AI-generated product descriptions, email marketing, and customer insights.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Commerce Platform (Subscription Revenue)
- **Shopify Basic/Sstandard/Plus**: Tiered subscription plans ranging from $29/month (Basic) to $2,500/month (Shopify Plus), providing hosted online store capabilities, templates, payment processing, and analytics
- **Shopify Lite**: $9/month plan for embedding commerce into existing websites, blogs, or social media — allows merchants to accept payments without a full Shopify store
- **Shopify Plus**: Enterprise tier for high-volume merchants (>$1M GMV/year) — dedicated infrastructure, custom checkout, wholesale channels (Handshake), and priority support

### Payment and Financial Services
- **Shop Pay**: One-click accelerated checkout that saves payment and shipping information. Over 150 million users as of 2024. Available on any website (not just Shopify stores), making it a standalone payment product competing with PayPal and Apple Pay.
- **Shopify Payments**: Integrated payment processing avoiding third-party gateways; Shopify charges 0.5-2% per transaction on top of payment network fees
- **Shopify Capital**: Cash advances to merchants based on sales history — advances range from $200 to $2 million, repaid as a percentage of daily sales. Over $3 billion advanced since 2016.
- **Shopify Credit**: Business credit card launched 2024 — targeting merchants with cash-back rewards and expense management features

### App Store and Ecosystem
- **Shopify App Store**: Over 16,000 apps (2025) spanning marketing (Klaviyo, Yotpo), inventory management (Skubana, DEAR), shipping (ShipStation, Sendle), accounting (QuickBooks, Xero), customer service (Gorgias, Reamaze), and more
- **Flow**: Native workflow automation tool allowing merchants to create automated sequences without code
- **Shopify Functions**: Server-side logic allowing merchants to customize checkout, discounts, shipping rates, and payment processing at the code level

### Logistics and Fulfillment
- **Shopify Fulfillment Network (SFN)**: Managed fulfillment service using a network of third-party warehouses. Launched 2019, expanded with Deliverr in 2022, then sold to Flexport in 2023.
- **Shopify Balance**: Business banking account for merchants — FDIC-insured, no monthly fees, with cash advances
- **Shopify Shipping**: Discounted shipping labels (USPS, UPS, FedEx) purchased directly within Shopify admin

### Developer and Enterprise Tools
- **Hydrogen**: Open-source React-based headless commerce framework for building custom storefronts on Shopify's infrastructure. Built on Remix (which Shopify acquired in 2022).
- **Storefront API**: GraphQL API enabling developers to build custom frontends (web, mobile, gaming) on top of Shopify's commerce infrastructure
- **Liquid**: Shopify's original templating language (Ruby-based), still used by millions of stores

### Commerce Verticals
- **Shopify for Retail**: POS system for physical stores — supports hardware (card readers, receipt printers) and integrates inventory, customers, and orders across online and offline channels
- **Shopify for Omnichannel**: Facebook and Instagram shopping integration, Buy Button for external sites, and native channels within Shopify admin

## Notable Facts

- **Snowdevil to Shopify**: Lütke originally named the company "Snowdevil" and used it to sell snowboarding gear. The platform he built to power this store became Shopify — one of the classic "scratch your own itch" founding stories in tech.
- **Tobi Lütke's deliberate approach**: Lütke is known for building slowly and deliberately, famously stating that he regrets Shopify's early geographic expansion before perfecting core features in North America first.
- **Shopify Rebellion**: In February 2021, Shopify formed an esports organization (Shopify Rebellion) which became a leading StarCraft II and League of Legends team. In September 2023, Shopify Rebellion purchased TSM's LCS (League of Legends Championship Series) spot for approximately $26 million.
- **The #DeleteShopify controversy (2017)**: Shopify faced significant backlash for hosting Breitbart News store, leading to a coordinated #DeleteShopify campaign and internal employee protests. Shopify eventually removed Breitbart in 2018, establishing a policy against extremist content.
- **Kanye West/Yeezy store removal (2025)**: Shopify removed Kanye West's Yeezy brand store within 24 hours after the store displayed Nazi swastika imagery, demonstrating enforcement of hate speech policies.
- **Deliverr misfire**: The $2.1B Deliverr acquisition (2022) followed by the quick sale to Flexport (2023) at significant loss illustrates the risk of acquiring capital-intensive logistics businesses without deep operational expertise in that sector.
- **Developer ecosystem moat**: The Shopify App Store generates substantial revenue for third-party developers while creating lock-in for merchants — once a merchant's operations are deeply integrated with Shopify apps, migration becomes prohibitively costly and complex.
