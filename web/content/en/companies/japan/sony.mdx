# Sony

Sony Group Corporation (TYO: 6758; NYSE: SONY) is a Japanese multinational conglomerate headquartered in Minato, Tokyo, renowned for its pioneering consumer electronics and dominant entertainment content businesses. Founded in 1946 by Masaru Ibuka and Akio Morita with ¥190,000 capital and eight employees, Sony transformed from a modest electronics maker into a global technology and entertainment powerhouse with fiscal year 2024 revenue of ¥12.87 trillion (~$90.14 billion). Sony is the world's largest manufacturer of CMOS image sensors (55% global market share in 2020), operates the PlayStation gaming platform (one of the most profitable entertainment businesses in the world), owns two major film studios (Sony Pictures Entertainment, Columbia Pictures), and maintains a leading music recording and publishing business (Sony Music Entertainment). The company employs approximately 113,000 people globally.

## Timeline

### 1946–1967: Founding and Early Electronics Innovation

- **May 7, 1946**: **Masaru Ibuka** and **Akio Morita** found **Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo K.K.** (Tokyo Telecommunications Engineering Corporation) in Nihonbashi, Tokyo, with ¥190,000 capital and eight employees. Their first product was an electric rice cooker — demonstrating their broad technical ambitions beyond any single category.
- **1949**: Sony listed on Tokyo Stock Exchange over-the-counter market.
- **1950**: Sony launches Japan's first **tape recorder** (Type-G) — revolutionary for education and journalism.
- **1955**: Sony introduces the **TR-55 transistor radio** — first Sony-branded product (the "TR" prefix stood for "Tokyo Recorders"). This established Sony's brand identity as an innovative electronics company.
- **1958**: Company renamed **Sony Corporation** — adopted from Latin "sonus" (sound) and American slang "sonny" (young person). Morita insisted on the Western name to aid global expansion.
- **1960**: Sony launches world's first **solid-state television** (TV8-301, 8-inch portable). Morita opened Sony Corporation of America in New York.
- **1965**: Sony introduces **TC-50** cassette tape recorder — establishing the compact cassette standard.
- **1968**: Launch of **Trinitron** color television — Sony's most profitable product of the 1970s. Trinitron used a proprietary cathode ray tube technology that offered brighter, sharper images. Sony held the Trinitron patent until 1996.

### 1968–1988: Global Expansion and Walkman Phenomenon

- **1970**: Sony establishes **Sony Corporation of America**. Morita begins strategy of global branding — insisting Sony products sell under the Sony name globally rather than private-label.
- **1971**: Ibuka steps down as president; Morita becomes president (Chairman until 1976).
- **1975**: Sony launches the **Betamax** videotape format — competing with JVC's VHS. The "**Betamax case**" (Sony Corp. v. Universal City Studios) in 1984 established the legal principle that home videotaping for time-shifting was fair use.
- **1979**: Sony launches **Walkman** (TPS-L2) — the world's first portable music player. Morita gave them away free to taxi drivers in Tokyo; within three months, 15,000 were sold monthly. The Walkman fundamentally changed how people consumed music. Over 200 million units sold across all Walkman models.
- **1982**: Sony introduces the **Compact Disc (CD)** format commercially (jointly developed with Philips). Sony's chairman Noboru Ishiguro championed CD as the format winner.
- **1983**: Sony CD player (CDP-101) launched commercially — starting the digital audio revolution. Sony and Philips released the final CD spec in 1980.
- **1985**: Sony's North American headquarters opened in New York.
- **1986**: Sony launches **FD Sony** (Walkman with FM radio).
- **1988**: Sony wins the **CBS Records** acquisition — renamed **Sony Music Entertainment** ($2 billion). This was Sony's first major entertainment acquisition and marked its entry into the global music industry.

### 1989–1999: PlayStation and Hollywood Entry

- **1989**: Sony acquires **Columbia Pictures Entertainment** for $3.4 billion — including Columbia Pictures and TriStar Pictures. This transformed Sony into a major Hollywood studio, directly competing with Disney and Warner Bros. Sony Pictures Entertainment was established.
- **1994**: **PlayStation** launched in Japan (December 3) — developed jointly with Nintendo (originally called "PlayStation" as the result of a 1988 Nintendo-Sony collaboration on a CD-ROM add-on called "PlayStation" that Nintendo later abandoned). Sony's deal with Nintendo dissolved; Sony went solo. PlayStation broke Nintendo's dominance in home consoles, achieving **61% global market share**.
- **1995**: PlayStation launched in North America. **Edwin L. "Eddie" Saito** became global CEO of Sony.
- **1997**: PlayStation 2 announced (released 2000). Noboru Ohga became chairman (Ishiguro retired).
- **1998**: Sony launches **PlayStation with DualShock** controller (defining gamepad design). Walkman sales begin declining due to MP3 and digital audio.

### 2000–2010: Game Boy, Blu-ray, and Cell Processor

- **2000**: **PlayStation 2** launched in Japan (March) — became the best-selling video game console of all time (155 million units). Nintendo and Sega both lost market share.
- **2001**: Ken Kutaragi, "Father of PlayStation," named CEO of Sony Computer Entertainment.
- **2003**: Sony launches **PlayStation Portable (PSP)** — entering handheld gaming.
- **2005**: **Howard Stringer** becomes CEO — the first non-Japanese CEO of a major Japanese electronics company. This was a landmark event in Japanese corporate history. Stringer was born in Wales and had risen through Sony's US media businesses.
- **2006**: Nintendo DS outsold PSP dramatically. Sony acquired **Konica Minolta's camera business** (March) — strengthening Sony's Alpha camera line.
- **2006**: Sony launched **PlayStation 3** (November) with **Cell processor** — technologically ahead but expensive ($499–599). Included Blu-ray Disc drive, which became decisive in the format war with HD DVD ( Toshiba surrendered in 2008).
- **2007**: Sony's CMOS sensor business began aggressive expansion — targeting smartphone manufacturers.
- **2008**: Nintendo DS became the best-selling handheld. Sony launched PlayStation Network (PSN) — online gaming and media services.
- **2009**: Sony surpassed Nikon to become the **world's third-largest camera manufacturer** (after Canon and Nikon).

### 2011–2018: Restructuring and Comeback

- **2011**: PlayStation Network hacked — 77 million accounts compromised; Sony took PSN offline for 23 days. $171M in identity theft protection costs. Ken Kutaragi retired as chairman of SCE.
- **2012**: **Kazuo Hirai** becomes CEO — launching the **"One Sony"** strategy to focus Sony's diverse businesses around imaging, gaming, and mobile. Sony sold its **Vaio PC business** to Japan Industrial Partners (March 2014).
- **2013**: PlayStation 4 launched ($399) — dramatically outsold PlayStation 3 and Xbox One. Sony's gaming division became its most profitable segment.
- **2014**: Sony introduced **PlayStation Now** (cloud gaming) and **Project Morpheus** (VR headset, later renamed PlayStation VR).
- **2015**: Sony introduced **Sony Interactive Entertainment** as new structure. Hiroki Totoki became CFO.
- **2016**: PlayStation VR released ($399). Sony sold its **battery business** to Murata Manufacturing. Sony's image sensor business surged as smartphone manufacturers (especially Apple) adopted Sony sensors.
- **2017**: PlayStation 4 surpassed 73 million units sold — exceeding PlayStation 2's lifetime sales. Sony announced **Aibo** robot dog revival (2018).
- **2018**: PlayStation 4 reached **86 million units sold** (became Sony's second-best-selling console). Sony's imaging sensor order book was fully subscribed for years ahead.

### 2019–2025: PS5, Sensors, and AI Era

- **2019**: Kenichiro Yoshida becomes CFO. PlayStation 5 announced. Sony's image sensors powered most flagship smartphones globally.
- **2020**: PlayStation 5 launched (November 12, 2020, US; November 19, Japan) — demand far exceeded supply. Sony semiconductor solutions (I&SS) became Sony's most profitable segment.
- **2021**: **April 1, 2021** — Sony completed transition to **Sony Group Corporation** holding company structure. New logo introduced. PlayStation Network had **114 million monthly active users**.
- **2022**: PlayStation 5 surpassed **25 million units sold** (though supply chain constraints hurt availability). Sony's Jim Ryan became head of SIE. Sony's CMOS sensor market share grew to **55% globally**.
- **2023**: Sony launches **PlayStation Portal** (handheld gaming device). Sony Music acquired **AWAL** (artist services). Sony's PS5 sales reached 50 million units. Sony's I&SS (imaging) segment revenue hit record highs due to smartphone demand.
- **2024**: Sony launches **PlayStation 5 Pro** (enhanced performance). Sony's CMOS sensors equipped flagship phones from Samsung, Apple, Google, Xiaomi. Sony's PlayStation Studios produced major franchises (Spider-Man, God of War, Horizon, Gran Turismo). Sony invests in **Sony AI** and **Honda EV venture (Sony Honda Mobility / Afeela)**.
- **2025**: Sony Group's FY2024 revenue ¥12.87 trillion (~$90.14 billion). Sony announces **Miraiam** (new AI agent research). Sony Pictures continues as major Hollywood studio (Spider-Man franchise alone generated $8-9B globally). Sony's gaming revenue remained strong with PS5 ecosystem expansion.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Game & Network Services (G&NS)
- **PlayStation 5** — current-generation gaming console ($449 standard, $549 Pro)
- **PlayStation 5 Pro** — enhanced version (2024)
- **PlayStation Portal** — handheld gaming device
- **PlayStation VR2** — virtual reality headset (2023)
- **PlayStation Store** — digital game marketplace
- **PlayStation Plus** — subscription service (Essential/Extra/Premium tiers)
- PS5 reached 50M+ units sold; PlayStation Network 114M+ MAU

### Music
- **Sony Music Entertainment** — recording (artist roster includes Beyoncé, Adele, Travis Scott, etc.)
- **Sony/ATV Music Publishing** — world's largest music publisher (now Sony Music Publishing)
- Handles ~28% of global music publishing
- **The Orchard** — digital music distribution

### Pictures
- **Sony Pictures Entertainment** — film and TV production
- **Columbia Pictures** — iconic film studio (Spider-Man, Jumanji, Hotel Transylvania)
- **TriStar Pictures** — film production
- **Crunchyroll** — anime streaming (100M+ registered users)
- Sony's Spider-Man franchise alone grossed $8-9B+ worldwide

### Electronics Products & Solutions (EP&S)
- **BRAVIA** OLED and LED TVs
- **Alpha (α)** cameras — world's third-largest camera maker
- **Cyber-shot** compact cameras
- **Xperia** smartphones
- **Walkman** (NW-ZX707, NW-WM1ZM2 high-res audio players)
- **WH-1000XM5** noise-canceling headphones (industry benchmark)
- **Aibo** robot dog
- **PlayStation accessories**

### Imaging & Sensing Solutions (I&SS)
- **CMOS Image Sensors** — 55% global market share (2020)
- Customers include Apple (iPhone), Samsung, Google Pixel, Xiaomi, Huawei, Sony's own Xperia
- **TOF sensors** for smartphones
- **Industrial and automotive sensors**

### Financial Services
- **Sony Life Insurance** — life insurance in Japan
- **Sony Bank** — internet banking
- **Sony Assurance** — insurance

### Emerging
- **Sony Honda Mobility (Afeela)** — electric vehicle brand (with Honda, targeting 2026)
- **Sony AI** — AI research
- **Sony Robotics** — Aibo, other robotics
