# Rakuten

Rakuten Group, Inc. (楽天グループ株式会社) is a Japanese multinational technology company headquartered in Tokyo, founded in **1997** by **Hiroshi Mikitani** (三木谷浩史). What began as an e-commerce marketplace (Rakuten Ichiba) grew into one of Japan's most diversified tech conglomerates, spanning online retail, fintech (Rakuten Card, Rakuten Bank, Rakuten Securities, Rakuten Insurance), digital content (Rakuten TV, Rakuten Books), telecommunications (Rakuten Mobile), and professional sports (Rakuten Eagles baseball team, Golden State Warriors ownership stake). With revenue exceeding **¥2 trillion (~$14 billion)** in FY2024 and over 1 billion registered users globally, Rakuten is a defining platform of Japan's digital economy. Its most ambitious and capital-intensive venture is **Rakuten Mobile**, a vertically integrated mobile network operator that built Japan's first commercially viable Open RAN-based 5G network.

## Timeline

### 1997–2003: Founding and E-Commerce Takeoff

- **1997 (February)**: Rakuten founded by Hiroshi Mikitani, a former Goldman Sachs investment banker who left to pursue internet commerce. Initial concept: **Rakuten Ichiba** (楽天市場), a B2C online marketplace where small and medium Japanese merchants could open virtual storefronts. Unlike Amazon's warehouse model, Rakuten's "store-within-a-store" approach preserved the personalized service of Japanese department store culture.
- **2000**: Rakuten listed on the **JASDAQ** (Japanese over-the-counter market), raising ¥10.7 billion. The capital funded rapid domestic expansion.
- **2001**: Rakuten introduced its **Super Point** loyalty program — one of the world's first large-scale digital loyalty currencies, rewarding purchases with points redeemable across the Rakuten ecosystem. The program became a key moat, incentivizing users to consolidate all online spending within Rakuten.
- **2002**: Rakuten launched **Rakuten Travel** (formerly Japan Online Travel), becoming Japan's dominant online travel booking platform. This was the first major expansion beyond general e-commerce into a vertical category.
- **2003**: Rakuten migrated to the **Tokyo Stock Exchange First Section** (main market), validating its status as a major Japanese corporation.

### 2004–2009: Dominance and Global Expansion

- **2004**: Rakuten Ichiba became Japan's **#1 e-commerce marketplace** by gross merchandise volume (GMV), surpassing both Yahoo! Japan Shopping and Amazon Japan. At its peak, Rakuten Ichiba hosted over 40,000 merchants and generated ¥650 billion in annual GMV.
- **2005**: Hiroshi Mikitani coined the **"Global ONE Rakuten"** vision — a unified ecosystem where users would use Rakuten services for virtually every aspect of their digital and physical lives: shopping, banking, travel, communications, entertainment, sports.
- **2006**: Rakuten made its first major international acquisition: **Buy.com** in the United States for $250 million. This was rebranded but never achieved significant market share against Amazon and was eventually shut down.
- **2007**: Rakuten acquired **Japan Online Travel** and rebranded it as **Rakuten Travel** — the platform dominated Japan's domestic travel market and later expanded internationally.
- **2009**: Rakuten acquired **Kobo Inc.**, a Canadian e-book platform, for $315 million. This gave Rakuten a global e-book and e-reader business (Kobo e-readers, over 5 million titles) and a technological foundation for digital content.

### 2010–2015: Fintech and Telecommunications Ambitions

- **2010**: Rakuten launched **Rakuten Broadband** (later **Rakuten Mobile**) — initially as a 4G LTE ISP service using infrastructure from partner KDDI. This marked Rakuten's entry into telecommunications.
- **2011**: Rakuten launched **Rakuten Super TV** (IPTV service) and expanded into digital content more broadly.
- **2013**: Rakuten's **2013 Rugby World Cup** sponsorship increased global brand visibility, and the company acquired a stake in the **San Diego Padres** MLB team.
- **2014 (February)**: Rakuten acquired **Viber** for **$900 million** — one of the largest acquisitions in Japanese tech history. Viber had 300 million registered users at the time. This gave Rakuten a global messaging platform to complement its domestic ecosystem.
- **2015**: Rakuten launched **Rakuten Card Smart**, a credit card with integrated loyalty points. Rakuten Card became Japan's largest digital payment platform by transaction volume.

### 2016–2019: Mobile Network and Transformation

- **2016**: Rakuten announced plans to become a **full mobile network operator (MNO)** — building its own 4G LTE network rather than relying on KDDI's infrastructure. Japan had only three MNOs (NTT Docomo, au/KDDI, SoftBank), and entering as a fourth would require massive capital investment.
- **2017 (October)**: Rakuten Mobile launched as an **MVNO** using KDDI's network, immediately undercutting competitors with aggressive pricing (¥2,980/month for unlimited data). The MVNO phase served as a customer acquisition vehicle while the proprietary network was built.
- **2019**: Rakuten began deploying its **Open RAN** 4G network in Tokyo — a technically risky but strategically important decision to use open, standards-based hardware from vendors including Altiostar (US), NEC, and Intel rather than traditional single-vendor RAN solutions from Ericsson, Nokia, or Huawei.

### 2020–2023: 5G Launch and Mobile Network Commercialization

- **2020 (April)**: Rakuten Mobile launched commercially using its **proprietary 4G LTE network** (built on Open RAN architecture), becoming the first major mobile network to use open RAN at scale globally. The launch was initially plagued by coverage gaps and network quality issues.
- **2020**: Rakuten acquired a **5% stake in the Golden State Warriors** NBA team, expanding its sports portfolio.
- **2020**: Rakuten posted its first **annual net loss** in 2019 (partly due to Rakuten Mobile infrastructure costs), but revenue reached ¥1.4 trillion.
- **2021 (March)**: Rakuten Mobile launched **5G services** using its own Open RAN infrastructure — the world's first commercial 5G network based on Open RAN architecture.
- **2021**: Rakuten Mobile surpassed **5 million subscribers**. The low price point (¥1,078/month for 5G unlimited) continued to attract price-sensitive Japanese consumers.
- **2022**: Rakuten Mobile achieved **96% population coverage** for 4G and 70%+ for 5G. The company announced **Rakuten Symphony** — a dedicated Open RAN infrastructure solutions provider targeting global telecom operators.
- **2023**: Rakuten Mobile reached **approximately 6 million subscribers**. The company expanded **Rakuten Studio** production arm and content offerings.

### 2024–2025: Profitability Push and AI Integration

- **2024**: Rakuten Group revenue reached approximately **¥2.2 trillion (~$14.8 billion)**. Rakuten Mobile continued to reduce losses. The Open RAN model attracted interest from Bharti Airtel (India), Dish Network (US), and other global operators.
- **2024**: Rakuten expanded **Rakuten Wallet** (cryptocurrency exchange) and deepened fintech-e-commerce integration.
- **2024**: **Rakuten Symphony** signed agreements with multiple Asian and African telecom operators to provide Open RAN network solutions, positioning Rakuten as a technology vendor.
- **2025**: Rakuten launched **Rakuten AI** — a suite of AI-powered services integrated across its ecosystem (recommendation engines, customer service chatbots, logistics optimization).

## Key Products and Business Segments

### E-Commerce (Rakuten Ichiba)
- Japan's largest B2C online marketplace with 60,000+ merchants, ~¥1.5 trillion annual GMV; the "omni-channel" model combines online marketplace with physical retail integrations
- **Rakuten Fresh**: grocery delivery service competing with Amazon Fresh and convenience store delivery
- **Rakuten Books**: physical and digital books, one of Japan's largest online bookstores

### Fintech
- **Rakuten Card**: Japan's largest credit card by active users, integrated with Rakuten's loyalty ecosystem; ~15 million cardholders
- **Rakuten Bank**: online banking with 10+ million accounts
- **Rakuten Securities**: online brokerage with 2+ million accounts, dominant in Japan for retail equity trading
- **Rakuten Pay**: mobile payment app with 50+ million downloads, QR code payments competing with LINE Pay
- **Rakuten Insurance**: life, auto, and travel insurance products

### Telecommunications
- **Rakuten Mobile**: Open RAN-based 4G/5G network; ~6 million subscribers (2024); unique in being the only major MNO using fully virtualized, Open RAN architecture
- **Rakuten Mobile 5G**: unlimited data plans from ¥1,078/month — among the cheapest in Japan
- **Rakuten Symphony**: sells network software and integration services to other operators globally using Open RAN stack

### Digital Content and Sports
- **Rakuten TV**: streaming service competing with Netflix in Japan, offering Hollywood movies and original Japanese content
- **Rakuten Eagles**: Tohoku Rakuten Golden Eagles professional baseball team (Nippon Professional Baseball), acquired 2004
- **Golden State Warriors**: 5% ownership stake; global sports marketing platform

## Corporate Governance

- **Founder, Chairman & CEO**: Hiroshi Mikitani (三木谷浩史) — one of Japan's most prominent entrepreneurs; developed Rakuten's unique ecosystem model; recipient of the Legion of Honour (France); competitive sailor
- **President & COO**: Masayoshi Hosoda — long-tenured executive managing group operations
- **Headquarters**: Tokyo, Japan
- **Listed**: Tokyo Stock Exchange (4755.T, since 2000, First Section)
- **Employees**: ~28,000 globally (2025)
- **Key subsidiaries**: Rakuten Mobile, Rakuten Card, Rakuten Bank, Rakuten Symphony, Viber, Kobo, Rakuten Travel
