# NTT

Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corporation (NTT) is Japan's national telecommunications carrier and one of the world's largest technology conglomerates, headquartered in Tokyo. Originally established as a state-owned monopoly in 1952, NTT was privatized and listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange in 1987 in what remained the largest stock offering in history ($36.8 billion). The 1999 reorganization created a holding company structure with NTT East and NTT West (regional fixed-line), NTT Communications (long-distance/international), NTT DOCOMO (mobile), and NTT Data (systems integration). NTT employs approximately 341,321 people and generated ¥13.70 trillion in revenue (FY2025), making it Japan's largest company by revenue and ranked 128th in the Fortune Global 500.

## Timeline

### 1952–1985: State-Owned Monopoly Era

- **1952**: Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Public Corporation (NTT) established as a government-owned monopoly under the Ministry of Communications, taking over the telephone network infrastructure of the former Japanese National Railways and Ministry of Communications.
- **1979**: NTT announced the **INS (Information Network System)** concept — a visionary plan for an integrated digital communications network that foreshadowed the modern internet. The concept was ahead of its time but laid intellectual groundwork for Japan's later broadband push.
- **1982**: NTT began developing fiber-optic network infrastructure as part of INS vision, investing heavily in research.
- **1984**: Legislation passed to privatize NTT, preparing for the transition from state-owned enterprise.

### 1985–1999: Privatization and IPO

- **1985**: **Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corporation** incorporated as a private company; shares listed on Tokyo Stock Exchange. The IPO was oversubscribed more than 200 times, reflecting enormous public interest in owning shares of the newly privatized telecom giant.
- **1987**: NTT shares listed on Tokyo Stock Exchange; the offering raised $36.8 billion — the largest stock offering in world history at the time and remains one of the largest global IPOs ever. Foreign investors were initially restricted from owning more than minority stakes.
- **1988**: **NTT Data Corporation** began operations as a subsidiary focused on systems integration and data communications — the foundation of what would become NTT Data, one of Japan's largest IT services companies.
- **1992**: **NTT Mobile Communications Network** (later **NTT DOCOMO**) started operations, entering the early mobile phone market. DOCOMO would later pioneer i-mode, the world's most successful early mobile internet platform.
- **1994**: DOCOMO launched **i-mode** service — a groundbreaking mobile internet platform that gave Japanese users email, web browsing, and online services years before smartphones. i-mode had 50 million subscribers at its peak and was a model for global mobile internet development.
- **1999**: NTT reorganized into a **holding company structure** — splitting into NTT East (Kanto/Koshinetsu/Hokuriku regions), NTT West (Kansai/Chubu/Chugoku/Shikoku/Kyushu regions), NTT Communications (long-distance and international), and NTT DOCOMO (mobile). The regional split was mandated by Japan's Ministry of Finance to promote competition.

### 2000–2015: Broadband Expansion and Global Expansion

- **2001**: DOCOMO launched **FOMA** — the world's first commercial 3G mobile service, beating other carriers globally by years. FOMA stood for "Freedom of Mobile multimedia Access."
- **2004**: NTT Communications launched the **Verio** brand globally following acquisitions, positioning as a global IP network provider.
- **2005**: DOCOMO's i-mode reached its peak influence; the company began strategic investments in overseas mobile carriers (investing in Philippines' Smart Communications, US carrier, and others).
- **2007**: NTT invested heavily in fiber-to-the-home (FTTH), achieving over 50% household coverage in Japan by decade's end — making Japan one of the world's most fiber-connected nations.
- **2010**: NTT acquired **Dimension Data Holdings** for £2.12 billion (~$3.24 billion) — a major step in NTT's global expansion of IT services and system integration, bringing Dimension Data's presence in 51 countries.
- **2013**: NTT DOCOMO was listed on Tokyo Stock Exchange (partial IPO, with NTT retaining 66% ownership).
- **2015**: NTT acquired an **86.7% stake in e-shelter**, Germany's largest data center operator, signaling NTT's ambitions in global data center infrastructure.

### 2016–2025: Consolidation and AI/6G Push

- **2018**: NTT announced plans to acquire **SkyPerfect JSAT** shares, expanding satellite communications capabilities.
- **2019**: NTT launched **NTT Ltd.** — an $11 billion holding company consolidating 28 brands including Dimension Data, NTT Communications, and others into a single global operating entity, simplifying the complex global subsidiary structure.
- **2020**: NTT acquired **CenturyLink's data center assets** in the US (16 facilities) as part of the global data center expansion strategy.
- **2021**: NTT announced a ¥5 trillion (~$45 billion) investment over five years in 5G and advanced network infrastructure — the largest telecom infrastructure investment in Japanese history to that point.
- **2022**: NTT and DOCOMO announced plans to integrate their mobile operations under a unified structure, simplifying the holding company complexity.
- **2023**: NTT formed partnerships with Sony and Intel for **6G research** — targeting commercial 6G deployment by 2030. NTT contributed its proprietary **IOWN (Innovative Optical and Wireless Network)** vision as the architectural framework.
- **2024**: NTT invested $2 billion in **global AI infrastructure**, including new data centers in the US, Europe, and Asia-Pacific regions.
- **2025**: NTT Corporation completed corporate rebranding from **Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corporation** to simply **NTT, Inc.** with a new corporate identity — reflecting its evolution from a national telecom utility to a global technology conglomerate.

## Key Products and Services

### Regional Fixed-Line Services
- **NTT East / NTT West**: Last-mile copper and fiber connections to approximately 45 million households and businesses across Japan. The regional monopolies were required as a condition of the 1999 breakup, though both now face competition from alternative operators.

### Mobile Communications (via NTT DOCOMO)
- **DOCOMO**: Japan's largest mobile operator with ~80 million subscribers. Pioneered i-mode (1999), FOMA 3G (2001), and Xi (LTE, 2010). 5G services launched 2020.
- Products: irumo mobile plans, DOCOMO Hikari fiber+mobile bundles

### Long-Distance and International
- **NTT Communications**: Global IP networking, enterprise VPN, cloud connectivity
- **NTT Ltd.**: Global managed services, consulting, and system integration across 71 countries

### Data and Systems Integration
- **NTT Data**: Japan's largest IT services company; provides systems integration, consulting, and outsourcing for government and enterprise clients. Revenues exceeded ¥3 trillion.
- **Dimension Data**: Global systems integrator (acquired 2010), specializing in digital infrastructure for enterprise clients

### Information Security
- **NTT Security**: Cybersecurity consulting, threat intelligence, managed security services (operates within NTT Ltd.)

### IOWN Initiative
- **IOWN (Innovative Optical and Wireless Network)**: NTT's next-generation network vision using photonic (light-based) cores instead of electronic ones — targeting 100x reduction in power consumption and 125x lower latency versus current networks. Seen as NTT's architectural answer to 6G and beyond.

## Corporate Structure

- **Parent**: NTT, Inc. (renamed from Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corporation, 2025)
- **Major subsidiaries**: NTT DOCOMO (66%-owned, publicly listed), NTT Data (majority-owned, publicly listed), NTT Ltd. (global operating entity)
- **Headquarters**: Tokyo, Japan
- **Employees**: 341,321 (consolidated, 2025)
- **Listed**: Tokyo Stock Exchange (TYO: 9432); NYSE (NTT)
- **Ownership**: Foreign investors hold approximately 44%; Japanese institutions (including government pension funds) hold the remainder
