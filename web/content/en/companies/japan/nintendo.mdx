# Nintendo

Nintendo Co., Ltd. (TYO: 7974) is a Japanese multinational consumer electronics and video game company headquartered in Kyoto, Japan. Founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi as a playing card company, Nintendo has transformed itself multiple times across its 135+ year history — from playing cards to toys, from toys to electronic games, and from dedicated game consoles to a broader interactive entertainment company. Today it is one of the world's most influential video game companies, known for creating some of the most beloved and best-selling gaming franchises in history: Super Mario, The Legend of Zelda, Pokémon, Metroid, and Animal Crossing. Nintendo's unique "lateral thinking with withered technology" philosophy (coined by former president Hiroshi Yamauchi) — using accessible, affordable technology rather than cutting-edge hardware — has defined its competitive strategy for decades. The Nintendo Switch has been one of the most successful gaming consoles in history, selling over 146 million units worldwide by late 2024.

## Timeline

### 1889–1950: Playing Card Origins

- **1889 (September)**: Nintendo was founded in Kyoto by **Fusajiro Yamauchi** as **Nintendo Koppai** — a company producing **Hanafuda** (flower cards), a traditional Japanese playing card game. Fusajiro used his own savings and a small loan to start the business. The company's first products were handmade playing cards from mulberry bark.
- **1902**: Nintendo began producing Western-style playing cards — the first company in Japan to do so.
- **1929**: The company was passed to his son-in-law Sekiguchi.
- **1933**: Nintendo merged with another card company to form **Nintendo Playing Card Company Ltd.**
- **1949**: A 22-year-old **Hiroshi Yamauchi** (grandson of the founder) became president — the youngest president of any major company in Japan at the time. He would transform Nintendo from a regional playing card maker into a global entertainment giant.

### 1950–1970: Diversification and Early Toy Experiments

- **1950s**: Hiroshi Yamauchi began aggressively expanding Nintendo's scope — believing the company needed to grow beyond playing cards. He invested in new product lines including **Ultra Machine** (a mechanical baseball pitching toy for children), **Love Tester** (a novelty toy), and various other experiments.
- **1963**: Nintendo was officially renamed **Nintendo Co., Ltd.** (dropping "Playing Card").
- **1965**: **Shigeru Miyamoto** (a young industrial design graduate from Kanagawa Institute of Technology) joined Nintendo as an artist and designer — he would go on to become Nintendo's most legendary game designer, creating Donkey Kong (1981), Mario (1983), The Legend of Zelda (1986), and countless other iconic games.
- **1975**: Nintendo partnered with **Mitsubishi Electric** to develop the **Color TV-Game** series — five handheld electronic games that sold over 1 million units.

### 1977–1985: Game & Watch, Donkey Kong, and NES

- **1977**: Nintendo released the **Color TV-Game 6** and **Color TV-Game 15** — the first home video game consoles produced by Nintendo, sold exclusively in Japan.
- **1978**: Nintendo released the **Game & Watch** — a groundbreaking handheld electronic game invented by Nintendo engineer Gunpei Yokoi. It featured a tiny LCD screen and sold **43.4 million units** worldwide over its lifetime. The Game & Watch established Nintendo's identity as a maker of innovative, affordable entertainment devices.
- **1980 (April 28)**: Nintendo released **Donkey Kong** — an arcade game designed by Shigeru Miyamoto. The game featured a character originally called "Jumpman" who would later be renamed **Mario**. Donkey Kong was a massive arcade hit, selling 65,000 arcade cabinets worldwide.
- **1983 (July 15)**: Nintendo launched the **Family Computer (Famicom)** in Japan — priced at ¥14,800. It was an immediate phenomenon, selling 500,000 units in the first two months.
- **1983**: The North American video game market **collapsed** (the "Video Game Crash of 1983") — caused by an oversaturated market of low-quality games. This disaster cleared the market for Nintendo's entry.
- **1985 (October)**: Nintendo launched the **Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)** in North America. Nintendo's "Seal of Quality" program prevented the low-quality games that had destroyed the Atari market — a masterstroke that rebuilt consumer confidence in video games.
- **1986 (February)**: Nintendo released **Super Mario Bros.** — designed by Shigeru Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka. It sold 40.24 million copies worldwide, defining the side-scrolling platformer genre.
- **1986**: Nintendo released **The Legend of Zelda** (for the Famicom Disk System) — the first game in one of the most beloved gaming franchises in history.
- **1989 (April 21)**: Nintendo launched the **Game Boy** — designed by Gunpei Yokoi. Bundled with **Tetris**, the Game Boy sold **118.3 million units** worldwide, making it the third best-selling video game console of all time.

### 1990–2000: Super Nintendo and the 64-Bit Era

- **1990 (November 21)**: Nintendo launched the **Super Famicom (SFC)** in Japan — a 16-bit console that became Japan's best-selling console of its generation.
- **1991 (August)**: Nintendo launched the **Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES)** in North America.
- **1994 (December)**: Nintendo secretly partnered with **Sony** to develop the **Nintendo PlayStation** (CD-ROM add-on for the SNES). This partnership collapsed — Sony used the technology to create the **Sony PlayStation**, which became one of Nintendo's greatest competitive threats.
- **1996 (June 23)**: Nintendo launched the **Nintendo 64 (N64)** — the first 64-bit home console. Despite technical innovations, the N64 struggled due to limited third-party support and cartridge media. Key games: **Super Mario 64** (1996), **The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time** (1998, widely considered one of the greatest games ever made).
- **1996**: Nintendo introduced the **Virtual Boy** — a 3D handheld console. It was a commercial failure (only 770,000 units sold) due to its uncomfortable design.
- **1999**: Nintendo launched the **Game Boy Color** — adding color graphics.

### 2000–2006: New Leadership and Wii Revolution

- **2000**: **Satoru Iwata** (previously president of HAL Laboratory) became Nintendo's president — succeeding Hiroshi Yamauchi, who had led Nintendo for 52 years. This was the first time a non-Yamauchi family member led the company.
- **2001 (September 14)**: Nintendo launched the **GameCube** — selling 21.74 million units, significantly less than Sony's PlayStation 2 (155 million).
- **2001**: Nintendo launched the **Game Boy Advance** — selling 81.51 million units.
- **2004 (March)**: Nintendo launched the **Nintendo DS** — a dual-screen handheld with touchscreen. The DS sold **154.02 million units** worldwide, becoming the second best-selling console of all time.
- **2006 (November)**: Nintendo launched the **Wii** — a motion-controlled home console targeting casual, non-gamer audiences. It became a cultural phenomenon, selling **101.63 million units** — the third best-selling home console of all time.

### 2007–2015: Smartphones, 3DS, and the Wii U Failure

- **2011 (February 26)**: Nintendo launched the **Nintendo 3DS** — capable of displaying 3D graphics without glasses. It sold 75.9 million units.
- **2013**: Nintendo announced plans to develop smartphone games — a major strategic shift for a company committed to dedicated gaming hardware.
- **2013 (November)**: Nintendo launched the **Wii U** — a commercial failure (only 13.56 million units) due to consumer confusion, weak third-party support, and the rise of mobile gaming.
- **2015 (July)**: Satoru Iwata died of cancer at age 55. He was succeeded by **Tatsumi Kimishima**.

### 2016–2020: Switch and the Return to Growth

- **2017 (March 3)**: Nintendo launched the **Nintendo Switch** — a revolutionary hybrid console usable as both a home TV console and portable handheld. Priced at $299, it was an immediate success — 2 million units in the first month. Key launch title: **The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild**.
- **2017 (October)**: Nintendo released **Super Mario Odyssey** — another critical and commercial triumph.
- **2019**: Nintendo introduced the **Nintendo Switch Lite** — a cheaper, handheld-only version.
- **2020 (March)**: Nintendo released **Animal Crossing: New Horizons** — a cultural phenomenon that sold 33 million copies and defined pandemic-era gaming.
- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic boosted Switch sales to record levels.
- **2021 (October)**: Nintendo launched the **Switch OLED** — featuring a 7-inch OLED screen and improved audio.

### 2021–2025: Switch Dominance and Switch 2

- **2022**: Nintendo reported that the Switch had become the **third best-selling home console of all time** (surpassing PlayStation 4).
- **2024**: Nintendo's **Super Mario Bros. Movie** (produced with Illumination) grossed over **$1.36 billion** worldwide — the highest-grossing animated film of 2023 and first $1B+ film from a video game IP.
- **2024 (December)**: Nintendo reported Switch total sales of **~146 million units** — making it the **second best-selling video game console of all time** (after PlayStation 2's 155 million).
- **2025 (April 2)**: Nintendo officially announced **Nintendo Switch 2** — featuring a larger screen, magnetic Joy-Cons, 4K TV output when docked, and full backward compatibility with Switch games. Launch year: **2025**.

## Key Products and Franchises

### Hardware
- **Nintendo Switch** — 6.2-inch touchscreen, detachable Joy-Con controllers, HDMI TV output (2017)
- **Nintendo Switch OLED** — 7-inch OLED screen, enhanced audio (2021)
- **Nintendo Switch Lite** — 5.5-inch screen, handheld-only (2019)
- **Nintendo Switch 2** — next generation, launching 2025

### Iconic Game Franchises
- **Super Mario** — platformers (Super Mario Bros., Super Mario 64, Super Mario Odyssey)
- **The Legend of Zelda** — action-adventure (Ocarina of Time, Breath of the Wild, Tears of the Kingdom)
- **Pokémon** — handheld games and trading cards (managed by The Pokémon Company)
- **Animal Crossing** — life simulation (Animal Crossing: New Horizons, 44M+ copies sold)
- **Mario Kart** — racing (Mario Kart 8 Deluxe, 64M+ copies — best-selling Switch game)
- **Super Smash Bros.** — fighting game crossover
- **Splatoon** — ink-shooting team shooter
- **Metroid** — action-adventure/sci-fi

## CEO and Leadership

- **Representative Director & President**: **Shuntaro Furukawa** (since 2018). Joined Nintendo in 2002.
- **Previous Presidents**: Hiroshi Yamauchi (1949–2000), Satoru Iwata (2000–2015, died), Tatsumi Kimishima (2015–2018)
- **Legendary Creator**: **Shigeru Miyamoto** — "Mario's father," still active as a senior managing director

## Corporate Structure

- **Headquarters**: Kyoto, Japan
- **Incorporated**: 1889 (as Nintendo Koppai)
- **TYO: 7974** — listed on Tokyo Stock Exchange
- Nintendo's business model: profit from hardware and proprietary game software sold through a closed ecosystem (Nintendo eShop, physical game cards)
