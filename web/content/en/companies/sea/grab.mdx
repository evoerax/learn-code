# Grab

Grab Holdings Limited is a Southeast Asian multinational technology company headquartered in Singapore, operating a super-app platform that provides ride-hailing, food delivery, grocery delivery, and financial services across **Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, the Philippines, Cambodia, and Myanmar**. Founded in 2012 by **Anthony Tan** (陈炳耀) and **Tan Hooi Ling** (陈慧玲) — both Harvard Business School graduates — Grab started as MyTeksi, a taxi-hailing app in Malaysia, before rapidly expanding across the region. The company is named for its mission to be the "qrabc" (Malay for "serve everyone"). Grab displaced Uber in Southeast Asia in 2018 by acquiring Uber's regional operations in exchange for an 18% equity stake. Grab listed on NASDAQ via a SPAC merger in December 2021 at a $40 billion valuation — the largest SPAC deal ever at the time. With 700+ million downloads, Grab processes millions of daily transactions and has pivoted toward financial services (GrabFinance), advertising, and enterprise solutions as core growth engines.

## Timeline

### 2012–2014: Foundation and Taxi-Hailing Origins

- **2012**: **MyTeksi** founded in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia by **Anthony Tan** and **Tan Hooi Ling** — both Malaysian nationals who met at Harvard Business School. The startup was bootstrapped with initial funding from a Malaysian government fund and anth12 and a small angel round. The app solved the problem of unsafe street hailing in Kuala Lumpur by connecting passengers with licensed taxi drivers.
- **2012**: MyTeksi won the **Intel Global Challenge** at UC Berkeley — validating the business model and providing early credibility and seed funding.
- **2013**: Expanded to **Singapore** as **GrabTaxi** — the company's international expansion began with Singapore, which became its headquarters. Also launched in the Philippines and Thailand.
- **2014**: Rebranded to **Grab** — moving beyond taxis to include private cars (GrabCar) and motorbikes (GrabBike). The GrabCar service was a direct response to demand for premium and reliable ride options.
- **2014**: Secured a **strategic partnership with Uber** — Uber invested in Grab and provided technology and operational support in exchange for a minority equity stake. The partnership gave Grab access to Uber's technology while allowing Grab to maintain regional brand independence.

### 2015–2017: Regional Expansion and Antitrust Battles

- **2015**: Launched **GrabCar** across all major Southeast Asian cities — differentiating from taxis by offering newer vehicles, fixed pricing, and safety features (driver ratings, trip sharing).
- **2015**: Introduced **GrabBike** (motorcycle ride-hailing) in Indonesia and Vietnam — motorbikes are the dominant form of two-wheeled transport in these countries and represented a massive underserved market.
- **2015**: Raised **$650M Series E** led by SoftBank Vision Fund — at $1.5B valuation, SoftBank became Grab's largest external investor and played a key role in funding the regional expansion against Uber.
- **2016**: SoftBank Vision Fund invested an additional **$1B** at a $3B valuation — massive capital infusion that enabled aggressive market share growth and driver incentives.
- **2017**: Launched **GrabPay** — digital payment wallet for driver earnings and passenger payments, initially within the Grab app. This was the foundation of Grab's financial services ambitions.
- **2017**: Became the **dominant ride-hailing platform across Southeast Asia** by some metrics — operating in 8 countries, with millions of registered drivers and passengers.

### 2018: Uber Acquisition and Competition with Go-Jek

- **March 2018**: In a landmark deal, Grab announced it would **acquire Uber's operations across Southeast Asia** — Uber exchanged its 18% Grab stake, received ~$600M in cash, and Uber CEO Dara Khosrowshahi joined Grab's board. The deal valued Grab at $11B post-money. This was the largest consumer tech M&A in Southeast Asia to date.
- **2018**: Uber officially exited Southeast Asia — marking the end of direct competition in the region and establishing Grab as the dominant player.
- **2018**: The acquisition triggered **antitrust scrutiny** from regulators in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Singapore's CCCS (Competition and Consumer Commission) launched an investigation and ultimately fined both Grab and Uber for the merger, though Grab was allowed to continue operating.
- **2018**: **Go-Jek** (Indonesia-based rival) raised $1B+ from Google, Tencent, and JD.com — intensifying the competition between Grab and Go-Jek for dominance across the region, particularly in Indonesia (Southeast Asia's largest market with 270M people).

### 2019–2020: Diversification and Financial Services

- **2019**: Launched **GrabFinancial** — a suite of financial services including GrabPay wallet, GrabPay card, and insurance distribution. Grab obtained e-money licenses in multiple countries.
- **2019**: Introduced **GrabMart** — grocery delivery service, leveraging the existing driver network for last-mile delivery of everyday goods.
- **2019**: Raised **$300M from Yamaha Motor** — deepening the partnership for motorcycle mobility solutions.
- **2020**: Launched **GrabKitchen** — cloud kitchen/food delivery infrastructure to support restaurant partners across the region.
- **2020**: Grab's revenue was severely impacted by COVID-19 — ride-hailing demand fell sharply during lockdowns, though food delivery surged, partially offsetting losses.

### 2021: NASDAQ Listing via SPAC

- **April 2021**: Grab announced a **$40 billion SPAC merger** with Altimeter Growth Corp (AGC) — the largest SPAC deal in history at the time. The deal gave Grab a public listing on NASDAQ while providing capital for expansion.
- **December 2021**: Grab officially began trading on NASDAQ under ticker **GRAB** — opening at $10.77/share, giving the company an implied valuation of approximately $40 billion. It was the largest Southeast Asian company to list on a US exchange.
- **Post-listing**: Grab's share price declined significantly through 2022-2023 as growth stalled, COVID headwinds persisted, and profitability concerns mounted — demonstrating the challenges SPAC-listed growth companies faced in the post-2021 market.

### 2022–2023: Path to Profitability

- **2022**: Grab cut costs aggressively in response to post-SPAC market pressure and slowing growth — reducing marketing spend, consolidating operations, and cutting staff by approximately 10%.
- **2022**: **GoTo Group** (formed from the merger of Gojek and Tokopedia in May 2021) remained a major competitor, particularly in Indonesia. The Indonesia market remained the most contested.
- **2023**: Introduced **GrabAds** — advertising platform leveraging Grab's 700M+ user base and rich behavioral data for targeted advertising. This became a key new revenue stream.
- **2023**: Grab reported **first positive adjusted EBITDA** in Q3 2023 — a major milestone signaling the company's path to sustainable profitability. Full-year 2023 adjusted EBITDA loss narrowed significantly.

### 2024–2025: AI Partnership and Profitability Milestone

- **2024**: Full year 2024 — Grab reported **first full year of positive adjusted EBITDA** at the corporate level, marking a major milestone after 12+ years of losses. Revenue grew approximately 17% YoY driven by delivery and financial services.
- **2024**: **Gross merchandise value (GMV)** exceeded $30 billion — a record high for the company, driven by delivery and mobile payments growth.
- **2025**: Announced a **$1.5 billion strategic partnership with NVIDIA** — Grab will use NVIDIA's AI infrastructure (GPUs, AI software) to enhance its mapping, safety, and autonomous vehicle technologies. This partnership signals Grab's ambition to compete in the AI era with its vast regional data advantage.
- **2025**: **Grab for Work** (enterprise mobility solutions) expanded — providing corporate transportation and delivery accounts for businesses across the region.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Mobility (Ride-Hailing)
- **GrabCar** — sedan and SUV ride-hailing across all 8 countries
- **GrabBike** — motorcycle ride-hailing dominant in Indonesia, Vietnam, Philippines
- **GrabTaxi** — licensed taxi booking in all markets
- **GrabShare** — shared ride-splitting option
- **JustGrab** — guaranteed car with transparent pricing
- Driver earnings: Grab takes ~20% commission from drivers (significantly lower than Uber's historical ~25-30%)

### Delivery
- **GrabFood** — restaurant food delivery (dominant in Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, Philippines)
- **GrabMart** — grocery and convenience delivery (leverages existing driver network for last-mile)
- **GrabKitchen** — cloud kitchen infrastructure for restaurant brand expansion

### Financial Services (GrabFinance)
- **GrabPay** — digital wallet for payments across the platform
- **GrabPay Merchant** — QR code payments for small merchants
- **GrabInsure** — insurance distribution (life, health, travel, driver insurance)
- **GrabFinance** — lending to small businesses and driver-partners (revenue from interest income)
- **GrabCard** — virtual and physical prepaid card

### Advertising and Enterprise
- **GrabAds** — targeted advertising platform leveraging user location data and behavioral insights
- **Grab for Work** — enterprise mobility accounts for corporate clients
- **GrabMart B2B** — supply chain solutions for merchants

## Corporate Structure

- **Co-founders**: Anthony Tan (CEO) and Tan Hooi Ling (President) — both Malaysian nationals, Harvard Business School graduates
- **Headquarters**: Singapore (regional HQ in Singapore; operations across 8 countries)
- **Ticker**: NASDAQ: GRAB (listed December 2021 via SPAC)
- **Key shareholders**: SoftBank (~18% post-SPAC), Uber (~18% post-acquisition), Mitsubishi UFST (~$150M 2023 investment), NVIDIA ($1.5B 2025 partnership)
- **Employees**: ~7,000 employees globally (2024)
- **Revenue**: ~$2.3B FY2024 (estimate, as Grab does not publicly report precise figures as a private company through most of history; pro forma combined revenue)
- **GMV**: $30B+ (2024)
