# Traveloka

Traveloka is an Indonesian online travel platform established in February 2012 in Jakarta, Indonesia, and headquartered in Singapore. It operates across Indonesia, Singapore, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, Philippines, Australia, and Japan, positioning itself as **Southeast Asia's largest online travel agency (OTA)** with a market position functionally comparable to Expedia in the global context. Founded by three Indonesian software engineers — **Ferry Unardi** (CEO), Albert, and **Deriantono Kusuma** (CTO, resigned 2018) — who met while studying and working in the United States, Traveloka has evolved from a flight metasearch engine into a comprehensive travel and lifestyle superapp. The company achieved unicorn status in 2017, reached a $3 billion valuation in January 2022, and has been profitable since 2023. A 2023 PwC study estimated that Traveloka contributed **$10 billion in Gross Value Added (GVA)** to Indonesia's economy between 2019-2022, making it one of Southeast Asia's most impactful internet companies.

## Timeline

### 2012–2013: Founding and Launch

- **February 2012**: Traveloka founded in Jakarta by **Ferry Unardi** (CEO), Albert, and **Deriantono Kusuma** (CTO). All three founders were Indonesian engineers who had studied or worked in the United States — a background that influenced Traveloka's product-first, technology-driven culture.
- **2012**: Initially launched as a **travel metasearch site** — aggregating flight search results from multiple airlines and online travel agents.
- **2013**: Transitioned from metasearch to a full **online travel agency (OTA)** — enabling users to book directly on the platform rather than being redirected to airlines or other booking sites. Launched hotel booking services.
- Seed funding from **East Ventures** (2012) and **Global Founders Capital** (2013) — early investors that enabled product development and initial market traction.

### 2014–2016: Mobile-First and Regional Expansion

- **2014**: Mobile apps launched for iOS and Android — Traveloka was early to recognize mobile's dominance in Southeast Asian internet usage.
- **2014–2016**: Expanded hotel booking coverage and launched attractions booking, airport transfer services — progressively broadening the platform beyond core flight/hotel.
- **2016**: Established operations in **Singapore, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, and the Philippines** — becoming a regional player rather than an Indonesia-only product.
- By 2016, Traveloka had become the dominant OTA in Indonesia — where the middle class was rapidly growing and domestic air travel was expanding rapidly.

### 2017: Unicorn Year

- **July 2017**: Raised **$350 million** in a funding round led by **GIC**, **Qatar Investment Authority (QIA)**, and **Expedia** — the largest funding round for a Southeast Asian travel tech company at the time.
- **2017**: Achieved **unicorn status** (valuation exceeding $1 billion), making Traveloka Indonesia's first unicorn and one of Southeast Asia's earliest unicorns.
- **2017**: Partnership with Expedia gave Traveloka access to Expedia's global hotel inventory — significantly expanding its international hotel offerings.

### 2018–2019: Growth and Market Leadership

- **2018**: CTO Deriantono Kusuma resigned — a significant leadership transition for the founding team.
- **2019**: Became **Southeast Asia's largest online travel startup**. A 2019 survey showed **79% of Indonesian millennials** used Traveloka regularly — demonstrating near-monopoly-level penetration among the key demographic.
- Launched **Traveloka PayLater** — buy-now-pay-later financial services for travel bookings, addressing the credit card penetration gap in Southeast Asian markets.
- **2020 (pre-COVID)**: Peak valuation estimated at $2.5-3 billion based on secondary market transactions.

### 2020–2021: COVID-19 Pandemic

- **April 2020**: Laid off approximately **100 employees** (~5% of workforce) as pandemic destroyed travel demand globally.
- **2020**: Refunded approximately **$100 million worth of 150,000 flight tickets** — a massive financial burden that Traveloka absorbed voluntarily to maintain customer trust during an unprecedented crisis.
- Introduced **flexible booking options** — allowing free date changes and cancellations across all bookings — a critical feature during the uncertainty.
- Facilitated **vaccination bookings for over 600,000 people** during Indonesia's COVID vaccination rollout — demonstrating the platform's utility beyond leisure travel.
- **2021**: Halted **SPAC merger discussions** — the company had been in advanced talks for a public listing via SPAC at a reported valuation of $6-7 billion, but pulled out due to market conditions and regulatory uncertainty.

### 2022: Resilience and Recovery Planning

- **January 2022**: Raised **$300 million** from a consortium of lenders — a debt financing round that provided runway without dilutive equity. (This was repaid early in 2024.)
- **2022**: Valuation declined to approximately **$3 billion** from peak (reflecting post-COVID recovery uncertainty in travel markets).
- Continued expansion into **lifestyle activities** — Tours, Experiences, and Attractions becoming a significant revenue driver.

### 2023–2025: Profitable Growth and New Verticals

- **2023**: Achieved **profitability** — a major milestone for a Southeast Asian superapp that had historically prioritized growth over profits.
- **2023**: A **PwC study** commissioned by Traveloka estimated the company contributed **$10 billion in Gross Value Added** to Indonesia's economy between 2019-2022 (including direct, indirect, and induced economic impact) — establishing Traveloka as critical infrastructure for Indonesian tourism.
- **2024**: Partnered with **Disney Cruise Line** — expanding into cruise bookings, a new vertical for the platform.
- **2025**: Expanded into **Australia and Japan** — significant geographic expansion beyond Southeast Asia into higher-spending travel markets.

## Key Products and Services

### Core Travel
- **Flights** — comprehensive flight search and booking; domestic and international; integration with 100+ airlines
- **Hotels** — over 220,000 properties globally (thanks to Expedia partnership); ranging from budget to luxury
- **Train tickets** — Indonesia domestic train bookings (a unique feature for Indonesian market)
- **Attractions & Experiences** — "Traveloka Xperience" — tours, activities,主题公园, local experiences

### Financial Services
- **Traveloka PayLater** — BNPL (Buy Now, Pay Later) for travel bookings; popular in markets with low credit card penetration
- **Traveloka Credit Cards** — co-branded cards with Bank Rakyat Indonesia (BRI) and Bank Mandiri
- **PayLater Card** — physical card linked to PayLater credit facility

### Insurance and Protection
- **Shariah-compliant health insurance** — offered through partnership with FWD Group
- Flight delay/cancellation protection
- COVID-19 coverage options

### Lifestyle
- **Carbon offset** — sustainable tourism filters and carbon offset options for bookings
- **Airport transfer** — pre-booked car services at major airports

## Competitive Position

Traveloka dominates the Indonesian online travel market with an estimated **65-70% market share** in online travel bookings. Its primary competitors:
- **TikTok.com** (formerly Tokopedia travel) — growing but smaller OTA in Indonesia
- **Traveloka** vs **Booking.com** (global platform with strong hotel inventory) — Traveloka has stronger flights, Booking.com has stronger hotels
- **Airbnb** — for accommodation, though Traveloka has direct hotel relationships

The company's early focus on flights (the highest-frequency travel booking) and its mobile-first approach aligned perfectly with Indonesian consumer behavior, creating a durable competitive moat.
