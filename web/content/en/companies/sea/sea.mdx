# Sea Limited

Sea Limited (NYSE: SE) is a Singapore-based Southeast Asian technology conglomerate operating three core businesses: **Shopee** (e-commerce), **Garena** (digital entertainment/gaming), and **SeaMoney** (digital financial services). Founded in 2009 as **Garena Online** by **Forrest Li** (李小冬) — a Singapore permanent resident and former Starburst Networks employee — the company initially operated as an online game publisher before expanding into e-commerce and fintech. Sea is often compared to a "Tencent + Alibaba + Paytm" combined for Southeast Asia, as it replicated Tencent's gaming monetization model, Alibaba's e-commerce playbook, and Paytm's fintech expansion within one regional group. The company listed on the NYSE in October 2017 at $8.90/share, raising $889 million, and grew to become Southeast Asia's most valuable internet company at its 2021 peak (~$200B market cap) before the global tech correction. With operations across Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines, Vietnam, Taiwan, and Brazil (Shopee), Sea Limited has transformed from a gaming company into a diversified technology platform serving hundreds of millions of consumers and merchants.

## Timeline

### 2009–2013: Garena — Gaming Platform Origins

- **2009**: **Garena Online** founded in Singapore by **Forrest Li** — initially operated as an online game publishing platform, licensing and publishing games for Southeast Asian markets. Garena's early success came from publishing MMORPGs (Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Games) and competitive PC games in a region where few global publishers had local presence.
- **2010**: Garena launched **Garena+** (later renamed **Shell**), a gaming social platform and client that integrated chat, friends lists, and game launching — creating a gaming ecosystem similar to Steam but focused on Southeast Asia. Garena+ became one of the most widely downloaded PC applications in Southeast Asia.
- **2012**: Garena launched **Heroes of Newerth** (HoN) in Southeast Asia — one of the region's first major competitive esports titles, establishing Garena as the pioneer of esports publishing in the region.
- **2012**: **Shopee** was launched as a **mobile-first social commerce marketplace** in Singapore — initially positioned as a C2C marketplace focusing on fashion and lifestyle products. Shopee's model combined elements of eBay's auction format with social features like following sellers and user reviews.
- **2013**: Garena published **League of Legends** in Southeast Asia — a transformative deal that made Garena synonymous with esports in the region. Riot Games' LoL became the dominant MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena) game across Southeast Asia, generating enormous revenue through Garena's publishing.

### 2014–2016: Shopee Scaling and SeaGroup Formation

- **2014**: Garena raised **$260M Series C** from Sequoia Capital India and others — valuing the company at ~$1B, crossing into unicorn territory. The funding accelerated both Garena's gaming expansion and Shopee's growth.
- **2015**: Garena rebranded to **SeaGroup** (stylized as "Sea") — reflecting the broader ambitions beyond gaming. SeaGroup comprised Garena (gaming), Shopee (e-commerce), and AirPay (digital payments).
- **2015**: **AirPay** launched — a digital payment service to facilitate transactions across Sea's platforms and third-party merchants. This was the precursor to SeaMoney.
- **2016**: **Shopee** expanded aggressively across all major Southeast Asian markets — Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines — copying the successful mobile-first, social commerce model from its Singapore launch. Shopee differentiated from Alibaba's Lazada by focusing on mobile and social features.

### 2017: NYSE IPO and Gaming Peak

- **October 2017**: Sea Limited (ticker: SE) listed on the **New York Stock Exchange** — priced at $8.90/share, raising $889M at a valuation of approximately $5.15B. The IPO was notable for being one of the few major Southeast Asian tech companies to list on a US exchange rather than Asian markets.
- **2017**: **Garena** reached peak profitability — generating the cash flows that funded Shopee's aggressive expansion. Garena's success was driven primarily by League of Legends, which remained the dominant game in Southeast Asia through 2017-2018.
- **2017**: Shopee launched in **Brazil** — an early international expansion beyond Southeast Asia, testing whether the Shopee model could work in a large, emerging market with similar mobile-first consumer behavior.

### 2018–2020: Shopee vs. Lazada — E-Commerce Dominance Battle

- **2018**: **Shopee overtook Lazada** (Alibaba-backed) as the #1 e-commerce platform in Southeast Asia by GMV — a major milestone. Shopee's mobile-first approach, aggressive marketing, and social commerce features resonated more with Southeast Asian consumers than Lazada's desktop-legacy model.
- **2018**: Sea raised **$575M from Tencent** — the Chinese tech giant took a minority stake, providing both capital and strategic endorsement. Tencent's involvement was significant given its track record as the world's largest gaming company.
- **2019**: **SeaMoney** (formerly AirPay) launched as a comprehensive digital payments service — including e-wallet, payment processing, and later lending products. SeaMoney expanded rapidly by leveraging Shopee's merchant and consumer base.
- **2019**: Shopee became the **most downloaded shopping app** in Southeast Asia — surpassing Lazada and other competitors on both iOS App Store and Google Play.
- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic accelerated Sea's growth dramatically — e-commerce adoption surged as lockdowns forced consumers online. Shopee saw GMV growth accelerate significantly in 2020, with gross orders increasing ~100% YoY in some quarters.

### 2021: Peak Valuation and Expansion

- **2021**: Sea Limited reached its **peak market cap of ~$200 billion** during the global tech boom — making it Southeast Asia's most valuable internet company by a wide margin. Sea's stock price rose from ~$40 at the start of 2020 to ~$360 in October 2021.
- **2021**: **Shopee** expanded into **India** (pilot) and further **Latin America** — testing new markets beyond the core Southeast Asian and Taiwanese operations.
- **2021**: Sea raised **$6.3 billion in convertible bonds and equity** — an enormous capital raise at peak valuations, providing war chest for continued expansion. The capital raise was controversial in hindsight as it occurred near the market peak.
- **2021**: **Garena** began experiencing declining game revenue — as major titles like League of Legends matured and faced competition from mobile games and other publishers. Garena's Free Fire ($1 per download, battle royale) became an important new title, particularly in emerging markets.

### 2022–2023: The Correction and Profitability Push

- **2022**: Sea's stock price collapsed from ~$360 to ~$50 — an ~85% decline from peak. The correction was driven by rising interest rates globally, concerns about Sea's path to profitability, and regulatory issues in some markets. Sea's $200B peak valuation fell to ~$25B.
- **2022**: Sea announced a **$1B share buyback** — attempting to signal confidence in the stock's undervaluation. The buyback was partially funded by Tencent's sale of some Sea shares.
- **2022**: **Shopee** withdrew from **India**, **France**, and several other markets — right-sizing the business after the overexpansion of 2021. This was a significant strategic pivot toward profitability.
- **2023**: Sea reported **first full-year profit** — a landmark achievement after 14+ years of losses. The shift to profitability was driven by Shopee's advertising revenue growth, reduced promotional spending, and Garena's cost rationalization. Net income was ~$16M for FY2023.
- **2023**: **Garena** restructured — cutting staff significantly and restructuring game publishing operations as Free Fire's revenue declined. Garena became a smaller, more focused business.

### 2024–2025: Stabilization and Growth

- **2024**: Sea Limited reported **$3.3B revenue** (FY2024) — with Shopee as the dominant contributor. E-commerce segment revenue grew driven by advertising and logistics services, partially offsetting declines in gaming.
- **2024**: **Shopee** maintained its #1 position in **7 out of 8 Southeast Asian markets** — with strong positions in Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia, Singapore, and Taiwan. Lazada remained the #1 in some categories in Indonesia but overall GMV leadership remained with Shopee.
- **2024**: **SeaMoney** (digital financial services) grew significantly — offering e-wallet, lending, and insurance products to consumers and merchants across Southeast Asia. SeaMoney had tens of millions of active users by 2024.
- **2025**: Sea announced continued investment in **AI for e-commerce** — using machine learning for product recommendations, fraud detection, and logistics optimization. Shopee's data advantage from hundreds of millions of transactions provided a strong AI training dataset.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Shopee (E-Commerce)
- **Mobile-first C2C/B2C marketplace** — dominant in 7 of 8 SEA markets
- **Shopee Live** — livestream e-commerce (social commerce)
- **ShopeePay** — integrated payment wallet within Shopee
- **ShopeeFood** — food delivery (competing with GrabFood and GoFood in respective markets)
- **SLS (Shopee Logistics Network)** — proprietary last-mile logistics solution
- **SPX Express** — Sea's logistics subsidiary for express delivery
- Revenue model: Transaction fees, advertising (Shopee Ads),增值服务 (premium seller services), ShopeePay take-rate

### Garena (Digital Entertainment)
- **Free Fire** — battle royale mobile game; one of the most downloaded mobile games globally in 2021-2022; popular in India, Southeast Asia, and Latin America
- **League of Legends: Wild Rift** — mobile MOBA version of LoL
- **Arena of Valor** — Tencent's MOBA game published by Garena in some SEA markets
- Game publishing for third-party titles across PC and mobile
- Revenue: In-app purchases, battle passes, character skins

### SeaMoney (Digital Financial Services)
- **SeaMoney e-wallet** — digital payments across Shopee and third-party merchants
- **SPayLater** — buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) service
- **C端贷款** — consumer and merchant lending
- **Insurance distribution** — partner with insurers to distribute policies
- **AirPay merchant services** — QR code payments for small businesses
