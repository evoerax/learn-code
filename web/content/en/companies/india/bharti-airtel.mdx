# Bharti Airtel

Bharti Airtel Limited is an Indian multinational telecommunications company headquartered in New Delhi, operating in 18 countries across South Asia, Africa, and the Channel Islands. Founded in 1995 by **Sunil Mittal**, Airtel has grown to become **India's second-largest mobile network operator** (behind Reliance Jio) and the **second-largest mobile operator globally** by subscribers, serving approximately **600 million customers** across its footprint as of September 2025. The company generated FY2025 revenue of ₹172,985 crore (~$20 billion) with net income of ₹37,481 crore (~$4.4 billion), making it one of India's most valuable and profitable companies. Airtel operates across the full telecom value chain — mobile telephony (5G, 4G, LTE Advanced), fixed-line broadband, satellite television, DTH, IPTV, and digital payments through Airtel Payments Bank. Its 2024 agreement to purchase a **24.5% stake in BT Group** (formerly British Telecom) for approximately £3 billion marked a major strategic move into global telecommunications infrastructure.

## Timeline

### 1984–1994: Pre-founding and Early Years

- **1984**: **Sunil Mittal** started assembling push-button phones in India — the beginning of his telecommunications career before founding Airtel.
- **1992**: Bharti Group (Mittal's company) successfully bid for a mobile phone network license in India's first mobile telecom auction, positioning the company for the upcoming liberalization of India's telecom sector.
- **1994**: License plans formally approved by the Government of India.

### 1995–2003: Founding and Domestic Expansion

- **July 7, 1995**: **Bharti Cellular Limited (BCL)** formally incorporated. Launched mobile services in **Delhi** — Airtel's first market.
- **1999**: Acquired control of **JT Holdings**, expanding operations to Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh — Airtel's first geographic expansions beyond Delhi.
- **2000**: Acquired **Skycell Communications** in Chennai, significantly expanding Tamil Nadu presence.
- **2001**: Acquired **Spice Cell** in Kolkata, establishing a pan-India presence in major metropolitan markets.
- **2002**: Listed on **Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)** and **National Stock Exchange (NSE)** — becoming one of the earliest listed Indian telecom companies.
- **2003**: Rebranded all cellular operations under single **Airtel brand** — creating India's first pan-India unified telecom brand.

### 2004–2009: Brand Building and International Entry

- **2004**: Acquired **Hexacom**; entered Rajasthan market. Launched **Hello Tunes** — India's first caller ringback tone service, which became enormously popular and a significant revenue stream.
- **2005**: Extended network to the **Andaman and Nicobar Islands**, completing pan-India coverage.
- **2007**: Launched mobile services in **Jersey and Guernsey** (Channel Islands) through Airtel-Vodafone joint venture with Vodafone.
- **2009**: Launched first international mobile network in **Sri Lanka**. Signed landmark **10-year network deal with Ericsson** for Africa networks — one of the largest telecom infrastructure contracts at the time.

### 2010: African Expansion — Zain Acquisition

- **April 2010**: Acquired **Zain's Africa operations** for **$10.7 billion** — the largest ever acquisition by an Indian company at that time. The deal gave Airtel operations in **15 African countries**: Benin, Burkina Faso, Chad, Congo, Democratic Republic of Congo, Gabon, Ghana, Kenya, Madagascar,马拉维, Niger, Nigeria, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, Tanzania, Uganda, and Zambia.
- **2010**: Acquired **Telecom Seychelles** for $62 million.
- **2010**: Acquired **70.90% stake in Warid Telecom Bangladesh** for $300 million.
- **2010**: Global rebranding — introduced new lowercase **"airtel"** logo, marking the company's ambition to be a global brand.

### 2011–2016: Consolidating and Restructuring

- **2012**: Tied up with **Walmart** for retail store partnerships across India.
- **2012**: Acquired **60% stake in Wireless Business Services Private Limited** (Qualcomm's India 4G joint venture) for ₹9.07 billion.
- **2013**: Acquired **100% of Qualcomm's 4G business** in India — giving Airtel a dominant position in Indian 4G spectrum.
- **2014**: Planned acquisition of **Loop Mobile** (called off later) — would have added significant Mumbai subscriber base.
- **2015**: Purchased **Augere Wireless Broadband India Private Limited**, adding 4G spectrum in key markets.
- **2016**: Sold operations in **Burkina Faso and Sierra Leone** to Orange — first African divestitures to improve portfolio quality.
- **2016**: Airtel Bangladesh merged into **Robi** (Airtel held minority stake, later fully merged).

### 2017–2020: Major Acquisitions and Tata Docomo

- **2017**: Merged **Airtel-Tigo in Ghana** with Millicom's Tigo (Airtel received 36.85% stake in combined entity).
- **2017**: Acquired **Telenor India** assets and customers — zero cash consideration (only ₹1,600 crore spectrum payments assumed) — giving Airtel ~400 MHz spectrum in 6 circles and ~30 million subscribers.
- **2017**: Acquired **Tikona Infinet Limited's 4G spectrum** for approximately ₹1,600 crore.
- **2017**: Signed agreement to acquire **Tata Teleservices** and **Tata Docomo's** consumer mobile business — a complex transaction involving debt of ₹740 billion.
- **2019**: Completed merger of **Tata Docomo** with Airtel on July 1 — making Airtel India's largest telecom company by revenue market share, ahead of Vodafone India (the former Hulett) and Reliance Jio.

### 2021–2023: Digital Services and 5G

- **2022**: Acquired strategic stake in blockchain startup **Aqilliz** — Airtel's first notable venture into Web3 infrastructure.
- **2023**: Airtel Africa completed listing on the **London Stock Exchange** — raising $750 million in primary capital and valuing Airtel Africa's business at ~$10 billion.

### 2024–2025: Strategic Investments and AI Partnerships

- **2024**: Collaboration with **Google Cloud** for cloud adoption and generative AI solutions for businesses in India — multi-year strategic partnership.
- **July 2024**: Completed **India's first non-standalone 5G Cloud-RAN trial with Nokia** — achieving over 1.2 Gbps throughput in a live network demonstration.
- **December 2024**: Signed **multi-billion dollar deal with Ericsson** for 4G/5G network deployment across India — a multi-year network modernization contract.
- **2024**: **Bharti Global** (Airtel's international arm) agreed to purchase **24.5% stake in BT Group** (formerly British Telecom) for approximately £3 billion — making Airtel the largest shareholder in one of Europe's largest telecommunications companies. The deal was expected to close in 2025.
- **March 2025**: Signed strategic agreement with **SpaceX's Starlink** for satellite-based internet services in India — pending regulatory approval.
- **September 2025**: **600 million customers** milestone achieved.

## Business Segments

### India Mobile
- **5G** services launched in major cities (2022); pan-India 5G rollout by 2024
- **4G/LTE Advanced**: Dominant position following Tata Docomo merger
- **2G/3G**: Still operational in rural areas
- **Airtel Thanks** — loyalty program with exclusive content, discounts, and priority service

### Africa Operations
14 countries: Chad, DRC, Congo, Gabon, Kenya, Madagascar, Malawi, Niger, Nigeria, Rwanda, Seychelles, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia
- Airtel Africa operates as a separately listed entity (LSE, 2023)
- Mobile money (Airtel Money) is a major differentiator — 31.8 million active users in Q4 FY2024

### Other Services
- **Airtel Payments Bank** — digital banking and payments
- **Airtel Business** — enterprise connectivity, cloud, cybersecurity
- **Airtel Xstream** — OTT video streaming aggregation
- **DTH (Direct-to-Home)** — satellite television service
- **Fixed-line broadband** — fiber to home (Xstream Fiber)

Airtel has demonstrated consistent revenue growth and margin improvement since the Tata Docomo merger eliminated the competitive threat from a major rival. The company's focus on 4G subscriber additions and ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) improvement through data bundling has been central to financial performance.

## Ownership Structure

- **Bharti Telecom** (controlled by Mittal family): 40.47%
- **Singapore Telecommunications (Singtel)**: 8.32%
- **Indian Continent Investment Limited**: 2.47%
- **Public float**: 48.74%
- **Note**: Singtel has been a long-term strategic investor since 1994 — Bharti and Singtel have cooperated on network sharing and technology
