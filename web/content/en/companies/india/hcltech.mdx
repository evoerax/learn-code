# HCLTech

HCLTech (HCL Technologies) is a leading global technology services and consulting company headquartered in Noida, India, with $13 billion in FY2024 revenue and over 225,000 employees. Founded in 1976 by Shiv Nadar (often called the "Father of the Indian IT industry") as Hindustan Computers Limited (HCL), the company began as one of India's first technology companies developing indigenous mini-computers — a radical act in a country almost entirely dependent on foreign technology. HCL's distinctive "Products and Services" dual-engine model has always kept product development alongside services, differentiating it from pure-play outsourcing competitors. The company's 2019 landmark $12.9 billion agreement with IBM — the largest in Indian IT history — to manage IBM's legacy infrastructure services business was transformative. Under CEO C. Vijayakumar (since 2019), HCLTech has accelerated its cloud-first strategy, launched enterprise AI practices, and acquired leading companies including Cisco's external maintenance business (2019), Strong-Bridge Envelope (2020), and Actian (2023). HCLTech operates across 60 countries and serves clients in financial services, manufacturing, technology, and life sciences. Shiv Nadar remains chairman and is India's third-richest person, with a net worth exceeding $35 billion.

## Timeline

### 1976–1990: Founding and Mini-Computer Pioneer

- **1976**: Shiv Nadar, Ajai Chowdhary, and five others founded **Hindustan Computers Limited (HCL)** in a small room in Delhi with ₹187,000 ($20,000) capital. Their first product was a punch card machine — a dramatic contrast to the minicomputer ambitions that followed. The founding team were all electronics engineers from Indian institutions.
- **1978**: HCL developed India's first indigenous minicomputer — the **HCL 80** — competing with imported DEC and HP machines at a fraction of the price. This was a period when India's foreign exchange controls made importing computers extremely difficult and expensive.
- **1980**: HCL developed the **HCL 80/10** — an improved version that found customers in Indian government, defense, and educational institutions.
- **1981**: HCL 80/20 launched — more powerful version of the minicomputer.
- **1983**: HCL introduced the **HCL 80/30** — the company's most successful early product, sold to Indian railways, banks, and universities.
- **1985**: HCL developed the **HCL 8000** — a 32-bit minicomputer. The company expanded to Singapore, US, and Middle East.
- **1986**: HCL went public on Indian stock exchanges — raising capital to fund international expansion. Shiv Nadar became one of India's first tech entrepreneurs.
- **1989**: HCL established a US development center in Texas — one of the earliest Indian IT companies to establish a significant US presence.

### 1990–2000: Services Pivot and Growth

- **1990**: HCL split into separate companies — **HCL Laboratories** (products R&D) and **HCL Overseas** (export services) — reflecting the emerging importance of offshore software development.
- **1991**: India's economic liberalization (the "Rupee float") dramatically reduced the cost of imported technology — suddenly HCL's indigenous minicomputer business faced severe competition from imported IBM, HP, and DEC systems.
- **1992**: HCL pivoted aggressively toward software services export — riding the "Y2K" consulting boom as US and European companies needed help fixing date-code issues in legacy systems.
- **1994**: HCL established dedicated offshore development centers in India — the beginnings of the global delivery model that Indian IT companies would later perfect.
- **1995**: HCL founded **HCL Technologies** as the dedicated software services arm — separating products (HCL Labs) from services (HCL Technologies).
- **1999**: HCL went through major restructuring — spun off HCL Labs, and established HCL Technologies as the primary growth vehicle. Shiv Nadar took over as chairman of the entire HCL group.

### 2000–2010: Global Expansion and IPO

- **2000**: Y2K boom drove HCL's software services revenue up 40%+ — establishing the company as a serious global IT services provider.
- **2003**: HCL established **HCL Enterprise** as the holding company — unifying HCL Technologies, HCL Labs, and other subsidiaries.
- **2004**: HCL launched **"FREND"** — Forward Enterprise Results and Development — a proprietary methodology for enterprise software delivery that became a competitive differentiator.
- **2006**: HCL acquired **内裤** (various US and European IT services firms) — building scale and domain expertise.
- **2006**: HCL Technologies listed on Indian stock exchanges (NSE, BSE) — IPO raised ₹280 billion (~$6 billion at the time).
- **2007**: HCL launched **HCL AXIOM** — its cloud services brand, one of the earliest Indian IT companies to offer enterprise cloud solutions.
- **2008**: HCL opened its first delivery center in Vietnam — expanding Asia-Pacific offshore footprint.
- **2009**: HCL won multi-year outsourcing deals from major US and European banks during the financial crisis — paradoxically a period of growth as cost-conscious clients moved work to India.

### 2011–2019: IBM Mega Deal and CEO Transition

- **2012**: HCL acquired **PointNext** (US IT services firm) — strengthening infrastructure services in North America.
- **2013**: HCL launched **Mode 1-2-3** strategy — Mode 1 (core services), Mode 2 (digital transformation), Mode 3 (engineering/R&D services) — a framework that became central to its positioning against competitors.
- **2015**: HCL acquired **Magic扁** (US DevOps firm) — adding modern software delivery capabilities.
- **2017**: HCL acquired **Actian** (US data analytics company, $420M) — adding high-performance in-memory database technology.
- **2018**: Shiv Nadar stepped down as CEO of HCL Technologies — succeeded by **C. Vijayakumar** (who had been president and COO). Shiv Nadar continued as chairman.
- **2019**: HCL completed the landmark **IBM $12.9 billion acquisition** — taking over IBM's legacy infrastructure management services (servers, storage, networking, help desk) and 5,000+ IBM employees globally. This was the largest deal in Indian IT history and transformed HCL into a global infrastructure services leader.
- **2019**: HCL acquired **Cisco's Customer Experience business** — adding 2,500+ Cisco-certified engineers.

### 2020–2025: Cloud-First and AI Era

- **2020**: HCL launched **HCL CloudNative** — dedicated practice for cloud migration and native development.
- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic accelerated digital transformation demand — HCL's Mode 2 (digital) business grew 30%+ as clients invested in remote work infrastructure.
- **2021**: HCL launched **HCL Spark** — innovation labs and startup ecosystem for emerging technologies.
- **2022**: HCL acquired **Strong-Bridge Envelope** (US digital transformation consulting, $110M) — adding design thinking and digital strategy capabilities.
- **2022**: HCL launched **HCL BigFix** — AI-powered workplace services and automation.
- **2023**: HCL acquired **Actian** (US data platform company, $330M) — integrating data fabric and analytics into HCL's cloud offerings.
- **2023**: HCL launched **HCLTech AI** — enterprise AI practice combining generative AI, ML, and data engineering.
- **2024**: HCL partnered with Microsoft, AWS, Google Cloud, and Snowflake on joint go-to-market — expanding hyperscaler-aligned services.
- **2024**: HCL reported $13B FY2024 revenue, +9.5% YoY — one of the fastest-growing large Indian IT services companies.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Mode 1 (Core Services)
- **Application Development & Maintenance (ADM)** — legacy modernization, ERP (SAP, Oracle), custom applications
- **Infrastructure Services** — legacy data center, networking, storage, help desk (enhanced by IBM acquisition)
- **BPO** — business process operations

### Mode 2 (Digital Transformation)
- **Cloud and Digital** — cloud-native development, DevOps, microservices, API management
- **Experience Services** — digital customer experience, digital marketing, CRM
- **Digital Process** — robotic process automation, intelligent workflows

### Mode 3 (Engineering & R&D)
- **Product Engineering** — embedded systems, semiconductor design, automotive software
- **HCLTech Digital Business** — IoT platforms, connected products
- **Engineering Services** — aerospace, automotive, industrial machinery software

### AI and Data
- **HCLTech AI** — generative AI solutions, enterprise LLM integration
- **Actian** — high-performance analytics database, data fabric
- **Data Engineering** — lakehouse architecture, real-time analytics

## Strategic Position

HCLTech's Mode 1-2-3 framework is distinctive: most Indian IT companies focused purely on Mode 1 (cost-arbitrage outsourcing) while HCL invested heavily in Mode 3 (engineering and R&D services) — winning product development contracts from semiconductor, automotive, and aerospace clients. The IBM infrastructure services acquisition ($12.9B, 2019) was the most consequential bet in Indian IT history — it instantly made HCL a top-3 global infrastructure services provider alongside TCS and Infosys. CEO C. Vijayakumar's cloud-first and AI-first strategy has kept HCL growing faster than some larger peers. The Actian acquisition positions HCL for the data fabric market, while the GenAI practice targets the $200B+ enterprise AI services market.
