# Wipro

Wipro Limited (NYSE: WIT) is a leading global technology services and consulting company headquartered in Bengaluru, India, with $10.7 billion in FY2024 revenue and over 230,000 employees. Founded in 1945 as Western India Vegetable Products (Wipro) — a vegetable oil manufacturer — the company transformed entirely under the leadership of Azim Premji (Chairman 1969-2019) into one of the world's most respected IT services firms. Unlike TCS and Infosys which focused purely on software exports, Wipro pursued a distinctive "40/40" model — 40% in services and 40% in business process services (BPO), differentiating through consulting and domain expertise. Under current CEO Thierry Delaporte (since 2020, former Capgemini executive), Wipro has accelerated cloud transformation, AI integration, and large-scale acquisitions including Capco ($1.5B, 2021) and Rizing (2022). Wipro operates across 66 countries and serves 1,500+ clients including 250+ of the Global 500. The company's unique culture of "post-it innovation" (employees encouraged to spend 20% of time on passion projects) has produced numerous internal entrepreneurs.

## Timeline

### 1945–1980: Origins as Vegetable Oil Company

- **December 29, 1945**: Wipro founded as **Western India Vegetable Products** by M.H. Premji (Azim Premji's father) in Amalner, Maharashtra, with initial capital of ₹21,000. Business: manufacturing vegetable oils, ghee substitutes, and soap under the brand name "Kisan" and "Sunflower."
- **1956**: Azim Premji, then 11 years old, inherited controlling stake in Wipro after his father's death. He initially continued his studies abroad at Stanford University before returning to run the business in 1966.
- **1969**: Azim Premji (age 21) took full control of Wipro after completing his electrical engineering degree at Stanford. He shifted the company's focus from consumer products to technology and professional services — a radical pivot for a profitable FMCG business.
- **1970s**: Wipro began manufacturing hydraulic cylinders and other industrial equipment, diversifying beyond consumer goods.

### 1980–1999: Entry into IT Services

- **1980**: Azim Premji visited the US and was inspired by the success of IBM and mini-computer companies. Wipro started providing IT services and hardware support — initially as a distributor for mini-computers (Data General, Prime).
- **1981**: Wipro set up Wipro Systems to focus on computer hardware and systems integration — one of India's earliest IT services companies.
- **1988**: Wipro Systems began developing specialized software for clients in the US — the precursor to the IT services business model.
- **1990**: Wipro GE Medical Systems established — joint venture with GE Healthcare to manufacture medical equipment in India.
- **1994**: Wipro's IT services division gained independence — began building proprietary software and providing IT consulting to global clients.
- **1995**: Wipro became the first Indian company to have a dedicated hardware R&D center — developing telecom equipment.
- **1997**: Wipro's IT services division was formally established and began focusing on Year 2000 (Y2K) compliance services — one of the first Indian firms to target this massive global market.

### 2000–2010: Growth and Global Expansion

- **2000**: Y2K crisis proved a windfall — Wipro's IT services revenue grew 66% as companies globally scrambled to fix date-code issues. This established Wipro's reputation in global enterprise software.
- **2000**: Azim Premji donated 60% of his personal wealth to the Azim Premji Foundation — establishing one of India's largest private charitable trusts.
- **2002**: Wipro acquired nRunnable (US-based) — early strategic move to strengthen US presence.
- **2003**: Wipro acquired Nervewire (US) for $18M — added financial services domain expertise in the US.
- **2004**: Wipro became the first Indian IT company to launch a global shared services center model — optimizing delivery across time zones.
- **2005**: Wipro's BPO division (Wipro BPO) became one of India's largest business process outsourcing companies — serving banking, healthcare, and insurance clients.
- **2007**: Wipro acquired enHabit (US) — building capabilities in healthcare IT.
- **2008**: Wipro became the first Indian IT services company to receive the prestigious Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award.
- **2009**: Azim Premji returned Wipro to profitability after the 2008 financial crisis by cutting costs and focusing on higher-margin consulting work.

### 2011–2019: Transformation and New Leadership

- **2011**: Azim Premji stepped back from daily operations — appointed Son **Soumitro** (Azim Premji's son) to lead Wipro's consumer care business, but retained full control of the technology services business.
- **2013**: Wipro launched "自动化" (Automation) initiatives — deploying robotic process automation (RPA) to reduce costs and improve delivery efficiency.
- **2015**: Wipro acquired ATCO's IT services division (Canada) — strengthening presence in North America.
- **2015**: Wipro's board announced that Azim Premji's son would not succeed him as chairman — breaking with Indian IT industry norm of family succession; independent directors would choose the next chairman.
- **2016**: Wipro launched digital practice — focusing on cloud, analytics, and IoT services. Began repositioning from pure-play outsourcing to "transformational" IT consulting.
- **2017**: Abidali Neemuchwala appointed CEO (from TCS) — brought fresh perspective on digital transformation.
- **2019**: Wipro completed acquisition of **Appirio** (cloud services, $500M) — adding Salesforce, Workday, and cloud implementation capabilities.
- **2019**: **Azim Premji** retired as chairman (effective July 2019), handing over to **Prakash Kollepara** as executive chairman — transition marked the end of an era (50 years of Premji family leadership).
- **2019**: Wipro had 175,000+ employees across 66 countries; revenue reached $8.6B.

### 2020–2025: Thierry Delaporte Era and AI Pivot

- **July 2020**: **Thierry Delaporte** (former Capgemini Europe COO, 25+ years at Capgemini) appointed CEO — the first non-Indian CEO in Wipro's history. This was a significant cultural shift.
- **2020**: Delaporte launched "SDP 2.0" (Service Delivery Platform) restructuring — reorganized Wipro from geography-based to service-line-based structure (20+ business units), improving accountability and client focus.
- **2021**: Wipro acquired **Capco** (global management consulting, $1.5B) — largest acquisition in Wipro's history; added financial services consulting depth across US and Europe.
- **2021**: Wipro acquired **Rizing** (SAP implementation services, ~$350M) — strengthening enterprise software implementation capabilities.
- **2022**: Wipro launched Wipro IVI (Industrial IoT) — partnership with BMW for connected vehicle solutions.
- **2022**: Wipro launched "Transformative AI" practice — integrating generative AI and LLM services across its consulting portfolio.
- **2023**: Wipro FullStride Cloud Services launched — comprehensive cloud transformation offering with hyperscaler partnerships (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud).
- **2024**: Wipro divested its Singapore-based data center assets to reduce debt and focus on cloud services.
- **2024**: Wipro reported $10.7B FY2024 revenue with operating margin of ~18% — the company achieved its highest-ever quarterly revenue in Q4 FY2024.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### IT Services (Core)
- **Application Development & Maintenance (ADM)** — legacy modernization, cloud-native development
- **Cloud Infrastructure Services** — AWS, Azure, GCP migration and management
- **Digital Operations** — ITOM, AIOps, automation
- **Cybersecurity Services** — consulting, SOC operations, zero-trust architecture
- **Data & Analytics** — data engineering, business intelligence, AI/ML

### Consulting and Business Process
- **Wipro Consulting** — strategy, operations, technology consulting
- **Capco** (acquired 2021) — financial services management consulting; 30+ offices globally
- **Wipro BPO** — business process outsourcing for banking, insurance, healthcare
- **Rizing** (acquired 2022) — SAP and Oracle implementation services

### Industry Solutions
- **Financial Services** (largest vertical) — banking, capital markets, insurance
- **Healthcare & Life Sciences** — pharma, medical devices, healthcare providers
- **Retail & Manufacturing** — CPG, retail, industrial manufacturing
- **Communications, Media & Entertainment** — telecom, media companies
- **Energy, Natural Resources & Utilities** — energy sector digital transformation

## Strategic Position

Wipro occupies a distinctive niche in Indian IT: unlike pure-play software exporters (TCS, Infosys), Wipro has historically maintained a consulting-heavy model with higher margins and deeper client relationships. Thierry Delaporte's restructuring and the Capco acquisition have accelerated Wipro's shift toward higher-value consulting work. The challenge is that Wipro is smaller than TCS ($27.9B) and Infosys ($18.6B) and lacks their global scale in some verticals — competing with Accenture and Capgemini in consulting while maintaining Indian IT cost advantages is a difficult middle position. The company's ~230,000 employees and $10.7B revenue make it the #3 Indian IT services company behind TCS and Infosys.
