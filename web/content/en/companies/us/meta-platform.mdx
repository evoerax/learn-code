# Meta

Meta Platforms, Inc. (Nasdaq: META), formerly Facebook, Inc., is the world's largest social media company, founded by Mark Zuckerberg in 2004. Headquartered in Menlo Park, California, Meta operates a family of applications used by over 3 billion people daily — Facebook (3.07B monthly active users), Instagram (2B+), WhatsApp (2B+), and Messenger (1B+). With $201 billion in revenue (2025) and a market capitalization exceeding $1.4 trillion, Meta is one of the most influential technology platforms globally, though it faces ongoing regulatory scrutiny over antitrust, privacy, and content moderation. CEO Mark Zuckerberg controls approximately 61.2% of shareholder voting power.

## Timeline

### 2004–2010: Facebook Founding and Growth

- **February 4, 2004**: **Mark Zuckerberg**, Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes launch **TheFacebook.com** from their Harvard University dorm room. The initial concept was "Facemash" — a beauty contest website rating Harvard students.
- **2004**: Facebook expands to Stanford, Columbia, and Yale. The name "TheFacebook" was inspired by "TheFacebook" (a Harvard face-book directory). In March 2004, the Harvard Crimson called it "the latest campuswide obsession."
- **2005**: Expands beyond Ivy League to most US universities. Raised $12.7M in Series A from Accel Partners. Renamed to **Facebook, Inc.** (dropped "The").
- **2006**: Facebook opens registration to anyone age 13+ worldwide (previously required university email). Launched **Facebook Platform** — allowing third-party developers to build apps within Facebook (e.g., FarmVille, Zynga Poker).
- **2007**: Microsoft invests $240M for 1.6% stake ($15B valuation). Facebook launches **Facebook Ads** — its first significant revenue product.
- **2008**: Sheryl Sandberg joins as COO (from Google) — transforming Facebook's advertising business. Facebook surpasses **200 million active users**.
- **2009**: Facebook becomes profitable for the first time. **Like button** launched — transforming social engagement.
- **2010**: Facebook hits **500 million users** — becoming the world's largest social network. Opens first international offices.

### 2011–2015: Mobile, IPO, and Instagram

- **2011**: Facebook launches **Timeline** profile redesign. Google+ launches as potential Facebook competitor (failed to unseat Facebook).
- **2012**: **May 18, 2012** — Facebook IPO on Nasdaq at $38/share, raising $16 billion ($104B valuation at float) — the largest tech IPO at the time. Acquired **Instagram** for **$1 billion** in cash (August) — a deal that raised significant scrutiny after the fact as Instagram had just 13 employees and $0 revenue.
- **2013**: Facebook hits **1 billion monthly active users**. Acquired **Onavo** (data analytics, VPN) — later used to identify acquisition targets (WhatsApp). Facebook introduces **Facebook Home** for Android (flop).
- **2014**: Facebook changes corporate name to **Facebook, Inc.** (from TheFacebook). Acquired **WhatsApp** for **$19 billion** (February) — the largest tech acquisition at the time. Acquired **Oculus VR** for **$2.3 billion** (March) — pivoting into virtual reality.
- **2015**: Facebook crosses **1.5 billion monthly active users**. Messenger spun off as separate app. WhatsApp introduced voice calling.

### 2016–2020: Growth, Cambridge Analytica, and Libra

- **2016**: Facebook introduces **Facebook Watch** video platform. Russia-linked accounts discovered spreading political disinformation during 2016 US election — beginning years of scrutiny.
- **2017**: Facebook Messenger launches AI assistant M (later discontinued). Facebook reaches **2 billion monthly active users**.
- **2018**: **March 2018** — **Cambridge Analytica scandal** erupts. It was revealed that 87 million users' data was harvested without consent by Cambridge Analytica for political advertising. Mark Zuckerberg testifies before Congress. Facebook's market cap drops $100B+ in days. Facebook introduces **Clear History** feature. Reaches **2.7 billion monthly active users** across family of apps.
- **2019**: Facebook launches **Facebook Pay** (digital payments). Libra Association formed (cryptocurrency project, later dissolved).
- **2020**: Facebook rebrands to **Meta Platforms, Inc.** (October 28, 2021) — signaling strategic shift to the "metaverse." Launched **Facebook Gaming** and **Neighborhoods** (local community features).

### 2021–2023: Meta Rebrand and Layoffs

- **2021**: Meta announces **Project Cambria** (high-end VR headset) and **Ray-Ban Stories** smart glasses. Facebook becomes Meta. Apple App Tracking Transparency (ATT) launch severely impacted Meta's advertising business (~$10B revenue hit estimated 2022).
- **2022**: Meta undergoes **largest workforce reduction in company history** — 11,000 employees (13% of workforce) laid off in November ("Layoff of the Year" per some analysts for its transparency). Reality Labs lost $9.4B in 2022. Meta's stock drops ~70% from its 2021 peak.
- **2023**: Meta launches **Threads** (July 5) — microblogging platform integrated with Instagram. Threads gained **100 million users in 5 days**, the fastest app to reach that milestone. Meta introduces **Meta AI** assistant across its apps. Meta's stock rebounds sharply — ending 2023 at record highs. Meta launches **Llama 2** open-source LLM. Meta ends Trump's suspension.

### 2024–2025: AI Renaissance and Policy Shifts

- **2024**: Meta launches **Llama 3** open-source AI models. **Meta Orion AR glasses** revealed (holographic AR prototype). Facebook introduces **Community Notes** (replacing fact-checking with X/Twitter-style crowd-sourced notes). Meta banned Russian state media. Meta crosses **3 billion daily active users** across family of apps.
- **2025**: Meta's Llama models become the foundation of a large open-source AI ecosystem. Meta **rolls back DEI initiatives** — eliminating diversity hiring programs and content moderation policies. Meta introduces **Manus AI** ($2B acquisition). Meta pivots toward AI infrastructure investment ($60B+ planned). Meta's market cap exceeds $1.4 trillion. WhatsApp reaches **3 billion users** globally.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Family of Apps
- **Facebook** — 3.07B monthly active users (largest social network)
- **Instagram** — 2B+ users, Reels, Stories, Shopping, Instagram Lite
- **WhatsApp** — 3B+ users, end-to-end encrypted messaging, WhatsApp Business
- **Messenger** — 1B+ users, video calling, payments (Brazil)
- **Threads** — 100M+ users, text-based microblogging (Instagram-integrated)

### AI Products
- **Meta AI** — AI assistant across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger
- **Llama** — open-source LLM family (Llama 3, Llama 4)
- **FAIR (Facebook AI Research)** — fundamental AI research
- **Emu** — image generation model
- **Manus AI** — autonomous AI agent (acquired 2025, $2B)

### Hardware (Reality Labs)
- **Meta Quest 3** — VR headset ($499)
- **Meta Quest Pro** — high-end enterprise VR ($999)
- **Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses** — camera + audio + Meta AI
- **Meta Orion** — holographic AR glasses (prototype)
- **Meta Portal** — video calling devices

### Advertising and Business
- **Meta Ads Manager** — advertising platform
- **Meta Business Suite** — business tools
- **Facebook Marketplace** — peer-to-peer commerce
- **WhatsApp Business** — business messaging API
