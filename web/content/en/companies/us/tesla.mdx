# Tesla

Tesla, Inc. (Nasdaq: TSLA) is an American electric vehicle and clean energy company founded in 2003 and headquartered in Austin, Texas. It is the world's largest electric vehicle manufacturer by market share and the most valuable automaker by market capitalization (~$900 billion as of 2025). Tesla's mission is "to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy." The company designs, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles (Model S, Model 3, Model X, Model Y, Cybertruck, Semi), battery energy storage products (Megapack, Powerwall), solar products (Solar Roof, solar panels), and AI computing hardware (Dojo supercomputer, custom FSD chips). Elon Musk, who became the company's largest shareholder and CEO in 2008, is synonymous with Tesla — his personal brand and social media presence (X/Twitter) have been both a massive marketing asset and a source of controversy. Tesla's vertically integrated business model (designing its own chips, batteries, motors, software, and manufacturing systems) and direct-to-consumer sales approach set it apart from traditional automakers. The company is also developing Full Self-Driving (FSD) technology and launched a robotaxi service in Austin in 2025.

## Timeline

### 2003–2004: Founding and Musk's Entry

- **July 1, 2003**: Tesla Motors, Inc. was founded in **San Carlos, California** by **Martin Eberhard** and **Marc Tarpenning** — both veteran Silicon Valley entrepreneurs. They aimed to build "a car manufacturer that is also a technology company" using lithium-ion battery technology for electric vehicles. The company was named after **Nikola Tesla** — the inventor of the AC induction motor.
- **Early 2004**: **Ian Wright** became Tesla's third co-founder (joined as VP of business development). He left in 2004.
- **February 2004**: **Elon Musk** led Tesla's **Series A funding round**, investing **$6.5 million** and becoming **Chairman of the Board**. Musk was the largest investor. He did not initially serve as CEO — that role went to Eberhard. J.B. Straubel joined as CTO in May 2004.
- **2005**: Musk invested an additional $12 million in a Series B round (total Musk investment: ~$18.5M by 2007). The team grew to about 20 engineers.

### 2006–2008: Roadster and the First Electric Sports Car

- **2006**: Tesla publicly announced the **Roadster** — a high-performance electric sports car based on the Lotus Elise chassis, capable of 0-60 mph in under 4 seconds and 245-mile range. Hundreds of deposits came in from Hollywood celebrities and tech elites.
- **2006**: Martin Eberhard was replaced as CEO by **Ze'ev Drori** (former PalmSource CEO) — the board brought in outside management to professionalize the company. Eberhard remained as president of technology until he was pushed out in 2007.
- **2007**: A legal settlement in 2009 officially recognized all five — Eberhard, Tarpenning, Wright, Musk, and Straubel — as Tesla co-founders.
- **2008 (October)**: **Elon Musk became Tesla's fourth CEO** (and effectively the leader since his board involvement in 2004). This was during the global financial crisis — Tesla was running out of cash and was close to bankruptcy. Musk personally invested another $40 million to keep the company alive.
- **2008**: The first **Roadster** was delivered to customer **Sanford Bishop** (a film producer and early investor). The Roadster sold approximately **2,450 units** worldwide between 2008 and 2012 at $109,000-$128,000 each.

### 2009–2012: IPO, Model S Development, and Toyota Partnership

- **2009 (May)**: Tesla received a **$465 million loan** from the US Department of Energy's Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) loan program — the same program that financed Ford and Nissan Leaf. Tesla used this loan to fund Model S development. The loan was **fully repaid in May 2013** with $12 million in interest.
- **June 2010**: Tesla went **public on NASDAQ** (June 29, 2010) — raising $226 million at $17 per share. It was the **first American car company IPO since Ford in 1956**. The stock closed at $23.89 on day one.
- **2010 (October)**: Tesla purchased the **NUMMI** (New United Motor Manufacturing Inc.) plant in **Fremont, California** from Toyota for **$42 million** — the former GM/Toyota joint venture factory. This gave Tesla its first major manufacturing facility.
- **2011**: Toyota and Tesla formed a partnership — Toyota invested $50 million in Tesla and gave access to the NUMMI plant. Toyota later sold its Tesla shares in 2016.
- **2012**: Released the **Model S** (June 22) — an all-electric luxury sedan with a range of up to 265 miles (later versions: 300+ miles). The Model S was the first Tesla built from the ground up as an electric vehicle. It won numerous awards including **Motor Trend Car of the Year 2013** (first unanimous winner in the award's history) and was named **Car of the Century** by the Automobile Magazine.

### 2013–2016: Profitability, Model X, and Gigafactory Vision

- **2013**: Model S became the world's best-selling plug-in electric car in 2015 and 2016.
- **2013**: Tesla posted its **first quarterly profit** (Q1 2013) — the company was nearly profitable on an annual basis. The stock rose significantly.
- **2014 (October)**: Tesla introduced **Autopilot** — an advanced driver-assistance system (ADAS) using cameras, radar, and ultrasonic sensors. All Tesla vehicles shipped from October 2014 included the necessary hardware for Autopilot, though the software was initially locked.
- **2014**: Began building the **Gigafactory** vision — a massive battery manufacturing plant that would bring battery costs down through scale. Gigafactory 1 (in Nevada) broke ground in 2014.
- **2015 (September)**: Released **Model X** — an SUV with falcon-wing rear doors, featuring a 0-60 mph time of 3.2 seconds (P90D) and a range of ~250 miles. Model X had ~25,000 pre-orders.

### 2017–2019: Model 3, Production Hell, and Global Expansion

- **April 2016**: Unveiled the **Model 3** — a more affordable ($35,000 base price) mass-market electric sedan. Over **325,000 reservations** were placed within a week — an unprecedented response. The Model 3 represented Tesla's attempt to bring EVs to the mainstream.
- **July 2017**: **Model 3 production ramp** — Tesla entered "production hell" as it struggled to ramp up manufacturing. Elon Musk famously slept at the factory floor and used a tent assembly line (the "GA4" tent) to accelerate production. By mid-2018, Tesla was producing 5,000 Model 3s per week — barely making its targets.
- **2017 (November)**: Tesla released the **Semi** truck (Class 8 electric truck) with prototypes shown to PepsiCo and others.
- **2018**: Tesla posted its **first full profitable year** ($2018 net income: $976M after years of losses). The Model 3 had finally achieved production stability.
- **2019 (February)**: Elon Musk publicly considered taking Tesla private at $420/share ("funding secured") — the tweet led to SEC charges, a settlement (Musk had to step down as chairman, Tesla paid $20M in fines). This was one of Musk's most controversial moments.
- **2019 (November)**: Unveiled the **Cybertruck** — a radical, angular, stainless steel pickup truck with a design unlike any conventional vehicle. The reveal had a famous incident: the "armored glass" windows cracked during a demonstration by Franz von Holzhausen (though this didn't prevent 250,000+ pre-orders).
- **2019**: Tesla opened **Gigafactory Shanghai** (December) — the first wholly foreign-owned automobile factory in China. It produced Model 3 and later Model Y for the Chinese market. This was critical for Tesla's growth in the world's largest auto market.

### 2020–2021: S&P 500, $1T Valuation, and Record Deliveries

- **2020 (January)**: Released **Model Y** — a compact crossover SUV sharing ~75% of parts with Model 3. It was produced at Gigafactory Shanghai and later Fremont and Berlin.
- **2020**: Tesla achieved **five consecutive profitable quarters** (Q3 2019 – Q2 2020) — making it eligible for **S&P 500 inclusion**. It was added to the S&P 500 in December 2020 — described as "the most valuable company ever added to the S&P 500."
- **2020**: Tesla's stock price increased **740%** in 2020 — driven by EV enthusiasm, inclusion in S&P 500, and Musk's cult-like following. By December 14, 2020, Tesla's market cap exceeded the **next nine largest automakers combined**.
- **2020**: Released **Model S Plaid** — a tri-motor version with 0-60 mph in 1.99 seconds, 200 mph top speed, and 348-mile range.
- **October 2021**: Tesla became the **sixth US company** to reach **$1 trillion market cap** — driven by record deliveries (936,000 vehicles in 2021, +87% YoY), strong profitability, and Elon Musk's acquisition of Twitter (which some investors saw as either a distraction or a sign of Musk's risk tolerance).
- **2021**: Tesla recorded **$53.8 billion** in revenue, up 73% from 2020.

### 2022–2023: Gigafactory Expansion, Cybertruck, and Record Profits

- **March 2022**: Opened **Gigafactory Berlin** (Germany) — Tesla's first European factory, producing Model Y. The Berlin factory quickly became one of Europe's most efficient car plants.
- **April 2022**: Opened **Gigafactory Texas** (Austin) — the company's headquarters and largest factory. The Texas factory produced Model Y and later Cybertruck.
- **December 2022**: Delivered the first **Tesla Semi** to **PepsiCo** — five years after the truck was unveiled.
- **2022**: Tesla delivered **1.31 million vehicles** — a new record, though it fell short of its goal of 1.5 million due to COVID lockdowns in Shanghai and supply chain disruptions.
- **2022**: Tesla's revenue reached **$81.5 billion** (+51% YoY). Net income: $12.6 billion (+128% YoY).
- **November 30, 2023**: **Cybertruck** deliveries began — the stainless steel, angular pickup truck that had taken 4 years to reach production. The Cybertruck had over **1 million reservations**. Starting price: ~$50,000 for rear-wheel drive, up to $100,000+ for Cyberbeast tri-motor.
- **2023**: Tesla launched **FSD (Full Self-Driving) v12** — a major rewrite that replaced rule-based code with end-to-end neural networks trained on video from millions of Tesla vehicles. This was a significant technical advance in autonomous driving.

### 2024–2025: Robotaxi, AI Chips, and New Markets

- **January 2024**: Tesla launched **FSD v12.1** to wide release — the first major version of FSD based purely on end-to-end AI neural networks.
- **April 2024**: Tesla announced a **10:1 stock split** (effective June 2024) — the second split after a 5:1 split in 2020.
- **2024**: Tesla's stock experienced significant volatility due to concerns about **Elon Musk's political activities** (his role in the Trump administration's Department of Government Efficiency/DOGE) and declining sales in key markets. The stock had a seven-week losing streak through March 2025 — its longest as a public company.
- **October 2024**: Unveiled the **Cybercab** — a dedicated self-driving robotaxi with no steering wheel or pedals, targeting production before 2027 at ~$30,000.
- **January 2026**: Tesla **lost its status as the world's largest BEV manufacturer** to China's BYD — a symbolic shift in the EV market.
- **January 28, 2026**: Tesla announced **discontinuation of Model S and Model X** in the US — focusing production on higher-volume models.
- **March 2026**: Announced the **Terafab Project** — a joint semiconductor project with SpaceX and xAI to build a vertically integrated mega-fab producing over **1 terawatt** of AI compute capacity annually. This was an ambitious expansion into AI chip manufacturing.
- **2025**: Tesla launched a **robotaxi service in Austin, Texas** — operating with human supervisors in the fleet. This was the first commercial deployment of Tesla's FSD technology for ride-hailing.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Electric Vehicles
- **Model S** — luxury sedan (discontinued in US as of 2026). Range: 370-405 miles.
- **Model 3** — mass-market sedan (starting ~$40,000). World's best-selling EV from 2018-2021.
- **Model X** — falcon-wing door SUV (discontinued in US as of 2026).
- **Model Y** — compact crossover SUV (~75% parts common with Model 3). World's best-selling car in Q1 2023.
- **Cybertruck** — stainless steel pickup truck (range: 250-500+ miles depending on config).
- **Semi** — Class 8 electric truck (500-mile range, delivered to PepsiCo).
- **Cybercab** — dedicated robotaxi (no steering wheel, planned production before 2027).

### Energy Generation and Storage
- **Megapack** — large-scale battery storage systems for utilities and grid operators. Each unit stores ~3.9 MWh. Tesla has deployed GWh-scale Megapack projects globally.
- **Powerwall** — residential battery storage paired with solar panels.
- **Solar Roof** — integrated solar panel tiles that replace conventional roof tiles.

### AI and Autonomous Driving
- **FSD (Full Self-Driving)** — Tesla's advanced driver assistance system, currently in Beta. v12 used end-to-end neural networks.
- **Dojo** — Tesla's custom AI training supercomputer for training autonomous driving neural networks.
- **Hardware 4 (HW4)** — Tesla's latest self-driving computer, with improved cameras and processing.

## CEO and Leadership

- **CEO**: **Elon Musk** (became CEO in October 2008; Chairman of the Board since 2004). Musk is also CEO of SpaceX, xAI, and owner of X (formerly Twitter). His dual-CEO roles and multiple ventures have been a subject of shareholder concern.
- **Co-founders**: Martin Eberhard, Marc Tarpenning, Ian Wright, J.B. Straubel (CTO until 2019), and Elon Musk (as largest investor and Chairman).
- **CFO**: **Vaibhav Taneja** (since 2024; previously at Tesla's accounting team).
- **SVP of Powertrain**: **Drew Baglino** (longtime Tesla engineer, left in 2024).

## Financial Highlights

- **2025 Revenue**: ~US$94.83 billion
- **2025 Net Income**: ~US$3.79 billion
- **2024 Production**: ~1.66 million vehicles
- **Employees**: ~140,000 (2025)
- **Market Cap**: ~$900 billion (2025)
- Tesla's automotive gross margin peaked at ~32% in 2022, then declined to ~17% in 2024 due to price cuts to stimulate demand.
- Tesla's energy business (Megapack, Powerwall) is growing rapidly — 2024 energy revenue exceeded $20 billion.

## Manufacturing

Tesla operates the following major manufacturing facilities:
- **Gigafactory Fremont, California** — Model S, Model X, Model 3, Model Y (US production)
- **Gigafactory Texas (Austin)** — Model Y, Cybertruck, Semi, 4680 battery cells
- **Gigafactory Shanghai** — Model 3, Model Y (for China and export markets)
- **Gigafactory Berlin** — Model Y (for Europe)
- **Gigafactory Nevada** — battery cells (Panasonic partnership), Megapack
- **Megafactory Lathrop, California** — Megapack battery storage
- **Planned**: Gigafactory Mexico (announced March 2023)

## Corporate Governance

- **Headquarters**: Austin, Texas, USA (moved from Palo Alto, California in 2021)
- **TSLA** on NASDAQ (Component of S&P 500 and Nasdaq-100)
- Elon Musk holds ~13% of Tesla shares (as of 2025)
- Major institutional investors: Vanguard, BlackRock, State Street
- Tesla's board has been criticized for being too close to Musk; several board members have resigned over the years.
