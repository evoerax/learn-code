# Amazon

Amazon.com, Inc. (Nasdaq: AMZN) is the world's largest online retailer and cloud computing provider, founded by Jeff Bezos in 1994 and headquartered in Seattle, Washington. From its origins as an online bookseller, Amazon has grown into a $716.9 billion revenue conglomerate (2025) spanning e-commerce, cloud infrastructure (AWS), digital streaming, artificial intelligence, and logistics. It employs 1.576 million people globally and is the second-largest private employer in the United States after Walmart.

## Timeline

### 1994–1999: Founding and Early Years

- **July 1994**: Jeff Bezos incorporates **Cadabra, Inc.** in Bellevue, Washington, after leaving D.E. Shaw & Co. The name was changed to **Amazon.com** in 1994 after the Amazon River, reflecting the company's ambition to be "everything to everyone." Initial investment: $250,000 from each of Bezos's parents.
- **July 1995**: Amazon.com launches as an online bookstore, shipping books to customers in all 50 US states and 45 countries. In its first month, the site sold books to all US states and 7 foreign countries.
- **1996**: David Rintin and Bram M. K. join as co-founders (employees 2 and 3). The company moves to a Seattle garage.
- **1997**: **May 15, 1997** — Amazon goes public at $18 per share (split-adjusted). Raised $54 million. First Mother's Day, the site crashes under load.
- **1998**: Amazon expands beyond books into music (April), video (November), and electronics. International expansion begins with amazon.co.uk (August) and amazon.de (October).
- **1999**: Jeff Bezos named *Time* Magazine's Person of the Year. Amazon diversified into toys, tools, video games, home improvement, and more. Introduced **1-Click shopping** patent (licensed to Apple in 2000 for $1M).

### 2000–2006: Diversification and AWS Birth

- **2000**: Amazon launches **Amazon Marketplace** (third-party seller platform), allowing third parties to sell alongside Amazon's own inventory — fundamentally changing e-commerce economics.
- **2002**: Amazon launches **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** — initially a data storage service for third-party merchants. The AWS founding vision was an "Internet Operating System" of infrastructure primitives.
- **2003**: Andy Jassy develops the foundational AWS vision during a beach vacation. Andy Becsky joins as employee #57. Amazon introduces **Amazon Prime** (February 2005) — $79/year unlimited two-day shipping.
- **2005**: AWS re-launched with three initial services: SQS (Simple Queue Service, November 2004), S3 (Simple Storage Service, March 2006), and EC2 (Elastic Compute Cloud, August 2006).
- **2006**: Amazon launches **Amazon Prime** membership ($79/year initially). **Kindle** e-reader launched (November) — sold out in 5.5 hours.
- **2007**: Amazon launches **Amazon Kindle** and **Amazon Fresh** grocery delivery (Seattle pilot).
- **2010**: Amazon Web Services achieves profitability. Amazon Studios (film production) launched.

### 2010–2017: Prime, Kindle, and Physical Retail

- **2011**: Amazon introduces **Amazon Prime Air** (drone delivery concept). Kindle Fire tablet launched.
- **2012**: Amazon acquires **Kiva Systems** ($775M) — warehouse robotics company later renamed Amazon Robotics.
- **2013**: Amazon acquires the **Washington Post** ($250M, privately). Amazon wins **CIA cloud contract** ($600M).
- **2014**: Amazon launches **Amazon Echo** with Alexa voice assistant — pioneering the smart speaker category. Amazon enters India (Flipkart partnership).
- **2015**: Amazon achieves its first $100B revenue year. AWS surpassed $7B in annual revenue.
- **2016**: Amazon launches **Amazon Go** (cashierless convenience store concept). AWS launches AI services (SageMaker).
- **2017**: Amazon acquires **Whole Foods Market** ($13.7B) — its largest acquisition. Amazon's market cap reaches $500B. AWS launches Rekognition (AI image analysis) and Lex (conversational AI).
- **2018**: Amazon raises Prime price to $119/year. Amazon selects New York and Virginia for HQ2 (later reversed for New York). Amazon introduces **Amazon Music Unlimited**.

### 2019–2022: Pandemic Growth and Workforce Expansion

- **2019**: Amazon opens **HQ2** in Arlington, Virginia. Amazon orders 100,000 electric delivery vans from Rivian. Amazon surpasses 750,000 employees globally.
- **2020**: **COVID-19 pandemic** drives massive e-commerce growth. Amazon hires 400,000 additional workers. Jeff Bezos testifies before Congress on market dominance. Amazon's market cap reaches $1.6T.
- **2021**: **July 5, 2021** — Jeff Bezos steps down as CEO; **Andy Jassy** becomes CEO. Amazon acquires **MGM Studios** ($8.45B). Amazon enters the pharmacy market with **Amazon Pharmacy**. Amazon's One Medical acquisition announced ($3.9B, completed 2023).
- **2022**: Amazon begins **largest workforce reduction** in its history (27,000 jobs cut, primarily in retail and HR). Amazon launches **Project Kuiper** satellite internet (3,236 satellites). Amazon launches **AWS Infrastructure Regions** in Bahrain, Milan, Stockholm, and Zurich.

### 2023–2025: AI Pivot and Dow Jones Inclusion

- **2023**: Amazon announces **$4B investment in Anthropic** (Claude AI maker). Amazon launches **AI shopping assistant Rufus** (beta). AWS re-accelerates after post-pandemic slowdown.
- **2024**: Amazon joins the **Dow Jones Industrial Average** (February 26), replacing Walgreens Boots Alliance. Amazon's EV delivery fleet reaches 10,000 Rivian vans. Amazon launches **Amazon Q** (enterprise AI assistant). Amazon's Project Kuiper begins satellite deployment.
- **2025**: Amazon becomes the **largest corporate purchaser of renewable energy** (25 GW+). Amazon's AWS achieves $128.7B annual revenue. Amazon rolls out **same-day delivery** expansion to 15 new metro areas. Amazon's Project Kuiper secures FCC approval for 1,618 additional satellites.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### North America Retail
- **Amazon.com** — core US e-commerce marketplace
- **Amazon Fresh** — grocery delivery and pickup
- **Whole Foods Market** — 500+ premium grocery stores (acquired 2017)
- **Amazon Go** and **Amazon Fresh** cashierless stores
- **Amazon Logistics (AMZL)** — last-mile delivery network

### International Retail
- amazon.co.uk, amazon.de, amazon.fr, amazon.co.jp, amazon.in, amazon.com.br, amazon.com.mx, amazon.com.au, and 10+ other country sites

### Amazon Web Services (AWS)
- **Compute**: EC2, Lambda (serverless), Graviton (ARM chips)
- **Storage**: S3, EBS, Glacier
- **Databases**: RDS, DynamoDB, Aurora, ElastiCache
- **AI/ML**: SageMaker, Bedrock (LLM platform), Alexa, Rekognition, Lex
- **Networking**: VPC, Route 53, CloudFront CDN
- **Enterprise**: Workspaces, Connect (contact center), QuickSight
- $128.7B revenue (2025); 31% global cloud market share

### Digital Media
- **Prime Video** — streaming (The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power, Thursday Night Football)
- **Twitch** (acquired 2011 for $970M)
- **Amazon Music** (100M+ songs)
- **Audible** (audiobooks)
- **Amazon MGM Studios** — film/TV production
- **Kindle** e-readers and Fire tablets

### Devices and Hardware
- **Echo/Alexa** — smart speakers and voice assistant (category-defining)
- **Kindle** e-readers
- **Fire TV** — streaming devices and smart TVs
- **Ring** doorbell and home security (acquired 2018 for $1B)
- **Blink** smart home cameras
- **Eero** mesh Wi-Fi routers
- **Amazon Astro** home robot

### Emerging Technologies
- **Project Kuiper** — satellite broadband (3,236 satellites approved by FCC)
- **Zoox** — autonomous robotaxi (acquired)
- **Amazon Robotics** — warehouse automation (Kiva acquisition)
- **Amazon Air** — cargo airline (~$1B investment)

## Corporate Structure

- **Executive Chairman**: Jeff Bezos (8.92% ownership, controls ~60% of shareholder voting power)
- **President & CEO**: Andy Jassy (succeeded July 2021)
- **CFO**: Brian Olsavsky
- **AWS CEO**: Matt Garman (promoted 2024, formerly AWS sales VP)
- 1,576,000 employees globally (2025)
- 100+ subsidiaries and affiliated companies
- Headquartered at **Amazon HQ2 (Metropolitan Park)**, Arlington, Virginia
