# Alphabet (Google)

Alphabet Inc. (Nasdaq: GOOGL, GOOG) is the parent holding company of Google and its subsidiaries, created in 2015 to serve as "a collection of companies" — each with its own identity and leadership. Google itself was founded in January 1996 as a research project by **Larry Page** and **Sergey Brin** while they were PhD students at Stanford University. The company was officially incorporated as Google Inc. on September 4, 1998. From a search engine startup operating out of a Stanford dorm, Alphabet has grown into the world's dominant internet platform, with 2025 revenue of approximately $403 billion, $132 billion in net income, and a market capitalization exceeding $3 trillion as of late 2025. Its core business is online advertising (Google Search, YouTube Ads, Display), but it also operates Android, Chrome, Gmail, Google Cloud, Waymo (autonomous driving), and a portfolio of moonshot bets through X (the moonshot factory).

## Timeline

### 1996–2003: Stanford Origins and Early Search Technology

- **1996 (September)**: Larry Page and Sergey Brin began the **Backrub** research project at Stanford — a new type of search engine that ranked pages by counting "votes" from other pages (an early version of what became **PageRank**). Unlike keyword-based search engines of the era, PageRank evaluated the entire link structure of the web to determine relevance and importance.
- **1997**: Page and Brin registered the domain **google.com** and used a 10-petabyte data center built from廉价 disk arrays at Stanford. The name "Google" derives from the mathematical term **googol** (10^100) — reflecting the founders' ambition to organize "a moby amount of information."
- **1998**: Andy Bechtolsheim (co-founder of Sun Microsystems) wrote the first check — a **$100,000** angel investment — after a demo of Google Search. This was the critical seed funding that allowed Google to incorporate.
- **1998**: Excite declined to buy Google for $1 million. (This would become one of the most expensive "no" decisions in business history.)
- **September 4, 1998**: Google Inc. was officially incorporated. First office: a garage in Menlo Park, California, rented from a friend of Larry Page. Early employees: 8 people.
- **1999**: Sequoia Capital invested **$12.5 million** at a valuation of $100 million. Kleiner Perkins added $8 million. John Doerr (Kleiner Perkins) joined the board.
- **June 2000**: Google became the world's largest search engine, indexing over **1 billion web pages**.

### 2001–2004: Early Growth and the IPO

- **2001**: **Larry Page stepped down as CEO**, replaced by **Eric Schmidt** (former Novell CEO) — who served as chairman and "adult supervision." Page remained as president of products; Brin as president of technology.
- **August 19, 2004**: Google went **public on NASDAQ** at $85 per share, raising $1.67 billion. The IPO was conducted via a **Dutch auction** — an unconventional method meant to give small investors equal access. Share price closed at $100 on day one.
- **2005**: Acquired **Google Earth** (from Keyhole, Inc.) — later became a widely-used mapping platform.

### 2006–2011: YouTube, Android, and Platform Expansion

- **October 2006**: Acquired **YouTube** for **$1.65 billion** in stock — a move considered expensive at the time but proved transformative. YouTube had only been founded 20 months earlier.
- **2007**: Acquired **DoubleClick** for **$3.1 billion** — giving Google dominance in online display advertising.
- **November 2007**: Launched **Android** mobile operating system — a free, open-source OS built by Android Inc. (acquired by Google in 2005). Android was positioned as an alternative to Apple's iOS.
- **September 2008**: Released **Google Chrome** web browser — within 3 years it became the world's most-used browser.
- **July 2008**: Android became the world's best-selling smartphone OS by unit volume, overtaking Symbian.
- **2011**: Launched **Google+** — Google's fourth attempt at social networking. It peaked at ~400 million monthly active users but never seriously challenged Facebook. Google+ was shut down in April 2019 after a data exposure incident.
- **2011**: Larry Page became CEO again (replacing Eric Schmidt). Page began restructuring Google into a more streamlined organization.

### 2012–2014: Hardware and Ecosystem Expansion

- **2012**: Released **Google Glass** (Explorer Edition) — smart glasses for consumers, which flopped commercially and was later repositioned as an enterprise tool.
- **2013**: Acquired **Waze** for **$1.1 billion** — real-time crowd-sourced navigation app, integrated into Google Maps.
- **January 2014**: Acquired **Nest Labs** for **$3.2 billion** — smart home devices (thermostats, cameras, doorbells).
- **2014**: Launched **Google Cardboard** — a simple VR headset that democratized virtual reality experiences.

### 2015: Alphabet Restructuring

- **August 2015**: Larry Page announced the creation of **Alphabet Inc.** via a blog post. The restructuring was effective **October 2, 2015**.
- **Rationale**: To make Google "cleaner and more accountable," allowing Larry Page to oversee all subsidiaries as Alphabet CEO, while **Sundar Pichai** became CEO of Google. Moonshot bets (Waymo, Verily, Calico, X, etc.) would operate as independent companies under Alphabet.
- **Structure**: Alphabet has two share classes — **Class C (GOOG)** carries no voting rights; **Class A (GOOGL)** carries one vote per share. Larry Page and Sergey Brin retain controlling interest through Class B shares (10:1 voting rights).
- **Domain**: Google chose **abc.xyz** because **alphabet.com** belonged to BMW.

### 2016–2019: AI First and Leadership Transition

- **2016**: Google rebranded its AI efforts under **Google AI** and introduced **TensorFlow** — its open-source machine learning framework, which became the most widely-used ML framework in the world.
- **December 2016**: Alphabet launched **Waymo** as a standalone subsidiary — spun out from the Google Self-Driving Car Project (started 2009). Waymo began real-world robotaxi service in Phoenix, Arizona in 2018.
- **2017**: Acquired **HTC**'s smartphone division (~2,000 engineers) for **$1.1 billion** — signaling Google's intent to build Pixel hardware in-house.
- **2018**: Google launched **Google Duplex** — AI technology that could make restaurant reservations and appointments by speaking to humans on the phone, demonstrating breakthrough natural language processing.
- **December 2019**: Larry Page and Sergey Brin **stepped down** from their executive roles. **Sundar Pichai** became CEO of both Google and Alphabet. Page and Brin remained as employees and board members.

### 2020–2022: Antitrust, Pandemic, and Cloud Growth

- **October 2020**: The **US Department of Justice** filed a landmark **antitrust lawsuit against Google** — alleging that Google unlawfully maintained monopolies in search and search advertising through exclusive deals (e.g., paying Apple $15–20 billion annually to be the default search engine on Safari/iOS). This was the most significant US antitrust case since Microsoft in 1998.
- **December 2020**: Alphabet became the **fourth US company** (after Apple, Microsoft, Amazon) to reach **$1 trillion market cap**.
- **2021**: Alphabet's 2021 revenue hit **$257.6 billion**, up from $182.5 billion in 2020 — Google's advertising business surged during the COVID-19 pandemic as commerce moved online.
- **2021**: Launched **TensorFlow Enterprise** — enterprise-grade version of TensorFlow.
- **2022**: The EU's **Digital Markets Act (DMA)** designated Google as a "gatekeeper" — requiring compliance with interoperability, data sharing, and fairness rules.

### 2023–2024: AI Race, First Dividend, and Antitrust Ruling

- **February 2023**: Launched **Bard** — Google's conversational AI competitor to OpenAI's ChatGPT — initially powered by the LaMDA language model.
- **December 2023**: Rebranded Bard as **Google Gemini** — a unified AI assistant spanning consumer and enterprise products. Gemini replaced Bard across Google Search, Gmail, Docs, and Android.
- **2023**: Alphabet's **Google Cloud** segment reached profitability — generating $8.1 billion in revenue in Q4 2023 alone. Google Cloud became a **$33 billion annual revenue** business by end of 2023.
- **January 2024**: Alphabet's board announced Alphabet's **first ever cash dividend** ($0.20 per share) and a **$70 billion stock buyback** program.
- **April 2024**: Google launched **AI Overviews** in Google Search — AI-generated summaries at the top of search results. The feature had a troubled rollout with inaccurate answers, leading to a temporary rollback before wider release.
- **April 2024**: A federal judge ruled that Google's **search distribution deals with Apple and Android device makers violated US antitrust law** — Google's first major antitrust loss in 24 years.
- **May 2024**: Google became the **fourth company** to exceed **$2 trillion market cap**.
- **December 2024**: Google unveiled the **Willow** quantum computing chip — a 105-qubit chip that solves a benchmark computation in 5 minutes that would take classical supercomputers 10 septillion years.

### 2025: AGI Push and Sovereign AI

- **2025**: Alphabet announced major investments in **sovereign AI** — AI infrastructure operated independently by national governments — in France, Japan, India, Germany, Saudi Arabia, and other markets.
- **2025**: Google DeepMind made significant progress on the **Gemini 2.0** model family, further integrating AI across Google Search, Gmail, Docs, and Android.
- **September 2025**: Alphabet became the **fourth company** to exceed **$3 trillion market cap** (after Apple, Microsoft, and Nvidia).

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Google Search and Advertising
- **Google Search** — world's most visited website (~92% global search market share). Foundation of Google's ~$400 billion advertising empire.
- **Google Ads** (formerly AdWords) — auction-based pay-per-click advertising platform.
- **YouTube Ads** — pre-roll and display advertising on YouTube.
- **Google Display Network** — reach across millions of websites and apps.
- **AdMob** — mobile advertising for app developers.

### Google Cloud
- **Google Cloud Platform (GCP)** — IaaS and PaaS competing with AWS and Azure.
- **Vertex AI** — managed ML platform for enterprise AI.
- **BigQuery** — serverless data warehouse.
- **Looker** — business intelligence/analytics.
- Google Cloud reached **$33 billion annual revenue** by end of 2023, growing ~28% year-over-year.

### Hardware
- **Pixel** smartphones — Google's own-brand Android phone (significant expansion after 2021 Pixel 6).
- **Pixel Watch, Pixel Buds, Pixel Tablet** — wearable and accessories.
- **Nest** smart home devices (smart speakers, displays, thermostats, cameras, doorbells).
- **Chromebook** laptops (manufactured by partners; Chrome OS by Google).
- **Fitbit** (acquired 2021).

### YouTube
- **YouTube** — world's second-largest search engine and video platform (~2.5 billion monthly active users).
- **YouTube Premium** and **YouTube TV**.
- **YouTube Shorts** — TikTok competitor (launched 2021).

### Moonshots / Other Bets
- **Waymo** — autonomous driving (robotaxi in Phoenix, San Francisco, Los Angeles; also trucking).
- **Verily** — life sciences and healthcare.
- **Calico** — longevity and anti-aging research.
- **Wing** — drone delivery.
- **X (the moonshot factory)** — autonomous robotics, agricultural AI (Mineral).
- **Isomorphic Labs** — AI-driven drug discovery.
- **Intrinsic** — industrial robotics software.

## CEO and Leadership

- **CEO (Alphabet & Google)**: **Sundar Pichai** — CEO of Google (2015), CEO of Alphabet (2019). Joined Google in 2004; previously led Chrome and Android.
- **Co-founders**: **Larry Page** and **Sergey Brin** — remain as controlling shareholders through Class B shares. Both stepped down from executive roles in December 2019.
- **Chairman**: **John L. Hennessy** (since February 2018) — former President of Stanford University.
- **CFO**: **Anat Ashkenazi** (joined 2024, previously at Eli Lilly).

## Financial Highlights

- **2025 Revenue**: ~$403 billion
- **2025 Net Income**: ~$132 billion
- **2025 Operating Income**: ~$130 billion
- **2025 Total Assets**: ~$595 billion
- **Employees**: ~191,000 (2025)
- Google's advertising business (Search + YouTube) still accounts for ~80% of total revenue.
- Google Cloud is now ~16% of revenue and growing rapidly.
- **2025 Market Cap**: ~$3 trillion (September 2025)

## Manufacturing

Google is primarily a **software and services company**. Pixel phones are manufactured by Foxconn and other ODMs. Google's AI workloads run on custom **Google Tensor Processing Units (TPUs)** — its own AI accelerators designed in-house and manufactured by TSMC.

## Corporate Governance

- **Headquarters**: Mountain View, California, USA
- **GOOGL** (Class A, voting) and **GOOG** (Class C, non-voting) on NASDAQ
- Component of S&P 500 and Nasdaq-100
- Larry Page and Sergey Brin hold ~13% of shares but ~56% of voting power through Class B stock
- Major institutional investors: Vanguard, BlackRock, State Street
