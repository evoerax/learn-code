# Netflix

Netflix, Inc. (Nasdaq: NFLX) is an American multinational entertainment and media production company headquartered in Los Gatos, California. Founded in 1997 as a DVD-by-mail rental service, Netflix transformed into the world's dominant subscription-based streaming platform, producing and distributing films, television series, documentaries, anime, and interactive content in over 190 countries. With **325 million paid memberships** globally (January 2026) and operations in every major market worldwide, Netflix is the largest single contributor to global internet bandwidth consumption and the defining company of the streaming era. The company's original content strategy — pioneered with **House of Cards** in 2013 — created a new model for prestige television that upended traditional Hollywood production and distribution economics. Netflix operates a pure subscription business model (no advertising on the main platform until 2022) and produces approximately **500+ original titles per year**, spending over $17 billion annually on content. In 2025, Netflix reported **$38.8 billion in annual revenue**, cementing its position as the world's largest subscription entertainment service by both revenue and subscribers.

## Timeline

### 1997–2002: Founding and DVD-by-Mail Origins

- **1997**: Netflix founded on **August 29, 1997** in **Scotts Valley, California**, by **Marc Randolph** (entrepreneur, former vice president at Pure Software) and **Reed Hastings** (computer scientist, co-founded Pure Software, which was acquired for $750 million in 1997). Hastings invested **$2.5 million** of his own money and became the dominant founder/CEO. Initial idea: DVD rental by mail, competing with Blockbuster. The company launched with 30 employees and **925 titles** — primarily DVDs because VHS tapes were too fragile for mailing.
- **1998**: Netflix launched its **first website** — the first e-commerce site to rent DVDs online. Early growth was modest; the core insight was that the US Postal Service could efficiently deliver DVDs nationwide, avoiding the need for physical retail locations.
- **1999**: September 1999: Netflix introduced the **monthly subscription model** — charging a flat monthly fee for unlimited DVD rentals (no per-rental fees). This was a revolutionary idea: Hastings famously said he got the inspiration from gym memberships where people pay regardless of usage. The per-rental model was dropped entirely by early 2000.
- **2000**: Netflix posted its first profitable year in 2000 ($5 million profit) — remarkably early for a dot-com era company. However, the dot-com crash severely impacted Netflix's stock price and growth prospects. Blockbuster attempted to acquire Netflix for **$50 million** in 2000 — an offer Marc Randolph and Reed Hastings accepted — but Blockbuster's board pulled out at the last moment. This would become one of the most expensive missed opportunities in entertainment history.
- **2001**: Netflix reached **100,000 subscribers**. After the dot-com crash, Netflix laid off one-third of its staff. The company refocused on its subscription model and DVD-by-mail operations.
- **2002**: **May 23, 2002**: Netflix went public on **Nasdaq** at **$15.00 per share** — raising $83 million. The IPO came during a difficult period for tech stocks but established Netflix's public market presence. Hastings retained approximately 30% ownership post-IPO.

### 2003–2007: Growth and Streaming Dawn

- **2003**: Netflix achieved **$6.5 million profit** on **$272 million revenue** — its first truly profitable year, validating the subscription model. Subscriber growth accelerated: 1 million subscribers by end of 2003.
- **2004**: Netflix profit grew to **$49 million** on **$500+ million revenue**. The company began experimenting with **recommendation algorithms** — a technology that would later become central to Netflix's competitive advantage.
- **2005**: Netflix's DVD-by-mail library had grown to 75,000+ titles. The company recognized that internet streaming would eventually replace physical media — an insight that drove early R&D investments in streaming technology.
- **2006**: Netflix launched the **Netflix Prize** — a $1 million competition to improve its recommendation algorithm by 10%. The competition attracted over 40,000 teams worldwide and resulted in a winning algorithm that improved recommendations by 8.9% (awarded in 2009). This established Netflix's reputation as a data-driven company.
- **2007**: January 16, 2007: Netflix launched its **streaming service** — branded as **"Watch Now"** — initially with only **1,000 films** available. All DVD-by-mail subscribers received unlimited streaming at **no additional cost**. This was the first major step in Netflix's transformation from DVD-by-mail to streaming company. By end of 2007, Netflix had 7.5 million subscribers.

### 2008–2012: Streaming First and International Expansion

- **2008**: January 2008: Netflix made streaming available to all DVD subscribers at no extra cost. **All content** on DVDs was progressively added to streaming. Netflix signed streaming deals with major studios including **Starz** (Disney/Paramount content) — a critical deal that expanded streaming's content depth. By end of 2008, Netflix had 12 million subscribers.
- **2009**: Netflix had **15 million subscribers** and was firmly established as the leader in DVD rental. The recommendation algorithm improvements from the Netflix Prize were being deployed across the platform.
- **2010**: September 2010: Netflix launched in **Canada** — its **first international market**. A $1 billion streaming deal with **Paramount, Lionsgate, and MGM** was signed, cementing Netflix's content relationships. By end of 2010, streaming accounted for **42% of Netflix usage** — a clear signal that streaming was the future.
- **2011**: Netflix launched in **Latin America** (43 countries) — the first major multi-country launch. The company began separating DVD and streaming pricing (a controversial move that caused customer backlash and a 60% stock price drop in July 2011).
- **2012**: Netflix launched in the **UK, Ireland, Denmark, Finland, Norway, and Sweden**. Netflix signed a **multi-year agreement with Disney** (2012) — Disney content became available on Netflix. By 2012, Netflix had **25 million US streaming subscribers** and 8 million international subscribers. The company joined the **Motion Picture Association of America (MPAA)** as its first streaming-only member.

### 2013–2016: Original Content Revolution

- **2013**: February 2013: Netflix released **"House of Cards"** — its first **original television series** and the first major streaming-only original drama. The entire 13-episode first season was released simultaneously (binge-release model), fundamentally changing how television content was consumed. House of Cards was critically acclaimed and won multiple Emmy nominations. This proved streaming services could compete with HBO in prestige content. In 2013, Netflix released several original series: **"Orange Is the New Black"** (prison drama, became massive hit), **"Hemlock Grove"**, **"Lilyhammer"**. Netflix also signed deals with **Marvel Television** (2013) for four original series (Daredevil, Jessica Jones, Iron Fist, Luke Cage). Netflix content spend reached **$2 billion** in 2013.
- **2014**: Netflix launched in **Austria, Belgium, France, Germany, Luxembourg, Switzerland, Italy, Portugal, Spain** — entering 6 new European markets simultaneously. Released 31 original series. Netflix acquired **ABQ Studios** in Albuquerque (New Mexico) — establishing its first owned production facility. By end 2014, Netflix had **57 million subscribers** worldwide (43M in US, 14M internationally).
- **2015**: Netflix launched in **Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Italy, Portugal, and Spain** — marking entry into Asia-Pacific. The Marvel series **"Daredevil"** premiered — the first of four Marvel/Netflix originals. Netflix crossed **75 million subscribers** globally. Netflix became the first streaming service to **surpass traditional TV** in number of US subscribers.
- **2016**: January 2016: Netflix announced expansion to **130 additional countries** — the most aggressive global expansion in its history. Netflix launched its service in **most of Africa, the Middle East, Eastern Europe, and Southeast Asia** in a single day. The Netflix app was available on virtually every internet-connected device. **"Stranger Things"** premiered in July 2016 — becoming Netflix's most-watched original series ever at the time and a global cultural phenomenon. Netflix reached **93.8 million subscribers** (49M US, 45M international). Netflix launched its **Netflix股价 service** — testing an ad-supported tier in Australia.

### 2017–2019: Global Scale and Content Spending Surge

- **2017**: Netflix announced **$8 billion** in original content investment for 2018 — signaling an unprecedented commitment to original programming. The company acquired **Millarworld** (Mark Millar's comic book company). Released **"The Crown"**, **"Mindhunter"**, **"13 Reasons Why"** — all significant original series. Netflix announced a **four-film deal with Adam Sandler** worth up to $275 million — a controversial deal that proved Netflix could attract major Hollywood talent. International expansion continued: launched in **India, Russia, South Korea, and Turkey**. By end 2017, Netflix had **117.6 million subscribers** (53M US, 65M international).
- **2018**: Netflix acquired **Albuquerque Studios** for under $30 million. Released **"Bird Box"** — the film became a cultural phenomenon with 45 million streams in first week. Announced **$8 billion content spend** for 2018 with **700+ original projects**. Netflix deal with **Paramount for "The Cloverfield Paradox"** ($50 million) — a major studio releasing a film directly on Netflix instead of theatrical. Netflix signed an **exclusive deal with Game of Thrones creators David Benioff and D.B. Weiss**. By end 2018, Netflix had **139 million subscribers** (58M US, 81M international).
- **2019**: Netflix launched in **additional European and Asian markets** (reaching 190+ countries total). **"When They See Us"**, **"Our Planet"**, **"The Witcher"** — all significant originals. Netflix joined the **MPAA** as its first streaming-only member. Netflix signed a **multi-million dollar deal with Prince Harry and Meghan Markle** for documentary content. Disney pulled its content from Netflix in 2019 as Disney+ launched. Netflix's content spend reached **$15 billion** in 2019. Stock peaked at **$385 per share** in June 2019.

### 2020–2022: COVID Era and Ad-Supported Tier

- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic drove massive subscriber growth as people stayed home. Netflix added **37 million subscribers** in 2020 — the largest annual gain in company history. Netflix reached **200 million subscribers** globally. In July 2020, Netflix appointed **Ted Sarandos** as **co-CEO** alongside Reed Hastings. Netflix launched **"Extraction"** (April 2020) — the most-watched original film at the time. Netflix launched its first **mobile games** (November 2021) — entering gaming. Netflix also acquired **Grauman's Egyptian Theatre** in Hollywood.
- **2021**: Netflix continued massive content investment (**$17 billion**). Released **"Squid Game"** (September 2021) — the Korean-language series became Netflix's most-watched show ever with 1.65 billion viewing hours in first month. Squid Game demonstrated Netflix's ability to produce globally resonant content from any market. Netflix acquired **rights to "Stranger Things 3: The Game"**. Netflix's subscriber growth began slowing in late 2021 due to post-pandemic reopening and market saturation. Stock fell from peak of $385 to ~$285 by end 2021.
- **2022**: Netflix's subscriber growth stalled — first quarterly loss in a decade in Q1 2022 (200K subscribers lost). Netflix announced it would launch an **ad-supported tier** in November 2022 (Netflix Basic with Ads at $6.99/month). Reed Hastings publicly admitted that the company's "password sharing" had contributed to subscriber stagnation. Netflix began **cracking down on password sharing** in 2023. In 2022, Netflix released **"Wednesday"** (addams family spin-off, 1.4B hours in first month), **"Stranger Things 4"**, **"Ozark"** finale, and **"Bridgerton"** season 1. Netflix entered live events: acquired rights to **WWE (WrestleMania)** for streaming — its first major live sports/entertainment event. Netflix acquired **Housemarque** (video game studio, acquired by Sony). Stock fell to **~$160** by end 2022 (down 55% from peak).

### 2023–2025: Recovery, Live Events, and Gaming

- **2023**: Netflix officially **ended its DVD-by-mail service** in September 2023 — closing the chapter on the company's original business that had been operational for **25 years**. Netflix began enforcing **password sharing restrictions** globally — requiring users to pay extra or establish their own accounts. This drove a significant subscriber bump (9.9M new subscribers in Q3 2023). Netflix launched its **first live event** — a Chris Rock comedy special (May 2023). Expanded into live events with **WWE programming** (WrestleMania, Raw). Netflix released **"The Night Agent"**, **"Wednesday"** season 1 (continued), **"Baby Monster"**, and anime including **"One Piece"**. Netflix expanded its **video game ambitions** — launched 55 games with 85 more in development. By end 2023, Netflix had **260 million subscribers** (83M in US/Canada, 177M internationally). Netflix's ad-supported tier (Basic with Ads) grew to **40 million monthly active users** globally.
- **2024**: Netflix strengthened its **live events** strategy: acquired rights to **NFL Christmas Day Games** (two games in 2024, expanding to more games in 2025) — a landmark sports deal bringing professional football to Netflix. Released **"Wednesday"** season 2, **"Stranger Things"** season 5 (finale), **"Squid Game"** season 2, **"The Night Agent"** season 2. Netflix's ad-supported tier reached **40 million monthly active users**. Netflix expanded **games** to include more premium titles. Netflix increased its **live sports** portfolio: announced **boxing matches** and additional WWE content. By end 2024, Netflix had **302 million subscribers** and revenue of approximately **$38.5 billion**.
- **2025**: January 2026: Netflix reported **325 million paid memberships** globally. Revenue for 2025: **$38.8 billion**. Netflix continued its **live sports expansion**: NFL Christmas games, boxing events, and WWE remained key differentiators. Netflix released **"Squid Game"** season 2 (December 2024) and **"Wednesday"** season 2 (2025). Netflix's **ad-supported tier** grew to 40 million+ monthly active users, becoming an increasingly important revenue driver. Netflix launched **"Netflix Studios"** branded productions in theatrical release — a partial return to theatrical distribution to support awards ambitions. Netflix expanded its **gaming** portfolio with premium cloud gaming tests.

## Key Products

### Streaming Platform
- **Netflix Standard** — 1080p streaming on two devices simultaneously. Core streaming tier.
- **Netflix Premium** — 4K UHD + HDR streaming on four devices, Dolby Atmos audio. ~$22.99/month.
- **Netflix Basic with Ads** — ad-supported streaming (6.5 minutes of ads per hour). $6.99/month. 40M+ MAUs (2024).

### Original Content (Flagship Series/Films)
- **"House of Cards"** (2013-2019) — Netflix's first original, political drama, 6 seasons
- **"Orange Is the New Black"** (2013-2019) — prison drama, 7 seasons, cultural phenomenon
- **"Stranger Things"** (2016-2025) — sci-fi horror, 5 seasons, global phenomenon
- **"Squid Game"** (2021-present) — Korean-language survival drama, most-watched Netflix original ever
- **"Wednesday"** (2022-present) — Addams Family spin-off, 1.4B+ hours in first month
- **"The Crown"** (2016-2023) — historical drama, 6 seasons, British royal family
- **"Bridgerton"** (2020-present) — period drama, multiple seasons
- **"Extraction"** (2020) — action film, most-watched Netflix original film at release

### Live Events
- **NFL Christmas Day Games** — streaming rights to NFL games (2024, 2025+)
- **WWE Programming** — Raw, SmackDown, and WrestleMania streaming rights
- **Chris Rock Live Comedy Special** — 2023, first major live comedy event
- **Boxing Events** — Jake Paul vs. Mike Tyson (November 2024)
