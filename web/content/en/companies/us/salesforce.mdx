# Salesforce

**Salesforce, Inc.** (NYSE: CRM) is the world's leading customer relationship management (CRM) software company and a pioneer of the Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) model. Founded in 1999 by Marc Benioff, Parker Harris, Dave Moellenhoff, and Frank Dominguez — four software engineers who left Oracle to build a new kind of enterprise software — Salesforce was among the first companies to deliver business software over the internet via subscription, eliminating the need for customers to install, maintain, and upgrade software on their own servers. Headquartered in San Francisco, Salesforce operates the **Trust Layer** as a core architectural principle — ensuring enterprise-grade security, reliability, and compliance across its cloud platform. The company serves over 150,000 customers in diverse industries and has grown from a pure CRM vendor into a broad enterprise computing platform spanning sales, service, marketing, commerce, analytics, AI, and integration. FY2025 revenue was **$37.9 billion** with **$6.2 billion** net income, making it the largest enterprise software company by revenue.

## Company History & Timeline

### Founding and SaaS Pioneering (1999–2003)

Marc Benioff had been at Oracle for 13 years, rising to become the company's youngest Senior Vice President, when he decided in **1998** that enterprise software was broken — customers were frustrated with expensive, complex, on-premise installations that took months to deploy and required armies of consultants. He discussed the idea of "enterprise software as a service" with his mentor, Oracle co-founder Larry Ellison, who provided crucial early guidance and invested in Salesforce's seed round.

Benioff recruited **Parker Harris**, a fellow Oracle alumnus, along with **Dave Moellenhoff** and **Frank Dominguez** to build the initial software. The four founders wrote the first version of the Salesforce automation platform in an apartment in San Francisco in **1999**, working nights and weekends while Benioff continued at Oracle. The company was initially called "Salesforce.com" (not adopting the simple "Salesforce" name until 2022), reflecting the dot-com era's preference for ".com" branding.

In **1999**, the company raised $2 million in seed funding from **Larry Ellison** (through his investment firm), with Oracle taking a minority stake. The founding team adopted the motto **"The End of Software"** — a provocative challenge to the entire enterprise software industry — and ran a now-famous marketing campaign in 2000–2001 lampooning traditional software companies with the tagline "Software is over." This aggressive positioning was controversial but effective in establishing Salesforce as the anti-traditional vendor.

The platform launched in **2000** with an initial customer list of about 20 companies. The "no software" concept was genuinely disruptive: customers accessed Salesforce via a simple web browser, paid a subscription per user per month, and never had to worry about infrastructure or upgrades.

### The Dreamforce Conference and Growth Era (2003–2009)

The first **Dreamforce** conference was held in **2003** at the Hyatt Regency San Francisco with approximately 5,000 attendees, establishing Salesforce's signature user conference tradition that would grow into one of the largest software conferences in the world (200,000+ attendees pre-pandemic).

In **2004**, Salesforce completed its **IPO on the NYSE** (ticker: CRM), raising $110 million at a valuation of $1.1 billion. The stock closed at $3.90 on day one, giving the company a market cap around $1.2 billion.

By **2009**, Salesforce crossed **$1 billion in annual revenue** — becoming one of the fastest enterprise software companies to reach this milestone — and launched **Service Cloud**, expanding from pure sales force automation into customer service and support. Service Cloud introduced the concept of the "social customer service" with integration to Facebook and Twitter.

### Platform Strategy and AppExchange (2005–2012)

In **2005**, Salesforce launched **AppExchange** — an online app store where third-party developers could build applications that ran on the Salesforce platform. AppExchange established Salesforce as a platform company, not just a CRM vendor, and created an ecosystem of specialized applications that extended Salesforce's functionality. By 2012, AppExchange had over 3,000 applications.

In **2008**, Salesforce launched **Force.com** — a platform-as-a-service (PaaS) offering that allowed developers to build entire applications on Salesforce's infrastructure.

### Acquisitions and Expansion Beyond CRM (2010–2018)

- **2011**: Acquired **Heroku** ($212 million), a cloud platform for developer-focused web applications.
- **2013**: Acquired **ExactTarget** ($2.5 billion) — a digital marketing platform that became the foundation of **Marketing Cloud**.
- **2016**: Acquired **Demandware** ($2.8 billion) for **Commerce Cloud**, entering the e-commerce platform market.
- **2018**: Acquired **MuleSoft** ($6.5 billion) — a hybrid integration platform connecting cloud and on-premise applications.
- **2019**: Acquired **Tableau Software** ($15.7 billion) — a data visualization and analytics leader.

### Slack Acquisition and Platform Consolidation (2019–2023)

The **$27.7 billion Slack acquisition** announced in **December 2020** and completed in **July 2021** was Salesforce's largest and most transformative acquisition. Slack Technologies had grown to become the dominant workplace communication platform, competing with Microsoft Teams.

In **2022**, Salesforce rebranded from "salesforce.com" to **"Salesforce, Inc."** in April 2022. In **January 2023**, Salesforce announced it would cut approximately **8,000 employees (~10% of its workforce)** — its first major layoff — citing "hiring over-hiring" during the pandemic boom. **Elliott Investment Management** also disclosed a multi-billion dollar stake and pushed for improved profitability.

In **March 2024**, Salesforce announced its first quarterly dividend of $0.40 per share. In **December 2024**, Salesforce announced it would acquire **Informatica** for approximately **$8 billion** — a major data management and ETL platform.

### AI and Agentforce Strategy (2023–2025)

Salesforce launched **Einstein GPT** in **March 2023** — claimed to be the world's first generative AI for CRM. In **September 2024**, Salesforce launched **Agentforce** — a platform for building autonomous AI agents that could take actions within Salesforce workflows without human intervention.

## Products and Business Segments

### Sales Cloud

Sales Cloud is Salesforce's original and flagship product — a comprehensive sales force automation platform covering:

- **Lead and Opportunity Management**: Tracking prospects through the sales pipeline
- **Account Management**: Centralized customer account records
- **Einstein Activity Capture**: AI-powered capture of emails and meetings
- **CPQ (Configure-Price-Quote)**: Automated pricing and proposal generation
- **Revenue Intelligence**: AI-driven forecasting and pipeline analytics

### Service Cloud

Service Cloud provides customer service and support tools:

- **Case Management**: Tracking and resolving customer issues
- **Field Service**: Dispatching technicians and managing service appointments
- **Messaging and Live Chat**: Real-time customer communication
- **Omnichannel Routing**: Intelligent routing of service requests
- **Einstein Service Agent**: AI-powered automated service responses

### Marketing Cloud

Marketing Cloud (built on ExactTarget) provides digital marketing tools:

- **Email Studio**: Large-scale email campaign management
- **Social Studio**: Social media publishing and monitoring
- **Advertising Studio**: Cross-channel advertising management
- **Marketing Cloud Personalization**: Real-time website and app personalization

### Commerce Cloud

Commerce Cloud (built on Demandware) is an enterprise e-commerce platform:

- **B2C and B2C E-commerce**: Online storefronts for retailers and wholesalers
- **Order Management**: Unified order processing across channels
- **Global Expansion Tools**: Multi-currency, multi-language support

### Platform and MuleSoft

- **Force.com / Platform**: Low-code application development platform
- **MuleSoft Anypoint Platform**: API-led integration connecting cloud and on-premise systems
- **Heroku**: Developer-focused polyglot cloud application platform
- **AppExchange**: The Salesforce marketplace with 5,000+ apps

### Data and AI

- **Data Cloud**: Customer Data Platform unifying all data sources
- **Tableau**: Self-service analytics and business intelligence
- **Einstein GPT / Agentforce**: Generative AI and autonomous agent capabilities

### Slack

Slack ($27.7B acquisition, 2021) serves as Salesforce's "digital HQ":

- **Channels**: Topic-based persistent chat rooms
- **Slack GPT**: AI-powered search and summarization
- **Slack Connect**: External collaboration with partner organizations
- **Workflow Builder**: No-code automation of routine tasks

## Corporate Structure and Leadership

**Marc Benioff** has been Chairman and CEO since founding in 1999 — one of the longest-tenured founders still leading a major tech company. Benioff is known for his advocacy of **Stakeholder Capitalism** and the "Ohana" cultural framework.

In **2020**, Salesforce appointed **Bret Taylor** (formerly Google's product leader) as **Co-CEO** alongside Benioff. Taylor stepped down in **January 2023**, returning Benioff to sole CEO.

**Other key executives**: **Amy Weaver** (President and CFO), **David Schellhass** (Chief Strategy Officer).
