# Samsung Electronics

Samsung Electronics Co., Ltd. (SEC) is the crown jewel of the Samsung Group chaebol, founded in 1969 and headquartered in Suwon, South Korea. It accounts for ~70% of Samsung Group's revenue and is the world's largest technology company by revenue in many years, with 2024 revenue of ~US$221 billion. SEC dominates in semiconductors (DRAM, NAND), smartphones (Galaxy S/Note/Z Fold series), displays (OLED/LCD panels for Apple, Sony, HTC), TVs (world #1 since 2006), and home appliances. It is majority-owned by foreign investors, with the National Pension Service (8.69%) and Samsung Life Insurance (8.51%) as largest domestic shareholders.

## Timeline

### 1969–1987: Early Years and Foundation

- **Jan 13, 1969**: Samsung Electric Industries established as an industrial part of Samsung Group in Suwon, South Korea. Initial joint venture Samsung-Sanyo Electric with Sanyo and Sumitomo Corporation for TVs, calculators, refrigerators, air conditioners, washing machines.
- **1970**: Samsung-NEC joint venture established for home appliances and audiovisual devices, later became Samsung SDI.
- **1973**: Samsung-Sanyo Parts created, predecessor of Samsung Electro-Mechanics.
- **By 1981**: Samsung Electric had manufactured over 10 million black-and-white televisions.
- **1974**: Samsung Group expanded into semiconductor business by acquiring Korea Semiconductor, on the verge of bankruptcy. This laid the foundation for Samsung's later chip dominance.
- **Feb 1983**: Lee Byung-chul (Samsung founder) announced the "Tokyo Declaration" — Samsung's plan to become a DRAM vendor. At the time, South Korea had zero semiconductor capability and faced a decade-plus technology gap.
- **1984**: Samsung successfully developed its first 64kb DRAM, reducing the technology gap from ~10+ years to ~4 years versus first-world competitors. Used technologies from Micron Technology (US) for DRAM and Sharp (Japan) for SRAM/ROM.
- **1988**: Samsung Electric Industries merged with Samsung Semiconductor & Communications to form **Samsung Electronics** (current entity). Also launched first mobile phone in South Korea that year.

### 1988–1995: Consumer Struggles and Quality Crisis

- **1988**: First Samsung mobile phone launched in Korean market — sales initially poor.
- **Early 1990s**: Motorola held 60%+ market share in Korea vs. Samsung's ~10%. Samsung's mobile phone division struggled with poor quality; exit from the sector was frequently discussed internally.
- Samsung's component-first strategy (rather than consumer brands) began taking shape during this period as a corrective pivot.

### 1995–2008: Component Manufacturing and Design Strategy

- **1995**: Lee Kun-Hee became chairman and decided Samsung needed fundamental change. Shelved under-selling product lines and pursued design-led manufacturing of components.
- **1996**: Declared "Year of Design Revolution" — building design capabilities as competitive asset. Goal: challenge Sony as world's largest consumer electronics manufacturer.
- **1992**: World's first 64MB DRAM developed.
- **1994**: World's first 256MB DRAM.
- **1996**: World's first 1GB DRAM.
- **1998**: Samsung sponsored the Nagano Winter Olympics — its first major global sporting event sponsorship.
- **2004**: World's first 8GB NAND flash memory chip developed.
- **2005**: Supply agreement with Apple sealed — Samsung began supplying memory chips to Apple. Samsung has remained a key Apple component supplier (A7 processors in iPhone 5S, etc.).
- **2007**: Samsung overtook Motorola to become the world's **second-largest smartphone manufacturer**.
- **2009**: Samsung achieved $117bn in revenue, **overtaking Hewlett-Packard** to become the world's largest technology company by sales.

### 2008–2020: Mobile Dominance and Galaxy Era

- **2009**: U.S. and EU fined Samsung (alongside other memory chip makers) for DRAM price-fixing involvement (1999–2002).
- **2010**: EU granted Samsung immunity from prosecution as informant in LCD price-fixing cartel investigation, leading to implications against LG Display and HannStar.
- **2010**: Chairman Lee Kun-hee returned from temporary retirement: "Samsung Electronics' future is not guaranteed, as most of our flagship products will be obsolete within ten years." Ambitious $400bn annual revenue target set.
- **2011**: Samsung sold its hard disk drive (HDD) operations to Seagate Technology for $1.4 billion.
- **2012**: **Kwon Oh-hyun** appointed CEO of Samsung Electronics. Samsung became the **largest vendor of smartphones** (remains #1 to this day).
- **2014**: Galaxy S4 launched. Samsung tested and pioneered enhanced 5G technology. Acquired SmartThings (smart home IoT).
- **2015**: Galaxy S6 and S6 Edge launched with significant redesign and **curved screen** technology — a design breakthrough.
- **2016**: **Galaxy Note 7 crisis** — devices overheated and caught fire due to defective batteries. Global recall and temporary production halt. Samsung recovered by launching Galaxy S8 and Note 8 in 2017. Acquired Harman International Industries for $8 billion — major step into automotive technology (connected car solutions).
- **2017**: Samsung reported **record profits** driven by semiconductor business (memory chips). Galaxy S8 and Note 8 launched, restoring consumer confidence.
- **2018**: Samsung overtook Intel to become the **world's largest semiconductor company** by revenue — the first time Intel was dethroned after decades of leadership.
- **2020**: Samsung introduced new logo and brand identity, dropping the blue color from its wordmark.

### 2021–Present: AI Era and Future Technologies

- **2021**: Announced $17 billion investment to build a new semiconductor manufacturing facility in **Taylor, Texas** — part of strategy to expand chip production amid global semiconductor shortage.
- **2022**: US President Joe Biden visited Samsung Electronics semiconductor complex in Pyeongtaek, South Korea, highlighting the strategic importance of the semiconductor industry.
- **2023**: Partnership with British Gas to integrate SmartThings energy management services. Samsung announced energy-efficient heat pumps for UK net-zero goals.
- **2024**: At CES 2024, Samsung demonstrated **Ballie**, an AI-powered home robot for daily tasks, pet monitoring, and smart home integration.
- **2025**: Samsung Electronics became the **largest shareholder** of South Korea's Rainbow Robotics (robotics AI). Co-CEO Han Jong-hee died on March 25, 2025 (age 63). **Jun Young-hyun** became the company's sole leader just a week later.
- **2025**: Samsung announced massive US manufacturing expansion including $40bn in new facilities.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Mobile Communications
- **Galaxy S series** (flagship Android smartphones) — Galaxy S24 Ultra with AI features
- **Galaxy Z Fold/Flip series** (premium foldable smartphones)
- **Galaxy A/M series** (midrange/budget)
- **Galaxy Tab S series** (tablets)
- **Galaxy Watch, Galaxy Buds** (wearables)
- Samsung Pay (mobile payment)

### Semiconductor (Device Solutions)
- **DRAM** — world #1, supplies Apple, Sony, HTC, Nokia
- **NAND Flash** — world #1
- **CMOS Image Sensors** — ISOCELL brand
- **Mobile processors** — Exynos (used in some Galaxy phones)
- **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)** — key AI chip component

### Consumer Electronics
- **QLED/Micro LED TVs** — world #1 TV manufacturer since 2006
- **Home appliances** — refrigerators, washing machines, air conditioners
- **Monitors** — Odyssey gaming monitors, Business monitors
- **Audio** — soundbars, wireless earbuds

## Corporate Governance

- **Executive Chairman**: Lee Jae-yong (de facto leader of Samsung Group, grandson of founder)
- **Vice Chairman & CEO**: Jun Young-hyun (Device Solutions)
- **President & CEO**: Roh Tae-Moon (Mobile Communications)
- Headquartered at **Samsung Digital City**, Suwon
- 262,647 employees globally (2024)
