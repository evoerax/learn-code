# Kakao

Kakao Corporation is a South Korean internet conglomerate headquartered in Jeju City, formed through the merger of Daum Communications and the original Kakao Inc. in 2010 (renamed Daum Kakao in 2014, then simply Kakao in 2015). Its flagship product **KakaoTalk** (launched March 2010) dominates the Korean messaging market with approximately **93% penetration** and achieved ~90% domestic market share by 2015. With 2024 revenue of ₩7.87 trillion and assets of ₩25.77 trillion, Kakao has expanded into entertainment (Kakao Entertainment), gaming (Kakao Games), fintech (Kakao Pay, Kakao Bank), mobility (Kakao T), and cloud services (Kakao Enterprise). Notable shareholders include founder Brian Kim (23.68%), Tencent/Maximo Pte (5.95%), and National Pension Service (6.10%).

## Timeline

### 2006–2014: Founding of Kakao Corp.

- **2006**: Kakao Inc. founded by **Kim Beom-soo** (金範洙), former CEO of NHN Corporation (which emerged from the Hangame and Naver.com merger). Kakao was created as a mobile-first internet company.
- **March 2010**: **KakaoTalk** officially launched as a free messaging app for smartphones. Within months it became Korea's dominant mobile messaging platform.
- **2012**: KakaoTalk reached 100 million registered users internationally.
- **2013**: Three of the world's top 10 Android games (by App Annie) were tied to KakaoTalk platform: Everybody's Marble, Cookie Run, and Anipang — generating approximately $200M revenue.
- **December 2013**: Named **world's third top publisher by monthly revenue at Google Play** (App Annie report). Ranked #1 publisher for iOS and Google Play in South Korea.
- **2014**: KakaoTalk nominated for **Most Innovative Mobile App Award at Global Mobile Awards 2014**.

### 2014–2015: Daum Merger and Rebranding

- **May 26, 2014**: Kakao Corp. announced merger with **Daum Communications** (one of Korea's top internet portals) via stock swap.
- **March 10, 2015**: Launched **KakaoTaxi** service (ride-hailing via app). Within 8 months, ~600,000 ride requests per day on the platform.
- **August 2015**: **Rim Ji-hoon** appointed as new CEO, replacing Kim Beom-soo after gambling and censorship issues within Kakao ecosystem. Kim Beom-soo remained as largest shareholder with 22.2% stake.
- **September 2015**: Company renamed from **Daum Kakao** to **Kakao**, restoring pre-merger brand name.

### 2016–2020: New Business Model and Fintech Expansion

- **January 2016**: Acquired **76.4% stake in LOEN Entertainment** (large Korean entertainment company) for $1.5 billion; rebranded to **Kakao M** (later Kakao Entertainment).
- **April 2017**: **Kakao Pay** spun off as separate company (digital payments, money transfer, insurance, investment).
- **2017**: Became approved as Korea's **first internet-only bank** (Kakao Bank) by financial regulators. Although K Bank launched first (months prior), Kakao Bank attracted 820,000 customers within **4 days** of its July 27, 2017 launch. The app was downloaded 1.5 million times in the same period. By one month: 3.5 million customers.
- **September 2017**: Kakao Bank lent ₩1.4 trillion ($1.2 billion) — constituting **40% of all loans in South Korea** for that month.
- **2017**: KakaoTaxi renamed to **Kakao T**. Launched premium extension **Kakao Taxi Black** (luxury cars from Mercedes Benz, Lexus, BMW; fares started at ₩8,000).
- **March 2018**: Rim Ji-hoon stepped down; **Yeo Min-soo and Joh Su-yong** appointed as co-CEOs.
- **April 2019**: Announced e-bike sharing service launch with initial fleet of 400 bikes in two major cities.
- **2020**: Kakao became Korea's **10th largest company by market capitalization** (surpassing Hyundai Motor), driven by pandemic-era demand for social IT infrastructure. Launched advertising on KakaoTalk. Recorded highest-ever first-quarter revenue and operating profit.

### 2021–Present: Entertainment Consolidation and Controversies

- **March 2021**: **Kakao Page** and **Kakao M** merged into **Kakao Entertainment**.
- **January 2022**: **Namkoong Whon** named CEO.
- **July 2022**: **Hong Eun-taek** appointed co-CEO — internal controversy due to past behavior toward coworkers.
- **October 2022**: Co-CEO Namkoong Whon **resigned after a massive service outage** caused by a data center fire, which locked out 53 million Kakao users — triggering massive public attention and accountability.
- **February 2023**: Kakao acquired **9.05% stake in SM Entertainment**, becoming second-largest shareholder. In March, Hybe sold its SM shares to Kakao following failed takeover bid. This led to **Financial Supervisory Service raid on Kakao's offices** on suspicion of stock manipulation based on allegations from Hybe.
- **March 2024**: Hong Eun-taek stepped down; **Chung Shin-a** appointed as CEO (first female CEO).
- **May 2024**: Korea's **Fair Trade Commission approved SM stake acquisition** with two corrective measures.
- **December 2025**: **Daum was split off** from Kakao under its subsidiary AXZ.
- **March 2026**: **LAAA Investment** (backed by LY Corporation/Yahoo Japan) acquired part of Kakao's stake in Kakao Games, becoming its largest shareholder.

## Key Products and Services

### Messaging & Social
- **KakaoTalk** — dominant messaging app (~93% Korea penetration, 90%+ market share)
- **KakaoStory** — image, video, music sharing
- **KakaoTV** — live streaming integrated with KakaoTalk open chatting

### Fintech
- **Kakao Pay** — digital payments, money transfer, insurance, investment (spun off 2017)
- **Kakao Bank** — internet-only bank (2017 launch; 820,000 customers in 4 days)

### Mobility
- **Kakao T** — ride-hailing (taxi, premium Black service), e-bike sharing, parking, navigation, car maintenance, express delivery

### Entertainment
- **Kakao Entertainment** — webtoons, web novels, music (Melon), TV series, films (via Kakao M)
- **Piccoma** — manga/novel content service platform (Japan-focused)
- **Kakao Games** — game development and publishing; VR/AR content

### Other
- **Kakao Enterprise** — cloud services
- **Kakao Healthcare** — digital healthcare
- **Kakao Style** — personalized style recommendations
- **KakaoHairshop** — beauty salon finder
- **KakaoMap** — map service (primarily Korea)
- **Klaytn** — blockchain platform (via Ground X subsidiary)
- **KakaoFriends** — character brand (diverse products including finance, distribution, foods)
- **Tistory** — blog hosting service

## Corporate Structure

- **CEO**: Chung Shin-a (since March 2024)
- **Headquarters**: Jeju City, South Korea
- **Major shareholders**: Brian Kim (23.68%), National Pension Service (6.10%), Tencent/Maximo Pte (5.95%)
- **Group companies**: Kakao Pay, KakaoBank, Kakao Mobility, Kakao Entertainment, Kakao Games, Kakao Enterprise, Kakao Healthcare, Kakao piccoma
- **Employees**: 10,644 (2020)
