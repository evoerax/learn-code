# Naver

Naver Corporation (stylized as NAVER) is a South Korean internet conglomerate headquartered in Seongnam, operating the search engine Naver — which holds approximately **75% of South Korea's search market share** and is often called "Korea's Google." Founded by Lee Hae-jin in 1999, Naver pioneered user-generated content in Korea through Knowledge iN (Q&A platform) and later Webtoon (digital comics). With 2021 revenue of ₩6.8 trillion and net income of ₩16.4 trillion, Naver has expanded globally through subsidiaries including Webtoon Entertainment (global digital comics), Wattpad (storytelling platform), and Poshmark (social commerce). Naver was designated a **Koreanchaebol (conglomerate)** by the KFTC in 2025.

## Timeline

### 1999–2000: Foundation and Launch

- **June 2, 1999**: Naver Corporation founded by **Lee Hae-jin** (李海镇), initially under the name **Naver Comm**.
- **1999**: Launched **Junior Naver** — a search service for children with content including Pororo (for ages 3-5), Pany's Room, children's songs, and Disney Zone. (Service scheduled to end May 27, 2025.)
- **July 2000**: Naver merged with **Hangame Communications** (a leading Korean online game company) plus other entities including Oneque and Search Solutions — creating a major internet player.
- **August 2000**: Naver launched **comprehensive search** — the first in Korea to organize search results by type (blogs, webpages, images, cafes, etc.) on a single page. This differentiated Naver from competitors and laid the foundation for its market dominance.

### 2001–2012: NHN Era and Global Expansion

- **2001**: Naver Comm renamed **NHN Corporation** (Next Human Network), though both Naver and Hangame continued operating under original brand names. NHN = NAVER + Hangame + Network.
- **2002**: NHN listed on **KOSDAQ** stock exchange. Launched **Knowledge iN** — one of the world's first Q&A platforms, allowing users to post questions and award points to helpful answers. By 2016: 100 million questions, 200 million answers.
- **2003**: NHN merged all Japanese subsidiaries into **NHN Japan**.
- **2005**: Launched **Happy Bean** online donation service. Established NHN USA branch.
- **2007**: Launched **Naver Japan** to pursue search engine business in Japan.
- **2008**: NHN first listed on **Forbes Global 2000**. Became largest market cap company on KOSDAQ before transfer to **KOSPI** main market in November.
- **2009**: Only two South Korean CEOs (including NHN's Kim Sang-hun) appeared on Forbes Asia's Fab 50 list.
- **2010**: NHN moved into new HQ — the **Green Factory** (27 floors above ground, 7 underground) in Bundang-gu, Seongnam. First Korean web company to build and operate its own data center (Data Center GAK in Chuncheon, Gangwon Province, built 2013).
- **2011**: NHN Japan launched **Line** messaging app, which quickly soared in global usage. NHN Japan, Naver Japan, and Livedoor merged into a single NHN Japan entity. NHN established Singapore branch.

### 2013–2015: Split from NHN and Naver Corporation Birth

- **January 2013**: NHN split — **Naver Corporation** and **NHN Entertainment** (formerly Hangame) formally separated after 13 years together. NHN Japan similarly split into Line Corporation (web services) and Hangame Japan (game services).
- **October 1, 2013**: Company officially renamed **Naver Corporation**, restoring its pre-merger name.
- **June 2013**: Naver became **first Korean web company to build and operate its own data center** (Data Center GAK in Chuncheon).
- **2015**: Launched **Naver Pay** (mobile payment service). Launched **Works Mobile** for B2B collaboration businesses. Merged with Entry Education Labs (software education platform).

### 2016–2020: Listing, AI, and New Business Units

- **2016**: Naver's annual revenue was ₩4.02 trillion.
- **2016**: Line Corporation double-listed on NYSE and Tokyo Stock Exchange.
- **August 2016**: Naver spun off app **SNOW** into separate subsidiary Snow Corp.
- **2016**: Launched **Project Flower** — long-term platform project supporting small businesses and creators, with 1.6 million local businesses, 400+ webcomic authors, 150 web novel writers, 10,000 illustrators.
- **September 2016**: Naver and Line announced combined €100 million investment into K-Fund 1, a European startup accelerator fund operated by Korelya Capital (founded by former French SME Minister Fleur Pelleron).
- **2017**: Established subsidiaries **Snow, Naver Labs, and Naver Webtoon**. Opened **Space Green** startup space inside Station F (France). Acquired **Xerox Research Centre Europe** (Grenoble), rebranded as **Naver Labs Europe**.
- **2018**: Launched **Naver Z Corp** focusing on Metaverse; launched **Zepeto** online game platform (metaverse/social gaming).
- **2017**: Launched AI translator **Naver Papago** (N2MT neural machine translation technology). Released **Naver Whale** browser (5 years in development).

### 2021–Present: Global Expansion and Major Acquisitions

- **January 2021**: Announced acquisition of **Wattpad** (global storytelling platform) for **US$600 million**.
- **March 2021**: **Line Corporation** merged with **Yahoo Japan** (operated by Z Holdings, a SoftBank Group subsidiary) under a new structure where Naver and Softbank Corp each hold 50% of A Holdings Corp (majority shareholder of Z Holdings). This created a tech giant competing with Google, Amazon, Facebook, Apple, Baidu, Alibaba, and Tencent.
- **May 2021**: Partnered with **CJ ENM** to acquire **Munpia** (Korea's third-largest web-novel platform).
- **October 2022**: Agreed to buy **Poshmark** (social commerce marketplace) for **US$1.2 billion** — finalized January 2023.
- **2023**: Acquired 9.05% stake in **SM Entertainment** (became second-largest shareholder); later acquired Hybe's stake as well, sparking regulatory scrutiny.
- **2024**: Fair Trade Commission approved SM Entertainment stake acquisition with two corrective measures.

## Key Products and Services

### Search & Portal
- **Naver Search** — Korea's dominant search engine (~75% market share)
- **Knowledge iN** — Q&A platform (2002–present; 100M+ questions)
- **Naver Cafe & Blog** — UGC platforms (23M blogs, 10M cafes)
- **Naver Dictionary** — supports 34 languages

### Digital Content
- **Naver Webtoon / Line Webtoon** — world's largest digital comics platform; launched 2004; 65M+ monthly users globally; split off as subsidiary Naver Webtoon in 2017
- **Wattpad** — storytelling platform (acquired 2021)
- **V Live** — K-pop streaming (merged with Weverse 2022, shut down Dec 2022)
- **Naver TV** — web dramas and user content

### AI & Technology
- **Naver Papago** — AI neural machine translator (15 languages)
- **Clova** — AI platform (virtual assistant, smart speakers WAVE/FACE)
- **Naver Whale** — AI-powered omni-tasking browser

### Commerce & Finance
- **Naver Pay** — mobile payment
- **Naver Shopping** — e-commerce

### Other Notable
- **Band** — social networking app (2012; Camp Mobile subsidiary)
- **SNOW** — photo/video app (200M+ users)
- **Zepeto** — metaverse platform

## Corporate Structure

- **CEO**: Choi Soo-Yeon
- **Headquarters**: Naver Green Factory, Bundang-gu, Seongnam, South Korea
- **Owners**: National Pension Service (8.23%), BlackRock (5.05%), Lee Hae-jin (3.74%)
- **Subsidiaries**: Webtoon Entertainment, Wattpad, Poshmark, Snow Corp, Naver Labs, Naver Cloud
- **Employees**: ~4,103 (excluding subsidiaries, 2021)
