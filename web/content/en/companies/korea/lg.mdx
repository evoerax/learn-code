# LG Electronics

LG Electronics Inc. (LGE) is a South Korean multinational appliance and electronics manufacturer headquartered in Seoul, founded in 1958 as **GoldStar** (金星). It is one of the largest Korean conglomerates and a global leader in home appliances, air conditioning systems, premium OLED televisions, automotive components, and display panels. LG Electronics is a flagship affiliate of **LG Corporation**, alongside LG Display, LG Innotek, and other LG Group subsidiaries. The company announced 2024 revenue of approximately **₩84.7 trillion** (~$62 billion USD) and is best known globally for its OLED TV technology, refrigerator compressors, and HVAC systems.

## Timeline

### 1958–1980: GoldStar Era — First Korean Electronics Pioneer

- **1958**: **GoldStar Co., Ltd.** (주식회사 금성사) founded by **Koo In-hwoi** (具仁会, 1915–1989) in Seoul, as Korea's first private electronics company. Initial products: electric fans, radios, and simple household appliances.
- **1960**: Produced Korea's first transistor radio — establishing Korea's consumer electronics manufacturing capability a decade before the rise of Samsung Electronics.
- **1965**: Established joint venture with ** Hitachi** (Japan) for air conditioning and refrigerator production — technology transfer agreement critical to building Korean manufacturing capabilities.
- **1966**: First Korean-made window-type air conditioner developed.
- **1967**: GoldStar produced Korea's first refrigerator (model R-30), cementing its role as Korea's primary electronics manufacturer during a period of rapid national industrialization (the "Economic Development Survey of Korea").
- **1975**: First Korean company to export television sets to the United States — significant milestone for Korean electronics competitiveness.
- **1977**: Developed and produced Korea's first microwave oven. Same year: introduced the first Korean air conditioner unit.
- **1978**: Developed and mass-produced Korea's first **color TV** (model CD-1400), marking the transition from black-and-white to color broadcasting era in Korea.
- **1979**: GoldStar introduced Korea's first VCR (video cassette recorder), further expanding into entertainment electronics.

### 1980–1995: Globalization and the Rise of GoldStar/LG

- **1981**: Established **GoldStar Taiwan** — first overseas production subsidiary, marking the beginning of GoldStar's international manufacturing footprint.
- **1982**: Founded **GoldStar Italy** — European production base.
- **1983**: Launched Korea's first 1-horsepower (HP) air conditioner with rotary compressor — a technology milestone in climate control.
- **1986**: GoldStar introduced the world's first 35-inch color TV — demonstrating engineering leadership in display technology.
- **1990**: GoldStar's annual revenue exceeded ₩1 trillion for the first time.
- **1991**: GoldStar developed Korea's first **LCD TV** prototype, anticipating the display technology revolution.
- **1992**: GoldStar USA established for North American sales and marketing.
- **1995**: **LG Group rebranded** GoldStar as **LG Electronics Inc.** — coinciding with the company's shift toward higher-value consumer electronics and the global rise of the "LG" brand. The rebrand also absorbed GoldStar's global manufacturing assets under the LG umbrella.
- **1995**: LG's "Golden Rover" campaign in China was among the company's first major forays into the vast Chinese consumer market.

### 1995–2010: Global Brand Building and OLED Innovation

- **1998**: LG introduced the world's first **60-inch plasma television** — establishing LG as a leader in large-format display technology.
- **1999**: LG launched **CYON**, its own mobile phone brand in Korea, competing with Samsung and other domestic brands.
- **2000**: LG developed the world's first **82-inch TFT-LCD** display, setting size records for flat-panel displays.
- **2002**: LG acquired **Hyundai LCD** and **Hyundai Information Technology** — strengthening LG's LCD panel manufacturing capabilities. These acquisitions laid the foundation for the later LG Display spin-off.
- **2004**: LG launched its **LG Chocolate** phone (KG800) — one of the most iconic mobile phone designs of the mid-2000s. The slide-up touchscreen design and red glow keypad became a cultural phenomenon, particularly popular in Asian markets.
- **2005**: **LG Display** was formally spun off as a separate publicly traded company, creating one of the world's largest LCD panel manufacturers.
- **2006**: LG launched the **LG Prada** phone (KE850) — co-designed with Italian luxury fashion house Prada. The phone was notable for being one of the first capacitive touchscreen phones, launched several months before the original iPhone (January 2007). LG sold 300,000 units in the first year.
- **2008**: LG launched **LG Arena** (KM900) with 3D S-Class user interface — the company's most advanced feature phone before the smartphone era.
- **2008**: LG became the **world's second-largest mobile phone manufacturer** (after Nokia), peaking at approximately 100 million units shipped globally.

### 2010–2016: Smartphone Era and Challenges

- **2010**: LG launched the **Optimus** smartphone line, running Android — LG was among the first major Android OEM partners for Google's mobile OS.
- **2011**: LG launched the **Optimus Black** (P970) — the thinnest smartphone at the time (6mm) with a NOVA IPS display for outdoor readability.
- **2013**: LG launched **LG G2** — widely considered the best Android phone of 2013. The rear-mounted button design (power and volume buttons on the back) was an innovative ergonomic choice that LG reused in subsequent generations. The G2 sold 3 million units in first 3 months.
- **2014**: LG launched **LG G3** — first QHD (2560×1440) smartphone display. The G3 achieved strong sales (approximately 10 million units) driven by superior display quality and competitive pricing against Samsung Galaxy S5.
- **2015**: **LG G4** launched with leather back cover option — premium design differentiation from Samsung and Apple. Sold approximately 5 million units.
- **2016**: **LG G5** launched as a modular smartphone — the G5's "Friends" ecosystem (Hi-Fi Plus audio module, CAM Plus camera grip) was an ambitious but ultimately unsuccessful attempt at modularity. This was a major commercial failure. Same year, **LG V10** launched with dual front cameras and secondary ticker display — pioneering design features later adopted by other manufacturers.

### 2016–2021: Smartphone Decline and Strategic Retreat

- **2016**: **LG G5** modular approach was a commercial failure, causing significant losses and damaging brand reputation.
- **2016**: LG's mobile division reported an operating loss of approximately ₩1.35 trillion ($1.1 billion) — beginning years of persistent mobile division losses.
- **2017**: **LG G6** abandoned modularity, returned to conventional unibody design. Strong build quality but competitive pressure from Samsung Galaxy S8 was intense.
- **2017**: LG launched **LG V30** — regarded as one of LG's finest phones with OLED full-vision display, Hi-Fi Quad DAC audio, and excellent camera. Sold approximately 1 million units — below break-even threshold.
- **2018**: **LG G7 ThinQ** and **LG V35 ThinQ** launched but struggled to differentiate against Samsung's Galaxy S9 and Apple's iPhone X.
- **2019**: **LG G8 ThinQ** launched but failed commercially. Mobile division losses continued — cumulative losses exceeded ₩5 trillion since 2016.
- **2020**: **LG Wing 5G** launched — an innovative swiveling dual-screen smartphone with T-shaped display. Technically ambitious but niche market appeal resulted in poor sales (~50,000 units in first month).
- **2021**: **LG Velvet** launched — new design philosophy (Raindrop camera, 3D Arc Design) but mid-range Snapdragon 765G processor undermined flagship ambitions. The LG Explorer Project followed with the **LG Rollable** (rollable display smartphone) — but before commercial launch, LG announced in April 2021 it was **exiting the mobile phone business entirely** after inability to find a buyer for the division.

### 2021–Present: Post-Smartphone Restructuring and B2B Pivot

- **2021**: LG officially withdrew from the smartphone market (July 31, 2021) — the last flagship device was the **LG V70** (unreleased). LG committed to providing software support for existing devices (some until 2024).
- **2021**: LG announced a strategic pivot to **B2B (business-to-business)** growth areas: electric vehicle (EV) components, robotics, smart factory solutions, and digital healthcare.
- **2021**: LG's vehicle components (VS) division secured major contracts with **General Motors** for the Ultium EV battery platform — a key milestone in LG's automotive components strategy. LG was also a major supplier of in-vehicle infotainment systems to multiple global automakers.
- **2022**: LG launched **LG Omnipod** — concept vehicle with flexible display interiors, demonstrating its vision for automotive display solutions.
- **2022**: LG's Home Appliance division launched the **LG Styler** (steam clothing care system) and **WashTower** (combined washer-dryer) as premium differentiation products.
- **2023**: LG reported record revenue of **₩84.5 trillion** (~$63.7B USD) — driven by the VS (Vehicle Components) division and premium home appliances. Operating profit reached ₩3.5 trillion ($2.6B).
- **2023**: LG Innotek (LG subsidiary) became a major supplier of camera modules for Apple's iPhone — the unit was a key earnings driver for the broader LG ecosystem.
- **2024**: LG launched new "**AI Home**" vision — integrating AI into its appliance ecosystem (refrigerators, washers, air conditioners) with ThinQ app integration and energy optimization features.
- **2025**: LG Electronics announced partnership with **Samsung SDI** for solid-state battery development for EVs. Also announced expansion of US manufacturing footprint for appliance production.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Home Appliance (HE) Division
- **Refrigerators** — premium InstaView door-in-door, linear compressor technology
- **Washers/Dryers** — AI DD (Direct Drive) motors, Steam technology
- **Air Conditioners** — DUAL Inverter voice control, residential and commercial HVAC
- **LG Styler** — steam clothing care system (premium niche)
- **Vacuum cleaners** — CordZero line (robot, canister, stick vacuums)

### Vehicle Components (VS) Division
- **EV battery systems** — Ultium Cells LLC (joint venture with GM); packs for multiple global automakers
- **In-vehicle infotainment** — Android Automotive OS-based systems
- **Automotive displays** — curved OLED for premium vehicles
- **Telematics** — connected car communication modules

### Home Entertainment (HE) Division
- **OLED TVs** — evo panel, Gallery Edition, rollable OLED R
- **QNED Mini LED TVs** — premium LCD line
- **webOS smart TV platform** — licensed to other TV manufacturers
- **Audio** — Soundbars (SP11, SP9), earbuds (Tone Free)

### Business Solutions
- **Digital displays** — commercial OLED for hotels, airlines, retail
- **Solar panels** — LG NeON R (residential/commercial photovoltaic)
- **Robot services** — Guide Robot, Serving Robot, Porter Robot (hospitality sector)
