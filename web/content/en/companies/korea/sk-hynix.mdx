# SK hynix

SK hynix Inc. (stylized as SK hynix) is a South Korean semiconductor company specializing in dynamic random-access memory (DRAM) and flash memory chips. It is the world's **second-largest memory semiconductor vendor** (after Samsung) and one of the "Big Two" memory manufacturers alongside Samsung. With 2024 revenue of ~₩66.19 trillion (~$49bn), SK hynix is a major affiliate of SK Group and plays a critical role in global AI chip supply chains, particularly as a supplier of High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) to Nvidia.

## Timeline

### 1983–1995: Hyundai Electronics Era

- **1983**: Hyundai Electronics founded by **Chung Ju-yung** (founder of Hyundai Group) to pursue electronics and semiconductor production. At the time, Hyundai was best known for automobiles, shipbuilding, and heavy industries.
- **Early 1980s**: Hyundai recognized the strategic importance of electronics for the automobile industry and expanded beyond core operations.
- Hyundai's early DRAM strategy faced severe challenges — high entry costs, difficulty achieving satisfactory yield rates compared to rival Samsung which had prior semiconductor experience.
- **1985**: Hyundai altered its DRAM strategy, subcontracting from foreign firms and importing chip designs. Its DRAM chip (using Vitelic Corporation's design) again failed in mass production due to low yield.
- **OEM foundry model** proved more successful — agreements with General Instruments and Texas Instruments helped stabilize operations.
- **1992**: Hyundai became the **world's ninth DRAM manufacturer**.
- **1995**: Rose to be among the world's top 20 semiconductor manufacturing companies.
- **1996**: Hyundai acquired **Maxtor** (US-based disk-drive manufacturer).

### 1998–2001: Asian Financial Crisis, LG Semicon Acquisition, and Hynix Rebranding

- **1997-1998 Asian Financial Crisis**: South Korean government pressured major conglomerates to restructure semiconductor businesses. Samsung was exempt due to competitive strength, but LG and Hyundai faced pressure to merge due to heavy losses during the 1996 semiconductor downturn.
- **1998**: Hyundai Electronics acquired **LG Semicon** for $2.1 billion, positioning directly against Micron Technology. LG Semicon was subsequently rebranded as Hyundai Semiconductor.
  - LG Semicon had been established in 1983 as Goldstar Electron, with operations in Seoul, Cheongju, and Gumi.
- **2001**: The semiconductor industry faced an 80% drop in memory chip prices, causing massive annual losses (~₩5 trillion) for Hyundai. Creditor banks intervened to provide assistance.
- **March 2001**: Hyundai Electronics rebranded as **Hynix Semiconductor** — a portmanteau of "high" and "electronics." Company began selling/spinning off business units to recover:
  - Hyundai Curitel (mobile phones)
  - Hyundai SysComm (CDMA mobile communication chips)
  - Hyundai Autonet (car navigation systems)
  - ImageQuest (flat panel displays)
  - TFT-LCD unit
- **2003**: Hyundai Group affiliates consented to forfeit voting rights and sell stakes in Hynix. Hynix formally **spun off from Hyundai Group** in August 2003.

### 2003–2012: Independent Recovery and SK Group Acquisition

- Hynix creditors (Korea Exchange Bank, Woori Bank, Shinhan Bank, Korea Development Bank) attempted multiple times to sell their stake but failed.
- Korean companies including Hyosung, Dongbu CNI, and former stakeholders (Hyundai Heavy Industries, LG) were considered but either denied or withdrew from bidding.
- **July 2011**: **SK Telecom** (nation's largest telecom company) and STX Group officially entered the bid. STX dropped out in September 2011, leaving SK Telecom as the sole bidder.
- **February 2012**: SK Group acquired Hynix for **$3 billion**. Company renamed **SK Hynix** and was incorporated into SK Group alongside SK Innovation and SK Telecom.
- This acquisition gave SK Hynix access to SK Group's resources and stability for future memory chip investments.

### 2012–2020: Growth and NAND Expansion

- **2012**: Following SK Group acquisition, company focused on expanding DRAM leadership and modernizing facilities.
- **2021**: SK Hynix acquired **Intel's NAND business** for $9 billion, resulting in the establishment of **Solidigm** (Intel's NAND SSD business rebranded under SK Hynix). This made SK Hynix a top-3 NAND flash memory maker.

### 2024–Present: AI Memory Leadership

- **September 26, 2024**: SK Hynix announced it had begun **mass production of the world's first 12-layer HBM3E chips** — a key memory for AI accelerators.
- **Q2 2025**: SK Hynix controlled **38% of the global DRAM market**.
- **March 2026**: Reports emerged that SK Hynix had **filed confidentially with the US SEC for a potential listing on the New York Stock Exchange** in 2026 — a major strategic move to access US capital markets and strengthen its global standing amid the AI chip race.

## Key Products

- **DRAM** — computing, consumer, network, and mobile memory
- **NAND Flash** — including SSDs
- **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)** — supplied to Nvidia for AI GPUs; HBM3E fifth-generation chips
- **CMOS Image Sensors**
- **Solid-state drives (SSDs)** — through Solidigm brand

## Manufacturing Facilities

- **Icheon** and **Cheongju**, South Korea
- **Wuxi**, **Chongqing**, and **Dalian**, China

## Corporate Structure

- **Parent**: SK Group (since 2012)
- **Major shareholders**: SK Square (20.07%), National Pension Service (7.90%)
- **CEO**: Awaited official appointment following March 2026 NYSE filing news
- 46,863 employees (2024)
