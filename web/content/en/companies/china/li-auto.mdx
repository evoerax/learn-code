# Li Auto

**Li Auto Inc.** (理想汽车, Lǐxiǎng Qìchē) is a Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer founded in 2015 by Li Xiang (李想), headquartered in Beijing, with manufacturing facilities in Changzhou, Jiangsu. Li Auto pioneered the extended-range electric vehicle (EREV) category in China — vehicles with a small gasoline engine acting as a range extender (generator) to charge the battery, eliminating pure EV range anxiety while delivering electric driving performance. This technology choice (rather than pure BEV) proved commercially decisive: Li Auto became the first Chinese EV maker to achieve annual profitability in 2023, and its L-series family SUVs became best-sellers in the Chinese mid-to-large SUV segment. The company listed on NASDAQ in July 2020 (ticker: LI) and Hong Kong Exchange in March 2021. FY2024 revenue was ¥144.5 billion with net income of ¥8.0 billion, making Li Auto one of the few profitable Chinese EV makers.

## Development Timeline

### 2015–2019: Founding, Rebranding, and Li One

Li Auto was founded in 2015 by **Li Xiang** (李想), who previously co-founded and built PCPop (泡泡网) — China's leading consumer electronics review site — and later Autohome (汽车之家), China's largest automotive media platform (NYSE: ATHM, later taken private). Li Xiang's deep automotive internet background gave Li Auto a unique customer-centric product perspective that emphasized family needs and practical features over technology spectacle.

The company originally operated as **Beijing Chehejia Information Technology Co., Ltd.** (北京车和家信息技术有限责任公司), developing low-speed electric vehicles (LSEVs) for car-sharing and ride-hailing markets. In 2018, a joint venture agreement with DiDi for LSEV supply collapsed, and the low-speed EV project was abandoned after government authorities declined to categorize the vehicles for road use. This pivot forced Li Auto to rebrand and pursue a different strategy.

In 2019, the company restructured as **Leading Ideal Inc.** (later shortened to **Li Auto** in 2020) and shifted focus to the premium extended-range SUV market — a strategic choice that differentiated Li Auto from pure BEV competitors like NIO and XPeng. The Li One, unveiled in April 2019, was a large 6-seat family SUV featuring a 1.2L turbocharged gasoline range extender (generating electricity to charge a 40.5 kWh battery), dual-motor AWD producing 326 hp, and an NEDC electric range of 180 km (approximately 130 km real-world). The range extender eliminated "range anxiety" — a major consumer barrier to pure EV adoption in China — by allowing the gasoline engine to extend total range to over 800 km.

### 2020: NASDAQ IPO and COVID-Era Growth

Deliveries of the Li One commenced in November 2019, and by early 2020 Li Auto was gaining traction among Chinese families seeking a practical alternative to pure EVs. In **July 2020**, Li Auto conducted its **NASDAQ IPO** (ticker: LI), raising **$1.1 billion** at a valuation of approximately $10 billion. The IPO was notably supported by major Chinese investors including **Meituan** (CEO Xing Wang became strategic investor) and **Source Code Capital**, reflecting confidence in Li Auto's product-market fit despite early-stage losses.

During 2020, Li Auto sold approximately **33,457 Li One vehicles** — impressive for a new brand in a competitive market. The COVID-19 pandemic actually benefited Li Auto as consumers sought personal vehicles over public transportation. The Li One's large cabin, family features (rear entertainment screens, refrigerator, massage seats), and gas-engine range extension resonated strongly in the Chinese family SUV segment.

### 2021–2022: L9 Launch and Product Family Transition

In **March 2022**, Li Auto launched the **L9** full-size flagship SUV — a 6-seat luxury vehicle positioned above the Li One, competing with the BMW X7 and Mercedes-Benz GLS at roughly half the price (¥459,800). The L9 featured Li Auto's new **Li AD Max** autonomous driving system (featuring a禾赛科技 Hesai lidar unit, 12 ultrasonic sensors, and 11 cameras), a 1.5L range extender, and an interior designed around family needs — dual15.7-inch rear entertainment screens, a 2024款的 15.7-inch center screen with OLED display, and a Spa-grade rear seat configuration.

In **September 2022**, Li Auto launched the **L8** (shorter-wheelbase version of the L9, positioned as an L9 "Pro" variant) and the **L7** (5-seat version without the third row, targeted at younger families). This rapid product family expansion was deliberate: Li Auto was pursuing a "one product per segment" strategy, with the L9 (full-size 6-seat), L8 (full-size 5-seat), L7 (mid-size 5-seat), and L1 (planned mid-size 6-seat) forming a complete family lineup.

In **October 2022**, Li Auto made the controversial decision to **discontinue the Li One** after only ~2 years of production, replacing it with the L8 and L7 at slightly higher price points. Li One owners who had purchased months earlier criticized the abrupt discontinuation, creating a public relations issue.

### 2023: Profitability Milestone and L7/L8 Ramp

2023 was Li Auto's breakthrough year. The company's **L-series product family** achieved rapid sales acceleration, driven by strong demand for the L7 (5-seat mid-size SUV, China's fastest-growing SUV segment). Deliveries surged:
- **2023**: 376,030 vehicles delivered (vs. 133,246 in 2022) — a 182% year-over-year increase

Li Auto became **the first Chinese EV maker to achieve annual net profit** in 2023, reporting net income of approximately ¥11.8 billion for the full year on revenue of ¥123.8 billion. This profitability was driven by:
1. **Operating leverage**: High average selling prices (¥300,000–450,000) with falling per-unit manufacturing costs as volume grew
2. **EREV cost advantage**: The 1.5L range extender avoided expensive large-battery costs that pressured pure BEV margins
3. **Minimal dealer network**: Li Auto uses a direct-to-consumer (DTC) model similar to Tesla, avoiding franchise dealer margins

In **November 2023**, Li Auto unveiled the **Li Mega** — its first pure battery electric vehicle (BEV), a full-size MPV (multi-purpose vehicle) competing with the Denza D9, Buick GL8, and XPeng X9. The Li Mega featured an 800V architecture, 5C ultra-fast charging (achieving 500 km of range in 12 minutes of charging), and a design distinctly different from Li Auto's other models.

### 2024: L6 Launch and Market Leadership

In **2024**, Li Auto expanded further with the **L6** mid-size SUV — a 5-seat vehicle positioned below the L7 to capture younger first-car buyers and urban families. The L6 competed directly with the问界 M7 (Aito M7), XPeng G6, and Tesla Model Y.

Full-year 2024 deliveries reached **500,508 vehicles** (up from 376,030 in 2023), with FY2024 revenue of ¥144.5 billion and net income of ¥8.04 billion. Li Auto maintained its position as one of the few profitable Chinese EV startups, though net income fell from 2023 levels due to intensified competition and price cuts in response to the BYD DM-i and Huawei AITO aggressive pricing.

### 2025: Pure EV Expansion with i-Series

In **2025**, Li Auto launched its **i6** (mid-size pure electric sedan) and **i8** (full-size pure electric sedan) — marking the company's first major expansion into the pure BEV market beyond the Li Mega MPV. This was a significant strategic shift: Li Auto was now competing in pure EV segments rather than relying solely on its EREV advantage. The i6/i8 used the same 800V/5C charging architecture as the Li Mega and featured Li Auto's latest Li AD Max vision-only autonomous driving system.

Li Auto's total 2025 deliveries were approximately **406,343 units** (down from 500,508 in 2024 due to market competition and consumer hesitation around the pure EV transition), though average selling prices and revenue per vehicle improved with the premium i-series mix.

## Product Lines

### Current Vehicle Portfolio

**Li L-series (EREV — Extended-Range Electric):**
- **L9**: Full-size 6-seat flagship SUV (2022, refreshed 2024); 1.5L turbo range extender + dual motors; 330 km CLTC electric range; 1,300+ km total range; ¥449,800–499,800
- **L8**: Full-size 5-seat SUV (2022, refreshed 2024); same platform as L9; ¥339,800–399,800
- **L7**: Mid-size 5-seat SUV (2022); most popular model; ~200,000 units sold; ¥319,800–379,800
- **L6**: Compact 5-seat SUV (2024); entry-premium; competing with Tesla Model Y; ¥249,800–299,800

**Li i-series (Pure BEV):**
- **Li Mega**: Full-size MPV (2023); 800V architecture; 5C ultra-fast charging; ~700 km CLTC range; ¥559,800
- **Li i6**: Mid-size pure electric sedan (2025); 800V; Li AD Max 3.0; ~600 km CLTC range
- **Li i8**: Full-size pure electric sedan (2025); flagship BEV sedan; ~650 km CLTC range

**Discontinued:**
- **Li One** (2019–2022): Original 6-seat EREV SUV; discontinued October 2022

### Technology

- **EREV (Extended-Range Electric)**: 1.2L or 1.5L turbocharged gasoline engine acting as generator; never mechanically connected to wheels; 40.5–58.8 kWh battery; eliminates range anxiety
- **800V Ultra-Fast Charging** (i-series and Li Mega): 5C charging delivers 500 km range in ~12 minutes at 500 kW peak power
- **Li AD Max**: Autonomous driving; Hesai lidar + 12 cameras + 12 ultrasonic sensors; highway NOA (Navigate on Autopilot) beta; vision-only version in 2024
- **SS Platform**: Li Auto's in-house vehicle platform for L-series; supports both EREV and BEV variants

## Corporate Structure

Li Auto Inc. is incorporated in the Cayman Islands and listed on both NASDAQ (LI) and the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (2015.HK). The company operates through its Chinese subsidiary, **Beijing Cheche Automotive Technology Co., Ltd.** (北京车和家汽车有限公司).

- **Chairman & CEO**: Li Xiang (李想) — co-founder, 32.2% voting power
- **President**: Yanan Shen (沈亚楠)
- **CFO**: Tie Li (李铁)
- **R&D Centers**: Beijing (HQ), Changzhou (manufacturing), Shanghai (EV development)
- **Manufacturing**: Changzhou plant (established 2016, capacity ~200K/year); second plant in Shunyi District, Beijing (acquired from Beijing Hyundai, online 2022)

## Acquisitions and Strategic Investments

Li Auto has not executed major acquisitions. Its most notable strategic move was the **DiDi joint venture** attempt (2018) for low-speed EVs, which collapsed and prompted Li Auto's pivot to premium EREV SUVs. Li Auto did not invest in any other EV startups or technology companies during 2014-2025.

| Year | Event | Significance |

## Key Learnings

- **EREV as a strategic bridge**: Li Auto's choice to build extended-range hybrids — rather than pure BEVs like NIO and XPeng — proved commercially decisive. By eliminating range anxiety while keeping the electric driving experience, Li Auto captured customers who wanted EV benefits without EV anxiety. When pure BEV competition intensified in 2024-2025, Li Auto's EREV lineup maintained strong margins while pure BEV competitors faced pricing pressure.
- **Li Xiang's product intuition**: Li Xiang's background building Autohome gave him deep insight into what Chinese families actually wanted in a car — not just technology features, but practical family features: large screens for kids, a refrigerator, comfortable third-row seating for grandparents. This product-first approach, rather than technology-first, was a key differentiator.
- **Discontinuing Li One was painful but necessary**: The October 2022 Li One discontinuation hurt customer trust (some owners protested), but it was necessary to transition fully to the superior L-series platform. The L8/L7 replacement generated far more revenue and goodwill than the legacy Li One would have.
- **Profitability first, then EV expansion**: Li Auto deliberately chose to prove the business model (achieve profitability with EREV) before investing heavily in pure EV technology. This "prove it, then expand" approach was the inverse of NIO's strategy (build brand with premium BEV, worry about profitability later), and by 2024 Li Auto was clearly executing the more financially sustainable path.
- **The 2024-2025 BEV pivot is risky**: Entering the pure BEV market with i6/i8 in 2025 puts Li Auto in direct competition with Tesla, XPeng, NIO, and BYD in a segment where it has no EREV-style differentiation. The competitive and margin pressure from this pivot will be significant.
