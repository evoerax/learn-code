# DJI

DJI (大疆创新, Da Jiang Innovations) is a Chinese multinational technology company headquartered in Shenzhen, founded in **2006** by **Frank Wang** (王韬, Wang Tao). DJI is the world's dominant drone manufacturer, commanding an estimated **70–80% of the global civilian drone market** and over **90% of the US consumer drone market** before regulatory restrictions. The company's products range from consumer aerial platforms (Mavic, Air, Mini, Phantom series) to professional cinema drones (Inspire), agricultural drones (Agras), industrial inspection systems (Matrice), and the **Ronin** camera stabilization platform. DJI's technological moat rests on proprietary flight control systems, camera/gimbal integration, and AI-powered obstacle avoidance — all developed in-house. The company is privately held and has never disclosed full financials, but is widely estimated to generate **$5–8 billion in annual revenue**, making it one of China's most valuable private technology companies. In 2024, DJI remained the global drone market leader despite US government restrictions (NDAA ban, Entity List designation) that have limited its US government and military market access.

## Timeline

### 2006–2012: Founding and Early Aerial Platforms

- **2006**: **Frank Wang** (born 1980, studied electrical engineering at HKU and later East China University of Science and Technology) founded DJI in Shenzhen with **¥200,000 (~$26,000)** of personal savings and support from his former professor. Initial product: flight control systems (stabilization hardware) sold to other RC aircraft hobbyists. Wang's vision was to build **"the iPhone of the sky"** — a drone that ordinary consumers could fly.
- **2009**: DJI launched the **Flameswheel** — a multi-rotor aerial platform, one of the earliest consumer-grade helicopter drone platforms. This validated that there was a market for stabilized multi-rotor flight systems.
- **2010**: Introduced the **Wooкong** flight controller — a professional-grade stabilization system that became widely used in aerial photography and film production. This established DJI's reputation in the professional market before its consumer breakthrough.
- **2012**: Launched the **Zenmuse** gimbal — a 3-axis brushless gimbal for aerial cameras, solving the problem of shaky aerial footage. The Zenmuse was DJI's first integrated camera-gimbal product and demonstrated the company's ability to combine mechanical and software engineering.

### 2013–2016: Phantom Revolution and Consumer Takeoff

- **2013 (January)**: Released the **Phantom 1** — the world's first **ready-to-fly (RTF) quadcopter** with an integrated camera. Before the Phantom, flying a drone required assembling separate components (frame, motors, ESCs, flight controller, camera, gimbal). The Phantom packaged all of this into a single plug-and-fly product at ~$700, democratizing aerial photography. Within months, aerial photographers worldwide were using Phantoms.
- **2013 (October)**: Released the **Phantom 2** with an improved flight controller and longer range (~1km), plus compatibility with the **Zenmuse H3-3D** gimbal — the first complete professional aerial photography kit for under $2,000.
- **2014**: Released the **Phantom 2 Vision** — with a built-in HD camera and Wi-Fi streaming to a smartphone app. This cemented the "drone as flying camera" paradigm.
- **2015**: Released the **Phantom 3** — a major leap with a **4K camera, 3-axis gimbal, and DJI Go app** providing live HD feed. The Phantom 3 became the default tool for amateur and professional aerial photographers alike, generating massive social media content.
- **2016 (September)**: Released the **Mavic Pro** — a **foldable drone** that collapsed to the size of a water bottle, solving the portability problem that had limited drone adoption. The Mavic Pro used a new **OcuSync transmission system** for 7km range and 1080p live video. It was a direct response to the GoPro Karma (which launched and failed simultaneously) and established DJI's technological lead over all competitors. The Mavic Pro won the 2017 **TIME Magazine Best Invention** award.

### 2016–2019: Enterprise Expansion and Matrice Platform

- **2016**: Released the **Inspire 2** — a professional cinema drone capable of shooting 5.2K Apple ProRes RAW, targeting Hollywood film productions. The Inspire 2 became the standard for high-end aerial cinematography, used in major motion pictures and commercials.
- **2017**: Released the **Phantom 4 Pro** — with **obstacle sensing in 5 directions** and the **ActiveTrack** feature allowing the drone to autonomously follow a subject. This was a major step toward intelligent, autonomous flight.
- **2018**: Released the **Mavic 2 Pro and Mavic 2 Zoom** — with Hasselblad camera optics (in the Pro) and 2x optical zoom (in the Zoom), further expanding professional use cases. DJI at this point had effectively created and dominated the premium consumer drone market with no serious competitor.
- **2018**: Released the **Tello** — a mini drone developed with Intel technology, targeting educational markets and casual consumers at an entry-level price point (~$100). This extended DJI's reach to first-time drone users.
- **2019**: Released the **Mavic Mini** (249g) — deliberately designed to weigh under the 250g regulatory threshold in many countries (avoiding registration requirements). The Mavic Mini sold millions of units and brought drone photography to a mass consumer audience.

### 2020–2022: Agricultural Drones and Market Leadership

- **2020**: Released the **Agras T20** — an agricultural spray drone capable of carrying 20 liters of pesticide or fertilizer, targeting China's vast farmland. The Agras series became the world's best-selling agricultural drones, used for precision spraying on rice, wheat, and fruit orchards across China, Southeast Asia, and Latin America.
- **2021**: Released the **Mavic Air 2** — a mid-range consumer drone with 4K/60fps camera, 34-minute flight time, and **APAS 4.0** obstacle avoidance. The Air series became the volume best-seller in DJI's consumer lineup.
- **2021 (March)**: Frank Wang stepped down as CEO in a surprise leadership transition, replaced by **Ronan X** (徐金城, Xu Jincheng), a longtime DJI executive. Wang remained as Chairman. The transition was described as a natural succession as Wang shifted to long-term strategy.
- **2022**: Released the **Mavic 3** — with a **Hasselblad L-Format CMOS sensor (4/3)", 46-minute flight time, and O3+ transmission** with 15km range. The Mavic 3 set new benchmarks for image quality in consumer drones and remains DJI's flagship consumer model in updated variants.

### 2023–2025: US Restrictions and New Product Lines

- **2022 (December)**: The **US Department of Commerce added DJI to the Entity List**, restricting US companies from supplying DJI with American technology without a license. This followed years of Pentagon and government scrutiny over DJI drones used by US government agencies.
- **2023**: US Congress passed legislation (FY2024 NDAA) **banning the US military and federal agencies from using DJI drones** on national security grounds. DJI drones were already banned from US military use since 2017. Despite these restrictions, DJI maintained its dominant US consumer market position.
- **2023**: Released the **Air 3** — dual-camera system (wide + medium tele), 46 minutes flight time, and improved AI subject tracking. Also released the **Mini 4 Pro** — 249g with full 360° obstacle sensing and 4K/100fps slow motion.
- **2024**: Released the **DJI Neo 2** — an ultra-compact drone with AI-powered subject tracking and 4K camera, targeting social media content creators. Also released updated **Agricultural drones (T50/T25)** with larger spray tanks and AI-powered crop analysis.
- **2024**: Released the **Matrice 350 RTK** — an industrial drone platform with 55-minute flight time and 20km transmission range, for enterprise inspection, mapping, and public safety applications.
- **2025**: Released the **Air 3S** — featuring a 1-inch CMOS sensor primary camera with 3-axis gimbal stabilization, enhanced night-time shooting capabilities, and improved APAS (Advanced Pilot Assistance Systems). The **Avata 3** series continued DJI's FPV (first-person view) drone leadership for immersive flying.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Consumer Drones
- **Mavic series** (Mavic 3 Classic, Mavic 3 Pro): Flagship foldable consumer drones with Hasselblad cameras, 46-min flight time
- **Air series** (Air 3, Air 3S): Mid-range with dual-camera systems, 46-min flight
- **Mini series** (Mini 4 Pro, Mini 3): Ultra-light (249g), below regulatory registration thresholds
- **Phantom 4 Pro V2.0**: Professional-grade professional consumer drone with mechanical shutter
- **FPV (Avata 2)**: First-person view racing/aerial experience drone

### Professional/Cinema
- **Inspire 3**: Cinema-grade drone with full-frame 8K ProRes RAW, dual-operator control, and DL lens mount

### Enterprise & Industrial
- **Matrice 350 RTK**: Industrial inspection platform with 55-min flight, 20km transmission, heavy payload capacity
- **Matrice 300 RTK**: Public safety and surveying platform used by law enforcement and emergency services
- **Agras T50/T25**: Agricultural spray drones with AI-powered precision mapping and autonomous field spraying
- **Dock 2**: Autonomous drone-in-a-box solution for automated perimeter security and inspection

### Camera Stabilization (Ronin)
- **Ronin 4D**: Cinema-grade camera and gimbal system used in film production — integrates a full cinema camera with 4-axis stabilization
- **RS 3 Pro, RS 4 Pro**: Professional handheld camera gimbals
- **OM (Osmo Mobile) series**: Smartphone gimbals for content creators

## Corporate Governance

- **Founder & Chairman**: Frank Wang (王韬) — visionary engineer who started the company with ¥200,000 and led DJI to dominate the global drone market; stepped down as CEO in 2021 but remains Chairman
- **CEO**: Ronan X (徐金城, Xu Jincheng) — longtime DJI executive who led product development before becoming CEO in 2021
- **Headquarters**: Shenzhen, Guangdong, China
- **Status**: Privately held — DJI has never listed on any public exchange and does not disclose detailed financials
- **Estimated Revenue**: $5–8 billion annually (widely estimated based on market share and unit volumes)
- **Global Market Share**: ~70–80% of global civilian drones, ~90%+ of US consumer market (before NDAA ban)
