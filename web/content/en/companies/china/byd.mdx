# BYD

BYD Company Limited (比亚迪股份有限公司, Build Your Dreams) is a Chinese multinational technology company headquartered in Shenzhen, founded in **1995** by **Wang Chuanfu** (王传福). What began as a lithium-ion battery manufacturer for mobile phones grew into the world's largest electric vehicle (EV) manufacturer and one of the few companies with complete vertical integration across batteries, semiconductors, motors, electronics, and automotive. BYD sold **1.77 million EVs in 2023** (including plug-in hybrids) and surpassed **Tesla as the world's largest BEV (battery electric vehicle) manufacturer by volume in Q4 2024**, achieving global sales of over 4 million vehicles including hybrids in 2025. With revenue reaching **¥755.4 billion (~$104 billion)** in FY2024, BYD is a defining company of China's transition to green mobility, driven by its proprietary **Blade Battery** (LFP chemistry), **DM-i hybrid technology**, and in-house semiconductor capabilities through **BYD Semiconductor**.

## Timeline

### 1995–2003: Battery Origins and Rapid Growth

- **1995**: BYD founded by **Wang Chuanfu** (age 29, formerly a professor of metallurgy at Beijing University of Science and Technology) with ¥2.5 million (~$300,000) in seed capital in Shenzhen. Initial focus: manufacturing **lithium-ion batteries** for mobile phones at one-fifth the cost of Japanese competitors (Sony, Sanyo). Wang's background in battery chemistry and microelectronics enabled rapid R&D.
- **1997**: BYD became the **world's second-largest supplier of lithium-ion batteries for mobile phones**, behind Sony but ahead of Sanyo, by mastering production processes that Japanese companies kept proprietary. Annual production reached 15 million batteries.
- **2000**: BYD developed its own **lithium-polymer battery technology**, staying ahead of the curve on mobile power trends.
- **2002 (July)**: BYD listed on the **Hong Kong Stock Exchange** (HKEX: 1211.HK), raising $160 million. The IPO provided capital for the auto industry pivot.
- **2003 (January)**: BYD acquired **Qinchuan Machinery** (秦川机械), a small state-owned automobile manufacturer in Xi'an, for ¥270 million (~$33 million). This gave BYD an auto production license — shocking Western analysts who doubted a battery company could build cars. The first BYD car, the F3, launched in 2005.

### 2004–2008: Auto Entry and Warren Buffett's Bet

- **2005**: Launched **BYD F3** — a compact sedan外观 strongly inspired by Toyota Corolla. The F3 became China's best-selling sedan within two years at roughly half the price of comparable imports.
- **2007**: BYD F3 achieved **100,000 unit sales** — validating the company's automotive ambitions. BYD also expanded into electric buses (K9) and electric taxis (e6).
- **2008 (September)**: **Warren Buffett** (via Berkshire Hathaway's MidAmerican Energy Holdings) invested **$230 million for a 10% stake** in BYD — the American icon endorsing Wang Chuanfu's vision. This was remarkable because Buffett rarely invested outside the US and never in a Chinese automotive company. The investment drove global attention to Chinese EVs before the market existed.
- **2008**: BYD launched the **F3DM** — the world's first **mass-produced plug-in hybrid vehicle** (before the Chevrolet Volt), available to Chinese consumers at ~¥150,000. Commercial sales to individuals began in 2008 (governments purchased fleet e6s earlier).

### 2009–2015: Blade Battery and Global Ambitions

- **2009**: BYD became China's **best-selling compact car brand**, with F3 sales exceeding 290,000 units in a single year.
- **2010**: Launched **Denza** — a premium EV brand joint venture with **Daimler AG** (Mercedes-Benz), reflecting BYD's ambitions to move upmarket. The Denza brand survived multiple restructurings and was eventually dissolved in 2021.
- **2011**: BYD began mass production of **electric buses (K9)** in Shenzhen — the city fleet became the world's largest electric bus fleet, demonstrating EV viability at scale.
- **2016**: Launched the **Blade Battery** — a lithium-iron phosphate (LFP) battery pack design where cells are arranged in long, blade-like arrays, dramatically improving thermal management and safety compared to traditional lithium-ion packs. The Blade Battery solved the industry-wide concern about battery fires in EVs.
- **2017**: BYD began **external sales of Blade Batteries** to other EV manufacturers, including **Tesla** (which used BYD LFP batteries in some Model 3 Standard Range Plus versions starting in 2022) and **Toyota** (for the bZ3 electric sedan). This established BYD as a tier-1 automotive supplier beyond its own vehicles.

### 2018–2022: DM-i Hybrid Breakthrough and Market Leadership

- **2018**: BYD launched **e-platform 2.0** — a modular platform for EVs, integrating the third-generation DM (Dual Mode) hybrid system. The company also started its ** autonomous driving** research and development, later launching the **DiPilot** system.
- **2020 (July)**: Launched the **Han EV** — a premium electric sedan positioned as the "Model S of China" with range up to 605km (NEDC), 0-100 km/h in 3.9 seconds, and the Blade Battery as standard. The Han became China's best-selling EV above ¥200,000 in its first year, selling over 40,000 units.
- **2021 (January)**: Launched the **DM-i hybrid system** (Dual Motor intelligent hybrid) — a revolutionary architecture using an **electric motor as the primary propulsion** with a small gasoline engine as a range extender, achieving fuel consumption as low as 3.8L/100km and 1,200km+ total range. The DM-i made hybrids more affordable than ever and triggered a crisis for traditional automakers.
- **2021**: Launched the **秦 Plus DM-i** (Qin Plus) — bringing DM-i technology to the mass market at ~¥110,000, cheaper than comparable gasoline cars in China. Waiting lists stretched to 5+ months due to demand.
- **2022 (March)**: **Stopped production of pure internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles**, becoming the first major automaker to go **fully electric or hybrid globally**. All new models from this point were NEV (New Energy Vehicle) variants.

### 2023–2025: Global Expansion and New Brands

- **2022**: Launched the **Seal** (海豹) — an electric sedan competing with Tesla Model 3, featuring the CTB (Cell-to-Body) battery integration technology. Also launched the **Dolphin** (海豚) — a compact urban EV, and **Atto 3** (元 Plus) — a compact SUV.
- **2023**: Total sales reached **1.77 million EVs** (including 760,000 pure BEVs and 1.01 million plug-in hybrids). BYD's overseas sales reached 242,000 units, up 334% YoY, with presence in **70+ countries**.
- **2024**: BYD's brand portfolio expanded with multiple new models and sub-brands. **Yangwang** (仰望) launched a luxury brand with the U8 off-road SUV (¥1.09 million) featuring independent four-motor platform technology and "floating car" capability. **Fang Cheng Bao** (方程豹) launched for personalized, performance-oriented EVs.
- **2024 (Q4)**: BYD overtook **Tesla** as the **world's largest BEV manufacturer by volume** (although Tesla's vehicles are higher-priced). BYD sold 594,000 BEVs vs Tesla's ~500,000 in Q4 2024.
- **2024 (October)**: BYD reached **10 million NEVs produced** — the first Chinese automaker to hit this milestone.
- **2025**: BYD launched the **DiSus** intelligent chassis system and expanded the **e-Platform 3.0 Evo** architecture. Revenue for FY2024 reached **¥755.4 billion (~$104 billion)**. The company employs **700,000+ people** globally, making it one of China's largest employers. BYD's **Atto 3, Dolphin, and Seal** are sold in Europe, Southeast Asia, Latin America, and Australia. The company operates ** factories in Brazil, Hungary (Europe), Thailand, and Uzbekistan**.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Automotive
- **Passenger EVs**: Han, Seal, Dolphin, Atto 3 (Yuan Plus), Tang, Song Plus, Qin Plus, Destroyer
- **Plug-in Hybrids (DM-i)**: Qin Plus DM-i, Song Pro DM-i, Seal 06 DM-i — dominant in China's PHEV market
- **Luxury (Yangwang)**: U8 (off-road SUV), U9 (electric supercar 0-100 km/h in 2 seconds)
- **Performance (Fang Cheng Bao)**: Baо 5, Baо 3 — hardcore off-road and sports models
- **Commercial Vehicles**: K9 electric bus (operates globally in 70+ cities), Q1 terminal truck, electric forklifts
- **New brands**: Denza (historical, with Daimler), Galaxy (mainstream hybrid/electric)

### Battery & Energy
- **Blade Battery (LFP)**: Safety-focused lithium iron phosphate battery with blade form factor; sold to Tesla (Model 3 in China), Toyota bZ3, and other OEMs
- **BYD Semiconductor**: IGBT and SiC power semiconductors for EVs — BYD is China's largest domestic chip producer for vehicles
- **Energy Storage Systems (ESS)**: Utility-scale battery storage for grid stabilization

### Mobile Components
- **Smartphone parts**: Printed circuit boards, camera modules, and assembly for Huawei, Xiaomi, Samsung (historically)

## Corporate Governance

- **Chairman & President**: Wang Chuanfu (王传福) — the founder, holds ~17% stake, regarded as a visionary engineer and one of China's most respected entrepreneurs
- **Executive Director & Co-CEO**: Wang Lin (王 Linn) — daughter of Wang Chuanfu, leads operations
- **Headquarters**: Shenzhen, Guangdong, China
- **Listed**: HKEX (1211.HK, since 2002), Shenzhen Stock Exchange (002594.SZ, since 2011)
- **Employees**: ~700,000 globally (2025)
- **Foreign ownership**: Berkshire Hathaway 10% (since 2008), remaining ~90% primarily held by Chinese institutions and Wang family
