# Xiaomi

Xiaomi Corporation (小米集团, HKEX: 1810) is a Chinese multinational consumer electronics and smart hardware company founded in 2010 by Lei Jun and headquartered in Beijing. Known for its "efficient innovation" philosophy and aggressive pricing strategy, Xiaomi disrupted the smartphone market by offering flagship-specification phones at near-cost prices — a model that transformed the industry globally. The company sold its first smartphone in 2011 and by Q3 2021 was the world's #2 smartphone brand by shipments, eventually reaching #3 globally. Xiaomi's business model combines hardware sales (smartphones, laptops, TVs, wearables, smart home devices) with an internet services ecosystem (MIUI OS, app store, advertising, gaming, fintech) and an AIoT (AI+IoT) platform connecting 190+ million smart devices. In 2021, Xiaomi announced a $10 billion electric vehicle program, launching the **SU7** electric sedan in 2024 — competing directly with Tesla and BYD in China's massive EV market. With $38 billion FY2024 revenue and operations spanning India (where it was #1 smartphone brand 2018-2021), Southeast Asia, Europe, and Latin America, Xiaomi is one of the world's most globally diversified Chinese consumer electronics brands. Lei Jun remains CEO and holds ~24% of the company.

## Timeline

### 2010–2013: Founding and Explosive Growth

- **April 6, 2010**: Xiaomi founded by **Lei Jun** (CEO, former Kingsoft CEO and金山软件创始人) along with seven co-founders in Beijing. Initial capital: $41 million. Company name "Xiaomi" (小米 = "small rice") was chosen as a humble, approachable name contrasting with grand tech company names.
- **August 16, 2010**: MIUI ROM officially launched — a custom Android-based ROM for smartphones. Built by a small team, MIUI was notable for its weekly update cycle and deep customization — gaining a passionate developer community before any hardware existed.
- **September 2011**: **Xiaomi Phone 1** (Mi 1) launched — sold exclusively online, priced at ¥1,999 (~$300), powered by a dual-core 1.5GHz processor with specs matching Samsung Galaxy S2 at half the price. 300,000 units sold in first day.
- **August 2012**: **Mi 2** launched — won "Best Phone of the Year" from many tech publications. The combination of Snapdragon processor, 2GB RAM, and MIUI at ¥1,999 made it a cult product.
- **2013**: Xiaomi expanded beyond smartphones with the **Mi Router** — first of many IoT/smart home products. Lei Jun's "铁人三项" (Iron Triangle) strategy was formalized: hardware + internet services + e-commerce.

### 2014–2016: Global Expansion and IPO Path

- **2014**: Xiaomi became **China's #1 smartphone brand** by shipments — reaching this milestone in just 4 years, the fastest in smartphone history. Raised $1.1 billion in funding at $46 billion valuation.
- **2014**: Xiaomi launched international expansion — entered **India**, **Singapore**, **Malaysia**, **Indonesia**, and **Philippines**. India would become its largest international market.
- **July 2014**: **Mi 4** launched — 5-inch 1080p display, 3GB RAM, Snapdragon 801 — priced at ¥1,999. Became one of China's best-selling phones.
- **2015**: Xiaomi overtook Samsung in **India** to become the #1 smartphone brand — driven by online sales and aggressive pricing (Redmi Note series).
- **2015**: Launched **Mi Air Purifier** — first major IoT home appliance. The 895 yuan ($145) air purifier sold out immediately and established Xiaomi's smart home ecosystem strategy.
- **2016**: Xiaomi launched **Mi Pay** (fintech) — competing with Alipay and WeChat Pay in mobile payments.
- **2016**: Lei Jun took direct control of hardware development — "小米手机部" reorganization after a difficult year of supply chain issues and declining market share (from 15% to 8% in China).

### 2017–2020: Recovery and Stock Market Listing

- **2017**: Xiaomi recovered strongly — launched **Mi 6** and **Mi Mix** (bezel-less concept phone). Mi Mix pioneered the all-screen smartphone trend.
- **2017**: Xiaomi surpassed $15 billion in revenue — Lei Jun set a goal of reaching 100 billion yuan (~$15B) revenue by 2020.
- **February 2017**: Xiaomi opened first **Mi Store** in India — beginning the offline retail expansion.
- **2017**: Xiaomi became the world's largest **wearable fitness band** manufacturer — Mi Band sold 50M+ units globally.
- **2018**: Xiaomi overtook Samsung to become **India's #1 smartphone brand** — maintained leadership through 2021.
- **July 9, 2018**: Xiaomi listed on **HKEX** at HK$17/share, raising $4.7 billion in the largest tech IPO in Hong Kong since 2014. Valuation at IPO: ~$54 billion.
- **2019**: Xiaomi launched **Mi 9** and **Redmi K20** — flagship and sub-flagship strategy. Redmi became independent brand in 2019 (led by Lu Weibing).
- **2019**: Xiaomi partnered with **美的 (Midea)** for smart home appliances — creating a competing ecosystem to Alibaba's AliGenie and Baidu's DuerOS.
- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic — Xiaomi's online sales model proved more resilient than competitors heavily dependent on offline retail.

### 2021–2023: Smartphone Peak and EV Entry

- **February 2021**: Xiaomi announced **$10 billion electric vehicle program** — Lei Jun personally leading the new EV division, committing 10 years and $10 billion to the initiative. This was Xiaomi's most ambitious diversification since founding.
- **March 2021**: Xiaomi surpassed **Apple** to become the world's **#2 smartphone brand** by shipments — a historic milestone for a Chinese brand.
- **March 2021**: US Department of Defense added Xiaomi to **Entity List** (Chinese military company designation) — a move that caused stock to fall 10%. Xiaomi challenged the designation in court and was removed from the list in May 2021 after proving no military ties.
- **2021**: Xiaomi launched **Xiaomi 11** series with Snapdragon 888 — flagship phones in partnership with Qualcomm.
- **2022**: Xiaomi launched **12** series and began SU7 EV testing — spy shots of SU7 prototypes circulated widely.
- **2022**: Global smartphone market declined; Xiaomi slipped to #3 globally behind Samsung and Apple.

### 2024–2025: SU7 EV Launch and New Era

- **March 28, 2024**: **Xiaomi SU7** electric sedan officially launched — three variants: SU7 (标准版, $29,900), SU7 Pro ($34,200), SU7 Max ($41,300). The SU7 Max features 800V architecture, 2.78s 0-100km/h acceleration, and 800km CLTC range.
- **April 2024**: SU7 received 88,898 orders within 24 hours of launch — exceeding even Tesla's China launch numbers. Xiaomi's first car was widely considered a design and engineering success.
- **May 2024**: Xiaomi delivered first SU7 vehicles — Beijing ceremony with Lei Jun personally handing over keys to early buyers.
- **June 2024**: Xiaomi opened flagship stores in shopping malls — beginning non-traditional auto dealership model.
- **2024**: Xiaomi's EV business lost approximately $9,200 per vehicle in initial production — standard for new EV entrants. The company guided that margins would improve significantly by 2026 as scale increases.
- **October 2024**: Xiaomi launched **SU7 Ultra** prototype at the Nürburgring — targeting the world's fastest four-door electric sedan.
- **2024**: Xiaomi maintained **#3 global smartphone market share** (14%) behind Samsung (19%) and Apple (18%). In India, Xiaomi remained top 3.
- **2025**: Xiaomi aims to deliver **100,000 SU7 vehicles** — with 20,000+ already delivered by early 2025.
- **2025**: Xiaomi EV business revenue exceeded $1 billion annualized run rate.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Smartphones
- **Xiaomi (Mi) series** — flagship line (Xiaomi 14, 15 series) with premium specs at mid-range prices
- **Redmi series** — sub-flagship/performance line; Redmi Note is India's best-selling smartphone line
- **POCO** — gaming/performance sub-brand targeting young consumers globally
- **Civi** — design-focused line targeting women/young adults in China

### AIoT and Smart Home
- **Mi Band** (fitness wearables) — world's #1 fitness band by volume
- **Mi Air Purifier, Mi Robot Vacuum, Mi Smart Speaker** — IoT home appliances
- **Xiaomi TV** — world's #5 TV manufacturer; Mi TV sells in 70+ countries
- **Xiaomi Smart Home Platform** — 190M+ connected IoT devices (excluding smartphones)
- **Mijia** — smart home appliances brand

### Electric Vehicles
- **SU7** — electric sedan (standard, Pro, Max variants); 2.78s 0-100, 800km range (Max)
- **SU7 Ultra** — high-performance prototype targeting Nürburgring record
- **Future models** — rumored SUV (MX11) launching 2025

### Internet Services
- **MIUI** — Android-based OS with 500M+ monthly active users
- **Mi App Store** — China's #3 app distribution platform
- **Xiaomi Finance (小米金融)** — fintech services (loan facilitation, insurance)
- **Xiaomi Gaming** — Mi Game Center, mobile gaming platform
- **Xiaomi Video, Mi Music** — content services with subscription models

## Corporate Structure

- **Founder, CEO & Chairman**: Lei Jun (owns ~24% of Xiaomi, 70%+ voting control via dual-class shares)
- **President**: Lu Weibing (head of Redmi brand and smartphone division)
- **CFO**: Alain Lam (from Goldman Sachs)
- **Headquarters**: Xiaomi Campus, Haidian District, Beijing
- **Listed**: HKEX (1810.HK)
- **Employees**: ~35,000 (2024); EV division ~7,000
- **R&D**: 17 global R&D centers (Beijing, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Wuhan, Shanghai, and internationally)
- **Manufacturing**: Internally designed but manufactured by Foxconn and BYD for smartphones; Xiaomi's own EV factory in Beijing

## Strategic Position

Xiaomi's "铁人三项" (Iron Triangle) of hardware + internet services + e-commerce was revolutionary — the company sells hardware at near-cost to acquire users, then monetizes through MIUI services, advertising, gaming, and fintech. This mirrors Amazon's Prime model but for the Chinese market. The challenge is that Chinese government regulation of pre-installed apps, advertising, and fintech has pressured internet services margins. Xiaomi's EV entry ($10B, 10-year commitment) is its most consequential strategic decision — competing against Tesla, BYD, and Huawei in China's EV market while the smartphone market matures. Lei Jun personally driving EV (after founding Xiaomi at 40) is seen as his "final entrepreneurial chapter."
