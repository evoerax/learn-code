# NetEase

NetEase (网易) is one of China's largest internet companies and a global leader in online gaming, founded in 1997 by Ding Lei (William Ding), who remains the company's CEO and controlling shareholder. Headquartered in Hangzhou, NetEase operates China's most enduring and profitable online game franchises — **Fantasy Westward Journey** (大话西游) and **Westward Journey Online** (梦幻西游) — which have been generating billions of yuan in annual revenue for over 20 years, making them among the longest-running and most profitable MMORPG franchises globally, comparable in longevity to World of Warcraft. Beyond gaming, NetEase operates Youdao (education technology), NetEase Cloud Music (streaming audio), and has diversified into content production, email services, and嚴選 (Yanxuan) e-commerce (divested in 2023). NetEase is listed on NASDAQ (NTES) and generated approximately **¥96.5B (~$13.7B)** in FY2024 revenue, with a market cap of approximately **$40-50B** as of 2025.

## Development Timeline

### 1997–2003: Founding, Email Portal, and NASDAQ IPO

NetEase was founded on June 29, 1997 in Hangzhou by **Ding Lei** (then 26 years old), initially as an online community and email service. Ding Lei had previously worked at a local government telecom bureau before founding the company with ¥500,000 in capital. The company's early days were focused on a free email service (yeah.net) and community forums — competing with Sina and Sohu for portal traffic.

The company went public on **June 30, 2000** on NASDAQ at $15.50/share, raising approximately $65 million. At the time of IPO, NetEase was one of the first Chinese internet companies to list on a US exchange. The dot-com crash shortly after listing devastated NetEase's stock — the share price fell from $53 to under $1 within two years, and there were reports that Ding Lei considered selling the company.

### 2001–2005: Survival, Gaming Pivot, and First Major Profit

Facing the dot-com bust and intense competition from portals, NetEase made a critical pivot into **online gaming**. In 2001, the company licensed **Lineage** (天堂) from韩国 NCSoft, which became China's most popular MMORPG for several years. More importantly, in 2002, NetEase began development of **Fantasy Westward Journey** (大话西游), which launched in 2002 and became one of China's most enduring and profitable games.

The decision to develop proprietary games rather than just license foreign titles proved transformative. **Westward Journey Online** (梦幻西游), launched in 2004, became a cultural phenomenon in China. Set in a fantasy interpretation of the Journey to the West (西游记) storyline, the game attracted millions of players and is estimated to have generated over **$1.5 billion in cumulative revenue** over its lifetime. Unlike many games that fade after a few years, Westward Journey Online has maintained a devoted player base for over two decades through continuous updates and expansion packs.

By 2003, NetEase was profitable, and Ding Lei had become China's youngest billionaire at age 32. In 2004, NetEase launched **Fantasy Westward Journey 2** (大话西游2), extending the franchise.

### 2005–2010: Diversification into Education and Music

NetEase used its gaming profits to diversify into adjacent businesses:

- **Youdao (有道):** Launched in 2006 as a language learning and translation tool; later expanded to a full education technology platform. Youdao's dictionary and translation products became widely used in China. Youdao was eventually spun off and listed on NYSE in 2019 as **DAO** (youdao.com IPO raised ~$125M).

- **NetEase Cloud Music (网易云音乐):** Launched in 2013, NetEase's music streaming service differentiated itself through its social features (song comment sections that became famous for their emotional and literary quality), algorithm-driven recommendations, and a catalog that initially included many indie and foreign songs not available on competitors. By 2024, it had over **200 million users** and ~90 million paying subscribers, making it China's #2 music streaming platform after Tencent Music Entertainment (which controls QQ Music and Kugou).

- **Yanxuan (网易严选):** Launched in 2016 as a direct-to-consumer e-commerce platform selling private-label products (ODM model). Yanxuan sold everything from household goods to clothing, positioning itself as a quality-focused, middle-class alternative to Taobao. The business grew rapidly but was eventually divested in 2023 amid profitability concerns.

### 2010–2016: International Game Licensing and Mobile Gaming

NetEase deepened its gaming business while becoming one of the first Chinese companies to successfully license and distribute Western games:

- **World of Warcraft (China):** In 2008, NetEase took over operations of Blizzard Entertainment's World of Warcraft in China from previous operator Source: The company secured rights to distribute Blizzard games in China, including Diablo III (2012), Hearthstone (2014), and Overwatch (2016). The World of Warcraft licensing relationship was extremely lucrative — at peak, World of Warcraft China generated hundreds of millions of dollars in annual revenue for NetEase as a percentage of the game's global operations.

- **Mobile gaming pivot:** As the Chinese gaming market shifted to mobile, NetEase launched numerous mobile titles. **Fantasy Westward Journey Mobile** (大话西游手游) and **Westward Journey Mobile** (梦幻西游手游) launched in 2015-2016 and became massive revenue generators, extending the PC franchise success to mobile. Mobile games came to represent approximately 60-70% of NetEase's gaming revenue by 2018.

- **Other notable games:** Identity V (第五人格, a horror survival game launched 2018), Onmyoji (阴阳师, 2016) — a Japanese folklore-themed RPG that became one of NetEase's most successful original titles and achieved significant popularity in Japan.

### 2017–2020: Growth, Regulatory Headwinds, and Stock Recovery

NetEase's stock reached an all-time high in 2017 as gaming revenue continued to surge. However, the Chinese government's 2018-2019 regulatory crackdown on the gaming industry (freezing new game approvals for months in 2018) and restrictions on gaming time for minors in 2019 created significant headwinds. NetEase's stock fell approximately 35% from its 2017 peak.

Despite the regulatory environment, NetEase continued to invest in game development. **Onmyoji** (阴阳师) became a cultural phenomenon in 2016-2017, driving significant revenue and expanding NetEase's reputation as a game developer beyond its MMORPG franchises. The game was also localized for Japan, where it achieved top-10 app store rankings.

**COVID-19 tailwind (2020):** The pandemic increased gaming consumption globally, and NetEase benefited significantly. FY2020 revenue reached ¥73.6B (+24%), with net income of ¥12.1B. The company also benefited from its expansion of international operations.

### 2020–2023: Blizzard Split, International Expansion, and New Growth Vectors

The most significant disruption was the termination of the **Blizzard Entertainment** partnership in early 2023. Blizzard announced in January 2023 that it would not renew its licensing agreement with NetEase when it expired in January 2023, ending a 14-year partnership that covered World of Warcraft, Hearthstone, Overwatch, Diablo III, and StarCraft II in China. The split was due to a contract dispute over intellectual property rights and revenue sharing — NetEase had been developing mobile versions of Blizzard games (including a mobile Diablo), which became a point of contention. The separation cost NetEase approximately **$750M** in annual revenue and created an opportunity for competitors to capture displaced Blizzard players.

In response, NetEase accelerated its **self-developed international titles** and **PC/console game development**. The company opened game studios in Montreal, Montreal, Seattle, and Tokyo, hiring veteran game developers from Activision, EA, and Rockstar. In 2023, NetEase announced a $500M investment in Diablo immortal follow-up development and multiple new game projects.

NetEase also announced plans to spin off **Youdao** (education technology) separately, which had become a profitable education hardware and software business with products including Youdao Dictionary Pen, Youdao Smart Learning Pad, and online courses. Youdao was listed on NYSE in October 2019.

### 2023–2025: Post-Blizzard Recovery and AI Integration

After the Blizzard split, NetEase focused on:

- **Fantasy Westward Journey series** continuing to be the cash cow, with new mobile and PC releases maintaining the franchises' revenue
- **New game pipeline:** Titles including **Naruto: Mobile** (if licensed), **Identity V** (global), and new original titles in RPG and competitive gaming genres
- **International expansion:** NetEase's overseas game revenue grew to approximately 20% of gaming revenue in 2024, targeting markets in Southeast Asia, Japan, and the US. Titles like **Knives Out** (海外版《荒野行动》) and **Lost Light** achieved millions of downloads in Western markets.
- **AI integration:** In 2024, NetEase integrated generative AI into its game development pipeline, including AI NPCs (non-player characters with generative dialogue), AI-powered game testing, and AI-generated art assets. NetEase's game studios also experimented with AI-generated game levels.

FY2024 revenue reached **¥96.5B** (~$13.7B), +16% YoY, with net income of approximately ¥25B. The stock recovered strongly from its 2022 lows, with market cap reaching $40-50B by 2025. Key growth drivers were mobile game expansions, Yanxuan divestiture, and international game publishing.

## Product Lines

### Online Games (Core Business)

- **Fantasy Westward Journey (大话西游):** MMORPG franchise (PC and mobile), generating billions annually since 2002. Multiple editions including Fantasy Westward Journey 2, Westward Journey Online 3, and various mobile versions.
- **Westward Journey Online (梦幻西游):** The most profitable Chinese MMORPG ever; set in Journey to the West universe; has maintained paying player base for 20+ years through continuous content updates.
- **Onmyoji (阴阳师):** Japanese yokai (supernatural creature)-themed RPG; one of NetEase's most successful original IP; strong in China and Japan.
- **Identity V (第五人格):** Horror survival game; global success with millions of downloads; distinct from typical Chinese mobile games in art style and gameplay.
- **Other games:** Minecraft China version (from Microsoft/Mojang), Diablo Immortal (until 2023), and various casual/midcore titles.

### Education (Youdao)

- **Youdao Dictionary Pen (有道词典笔):** Hardware device for scanning and translating text; sold millions of units; key product bridging hardware and education.
- **Youdao Smart Learning Pad:** Tablet for K-12 education with AI-powered learning features.
- **Youdao Premium Courses:** Online language and test preparation courses.
- **Youdao Translation:** AI-powered translation services and APIs.

### Cloud Music

- **NetEase Cloud Music (网易云音乐):** Music streaming with ~200M users, ~90M paying subscribers; known for its emotionally resonant song comment sections and strong indie/foreign catalog; China's #2 music platform.

### Former / Divested

- **Yanxuan (网易严选):** ODM e-commerce platform sold goods directly from manufacturers; divested in 2023.
- **Weibo-like news portal:** NetEase News app, a significant news aggregation platform (though smaller than Tencent's and ByteDance's).

## Competitive Landscape

- **vs. Tencent Games:** NetEase is China's #2 game company by revenue after Tencent. Tencent controls League of Legends (Riot), Honor of Kings, PUBG Mobile, and has stakes in many studios. NetEase's MMORPG franchises are more durable than Tencent's MOBA/Battle Royale titles, but Tencent's scale and distribution advantages are enormous.
- **vs. ByteDance Gaming:** ByteDance's gaming division (Nuverse) is a growing competitor, but ByteDance's gaming strategy has been inconsistent.
- **Music Streaming:** NetEase Cloud Music (#2) vs. Tencent Music Entertainment (#1 with QQ Music, Kugou, Kuwo). The competition is primarily over licensing deals with major labels — Tencent has historically had better access to major Western label catalogs.
- **Education:** Youdao competes with Alibaba's DingTalk Education, Tencent's K-12 platforms, and specialized EdTech companies.

## Key Learnings

- NetEase's Westward Journey franchises are among the most remarkable examples of game longevity globally — generating billions in annual revenue for 20+ years through continuous content updates, compared to most Western games that peak and decline within 2-3 years. The key was treating the games as live services with constant content drops rather than finite products.
- The 2023 Blizzard split showed the risk of building a business around licensed IP — even 14 years of partnership and billions in revenue didn't create a stable long-term arrangement. NetEase's response (accelerating first-party IP development and international expansion) was the correct strategic reaction, but the ~$750M revenue hole took time to fill.
- Ding Lei's decision to control ~45% of NetEase while remaining CEO for 27+ years is highly unusual in Chinese tech — most founder-CEOs eventually cede control or take outside investment. His ownership structure means NetEase's strategy is highly aligned with his personal risk tolerance and interests, for better or worse.
- NetEase Cloud Music's success against Tencent Music's dominance was driven by differentia on user community — the emotional "song comments" became a cultural phenomenon and social feature that QQ Music couldn't easily replicate, showing that community features can be a sustainable competitive moat even against larger, better-resourced competitors.
