# DeepSeek

DeepSeek AI (深度求索) is a Chinese artificial intelligence company headquartered in Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province, founded in 2023 with the stated mission of pursuing Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). The company originated from **High-Flyer** (幻方量化), a quantitative hedge fund established in 2015 by Zhejiang University graduates that accumulated significant GPU computing resources. DeepSeek emerged as a separate AI research organization when High-Flyer spun out its large language model research division. The company's January 2025 release of **DeepSeek-R1** sent shockwaves through the global AI industry — demonstrating performance comparable to OpenAI's GPT-4o and o1 at a fraction of the training cost, and challenging assumptions about China's AI capabilities relative to US companies. DeepSeek's models are open-source and available through APIs, with the company claiming it trained its V3 model for approximately **$6 million** compared to the estimated $100 million+ for comparable OpenAI models.

## Origins: High-Flyer Quantitative Hedge Fund

### 2015–2023: High-Flyer and Accumulated Computing Power

- **2015**: **High-Flyer (High-Flyer Quant)** founded by graduates from Zhejiang University, including **Peng Zheng** and **白天明** (Bai Tianming). The fund specialized in algorithmic trading using AI and machine learning.
- High-Flyer grew to become one of China's largest quantitative hedge funds, managing tens of billions of RMB in assets under management.
- By 2023, High-Flyer had accumulated **10,000+ Nvidia A100/H100 GPUs** — an enormous compute infrastructure that put it in a rare position among Chinese AI organizations. This was possible because Chinese quant funds had been major buyers of Nvidia GPUs before the 2022 US export restrictions.
- **2023**: High-Flyer decided to spin out its LLM research team as an independent company, creating **DeepSeek AI** to focus specifically on frontier AI research.

## 2024: First Model Releases

- **January 2024**: DeepSeek released its first LLM, establishing the company's presence in the open-source AI community.
- **February 2024**: Released **DeepSeekMath** — a specialized mathematical reasoning model that achieved state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks.
- **June 2024**: Released **DeepSeek-Coder-V2**, an open-source code generation model that rivaled GPT-4 Turbo on coding benchmarks while being significantly more efficient.
- These early releases established DeepSeek as a serious research organization, though still relatively unknown outside AI practitioner circles.

## November 2024: R1-Lite-Preview and Growing Attention

- **November 2024**: Released **DeepSeek-R1-Lite-Preview**, a reasoning model demonstrating chain-of-thought capabilities comparable to OpenAI's o1-preview. The model was made available through DeepSeek's API and web interface, gaining significant attention from the AI research community.
- Early evaluations showed R1-Lite-Preview matched or exceeded OpenAI's o1 on several reasoning benchmarks.

## December 2024: DeepSeek-V3 Release

- **December 2024**: Released **DeepSeek-V3**, a 671-billion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) language model that shocked the AI industry.
- **Training cost**: DeepSeek claimed V3 was trained for approximately **$6 million** in compute costs (using 2,000 H800 GPUs over ~2 months), compared to estimates of $100+ million for GPT-4 class models trained by OpenAI/Google.
- **Architecture**: DeepSeek-V3 used an innovative MoE architecture with 671B total parameters but only 37B active parameters per token, dramatically reducing inference costs while maintaining high quality.
- **Key innovations**: DeepSeek-V3 introduced **Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA)** and **DeepSeekMoE** architecture, along with **auxiliary-loss-free** load balancing — technical advances that improved training stability and efficiency.
- **Performance**: On benchmarks, V3 matched or exceeded GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet on most tasks, while being significantly cheaper to run.
- **Hardware**: Trained on **Nvidia H800** chips (the export-controlled version of the H100, limited to 800GB/s interconnect vs 900GB/s for H100) — demonstrating that frontier AI could be developed despite US chip restrictions.

## January 2025: DeepSeek-R1 — Industry Disruption

- **January 20, 2025**: Released **DeepSeek-R1**, a reasoning model that matched the performance of OpenAI's o1 on reasoning, math, and coding benchmarks.
- **Key differentiator**: R1 was trained using **Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)** — a reinforcement learning technique DeepSeek developed that does not require supervised fine-tuning, only rule-based rewards for accuracy and formatting.
- **DeepSeek-R1-Zero**: A notable variant trained purely with RL (no SFT), demonstrating that reasoning capabilities can emerge from pure reinforcement learning — a significant research finding that challenged conventional wisdom about LLM training pipelines.
- **Benchmark results**: R1 achieved **79.8% on MATH-A** (vs 77.3% for OpenAI o1), **96.3% on AIME** (vs 74.6% for o1), and **2024 Elo on LiveCodeBench** (vs 1871 for o1).
- **Open-source**: DeepSeek released R1 under an open license, along with distilled 1.5B–70B parameter versions (R1-Distill) that could run on consumer hardware.
- **Market impact**: DeepSeek-R1's release caused a **$600+ billion one-day decline in Nvidia's stock market cap** (January 27, 2025) as investors questioned whether frontier AI training required far less compute than previously assumed.

## May 2025: R1-0528 and Continued Innovation

- **May 2025**: Released **R1-0528**, an updated version of R1 with improved reasoning and reduced hallucination rates.

## August 2025: DeepSeek-V3.1

- **August 2025**: Published **DeepSeek-V3.1**, a further refined version of V3 with improved multilingual capabilities and longer context windows.

## Key Technologies and Innovations

### Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA)
MLA is DeepSeek's novel attention mechanism that reduces the KV cache memory requirement during inference, enabling longer context windows and lower memory costs compared to standard multi-head attention.

### DeepSeekMoE Architecture
Mixture-of-Experts architecture that activates only a fraction of the model's parameters per token, dramatically reducing inference cost while maintaining model quality.

### Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)
DeepSeek's RL training method that generates groups of responses and uses relative comparison for reward signals — more sample-efficient than standard PPO and not requiring a separate critic model.

### Auxiliary-Loss-Free Load Balancing
A technique to balance expert utilization in MoE models without introducing auxiliary losses that can harm training stability or model quality.

## Models and Products

### Foundation Models
- **DeepSeek-V3** (Dec 2024) — 671B MoE, 37B active params; general purpose
- **DeepSeek-V3.1** (Aug 2025) — improved multilingual, longer context

### Reasoning Models
- **DeepSeek-R1** (Jan 2025) — o1-class reasoning, RL-trained
- **DeepSeek-R1-Zero** (Jan 2025) — pure RL reasoning (no SFT)
- **R1-0528** (May 2025) — improved reasoning

### Distilled Models (from R1)
- R1-Distill-Qwen (1.5B, 7B, 14B, 32B, 70B) — based on Qwen architecture
- R1-Distill-Llama (8B, 70B) — based on Llama architecture
- These distilled models bring o1-class reasoning to consumer-grade hardware

### Specialized Models
- **DeepSeek-Coder-V2** — code generation (June 2024)
- **DeepSeekMath** — mathematical reasoning (Feb 2024)

## Corporate Structure

- **Parent**: High-Flyer Quant (Hangzhou)
- **Headquarters**: Hangzhou, Zhejiang, China
- **Founder**: Peng Zheng (High-Flyer), with research team led by DeepSeek researchers
- **Funding**: Self-funded from High-Flyer's hedge fund operations; no external VC funding
- **Note**: DeepSeek is notable as one of the few frontier AI labs without external venture capital — the high-Flyer quant fund's profits funded the entire operation

## Impact on Global AI Industry

### Nvidia Stock Impact
DeepSeek's January 2025 R1 release triggered the largest single-day drop in Nvidia's market cap, reducing it by over $600 billion in one day. The market reaction reflected concerns that:
1. Frontier AI models could be trained for orders of magnitude less than previously assumed
2. Nvidia's GPU dominance might face pressure from efficiency innovations
3. Chinese AI companies could close the gap with US firms using only controlled hardware

### OpenAI and US AI Companies Response
- OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic publicly acknowledged DeepSeek as a serious competitor
- Several US AI companies accelerated their own efficiency research in response
- The incident renewed debate about the effectiveness of US chip export controls

### Policy Implications
- US lawmakers cited DeepSeek as evidence that chip export controls were ineffective
- Simultaneously, some argued that the controls still meaningfully restricted China's access to cutting-edge chips
- DeepSeek's success with H800 chips demonstrated that software innovation can partially compensate for hardware constraints
