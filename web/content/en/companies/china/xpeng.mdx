# XPeng Motors

XPeng Motors (Guangzhou Xiaopeng Motors Technology Co., Ltd.) is a Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer headquartered in Guangzhou, Guangdong, China, with additional offices in Mountain View, California and Munich, Germany. Founded on August 15, 2014 by He Xiaopeng (co-founder of UCWeb, sold to Alibaba for $4.3B in 2014), alongside Xia Heng, He Tao, and Yang Chunlei, XPeng has rapidly evolved from a technology-focused startup into a major EV manufacturer competing with Tesla, BYD, and Li Auto in the Chinese market. With 2024 revenue of CN¥40.87 billion (~$5.7B) and 190,068 vehicles delivered, XPeng distinguishes itself through advanced autonomous driving technology (XPILOT with lidar), the proprietary Turing AI chip, and strategic partnerships with Volkswagen Group (4.95% stake) and global technology companies.

## Timeline

### 2014–2018: Founding and Early Development

- **August 15, 2014**: Company founded in Guangzhou by **He Xiaopeng** (何小鹏), co-founder of UCWeb (mobile internet browser acquired by Alibaba for $4.3B in 2014), along with Xia Heng, He Tao, and Yang Chunlei. He Xiaopeng left Alibaba in 2017 to focus full-time on XPeng.
- **2014–2017**: Development period focused on EV technology research and prototyping. Company initially operated under the name Xiaopeng Motors.
- **January 2017**: Became first Chinese company to receive a government permit for road testing autonomous vehicles.
- **2018**: Started production of **XPeng G3** SUV at the Zhaoqing manufacturing base. G3 launched at CES in Las Vegas in January 2018 — making XPeng the first Chinese EV startup to debut at CES.

### 2019–2021: Product Expansion and IPO

- **2019**: P7 sedan premiered at Auto Shanghai. Company launched ride-hailing service in Guangzhou using G3 vehicles — a strategic move to accumulate autonomous driving data.
- **June 2020**: P7 deliveries began — the company's first volume-production sedan and direct competitor to Tesla Model 3 in China. The P7 featured XPILOT 2.5 with 14 cameras, 5毫米波雷达, and 12 ultrasonic sensors.
- **August 27, 2020**: NYSE IPO raised **$1.5 billion** at $15/share. Stock opened at $23, giving XPeng a ~$17B market cap on day one. Key investors included Alibaba, Coatue,idelity, and Sequoia Capital China.
- **2021**: Dual primary listing on **Hong Kong Stock Exchange** (9868.HK) in July — raising an additional ~$1.5B. First exports to Norway (P7 and G3). Launched **P5** — world's first mass-produced car with lidar (2 units at CNY 200,000 price point, competing with Tesla's vision-only approach).

### 2022–2023: Strategic Partnerships and Scale

- **2022**: Achieved 120,757 unit deliveries despite supply chain challenges from COVID-19 lockdowns. Expanded manufacturing with new Wuhan facility announced.
- **July 2023**: **Volkswagen Group invested $700 million** for a **4.99% stake** in XPeng — the beginning of a strategic technology partnership. VW committed to joint development of new EV models for the Chinese market.
- **August 2023**: Acquired **DiDi's autonomous driving unit** for $744 million — absorbing DiDi's research team and IP focused on robotaxi and advanced driver assistance systems. This gave XPeng access to DiDi's extensive driving data and autonomous driving stack.
- **2023**: Total deliveries reached 141,601 units. Volkswagen and XPeng announced plans to jointly develop two VW-branded EVs based on XPeng's SEPA 2.0 platform.

### 2024–2025: Technology Leadership and Growth

- **February 2024**: Expanded technology cooperation with Volkswagen — including joint development of advanced driver assistance and autonomous driving features, and connectivity services.
- **April 2024**: E/E Architecture (electrical/electronic architecture) technology strategic cooperation with Volkswagen China.
- **November 8, 2024**: Unveiled **Iron humanoid robot** — 5'8", 154 lbs, 60+ joints with 200 degrees of freedom. Currently deployed in XPeng production lines for quality inspection and material handling tasks.
- **2024**: Launched P7+ (November 2024) — full-size sedan featuring XPeng's new **"vision-only" autonomous driving** system (without lidar), using 4D mmWave radars and LOFIC (Lateral Overflow Integration Capacity) camera sensors. This represented a major pivot in sensor strategy.
- **2025**: Launched **Mona M03** (compact sedan), **P7+**, and **G7** with Turing AI chip (June 2025). **G6** facelift (mid-size SUV). **X9** flagship MPV continued sales.
- **January 2025**: Strategic cooperation with **BP Pulse** for charging infrastructure — XPeng and Volkswagen China vehicles gain access to BP's charging network.
- **2025**: Sales surged to **429,445 units** (2025), up 126% YoY from 190,068 in 2024 — making XPeng one of the fastest-growing Chinese EV brands.

## Key Products

### Sedans
- **Mona M03** (2024) – Compact entry-level sedan, built on cost-reduced platform
- **P7** (2020, second generation 2024) – Mid-size sedan; direct Tesla Model 3 competitor
- **P7+** (2024) – Full-size sedan; first vision-only (no lidar) XPILOT platform

### SUVs
- **G6** (2023, facelifted 2025) – Mid-size SUV; 800V architecture for ultra-fast charging
- **G7** (2025) – Mid-size coupe SUV; Turing AI chip

- **X9** (2023) – Full-size luxury MPV; smart minivan with rear-wheel steering

### Discontinued
- **P5** (2021–2024) – Compact sedan; first mass-produced lidar vehicle
- **G3** (2018–2023) – Compact SUV; initial production model

## Key Technologies

### XPILOT Autonomous Driving
XPeng's in-house autonomous driving system evolved from XPILOT 2.5 (radar + camera + lidar) to the 2024 vision-only P7+ system. The company uses a combination of HD maps, lidar (on older platforms), and vision AI. XNGP (Navigation Guided Pilot) provides point-to-point autonomous driving in urban and highway environments in major Chinese cities.

### Turing AI Chip
Launched June 2025 in G7:
- 40-core processor, dual NPUs
- 64GB LPDDR5x RAM
- 750 TOPS sparse compute capability
- Runs AI models up to 30 billion parameters
- Powers in-car assistant, autonomous driving, and productivity apps

### Battery and Charging
- **CATL** partnership for battery cells (NMC and LFP options)
- **800V architecture** on G6, G9, X9 enabling 10-80% charge in ~12 minutes
- **5C charge rate** (2025 vehicles): 10-80% in approximately 12 minutes
- 1,000+ company-owned charging stations; access to 200,000+ third-party stations
- Free lifetime charging for customers

### Manufacturing
- **Zhaoqing** – Primary wholly-owned factory
- **Wuhan** – New factory (production started 2023)
- **Guangzhou** – New factory (under construction)
- **Graz, Austria** – Partnership with **Magna Steyr** for European market production

## Subsidiary: XPeng AeroHT

XPeng AeroHT (originally HT Aero, founded 2013; renamed after XPeng investment in 2020) focuses on electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft:
- **XPeng Voyager X2** – eVTOL multicopter; received CAAC flight certification in China
- **XPeng Voyager X1** – eVTOL multicopter
- **Land Carrier** – 6x6 eVTOL carrier van (planned 2026); designed to transport the X2

He Xiaopeng has stated flying cars are a 20-year strategic bet for XPeng, potentially enabling robotaxi services in areas beyond road infrastructure.

## Volkswagen Partnership

The Volkswagen partnership is the most significant OEM alliance for XPeng:
- **July 2023**: VW purchased 4.99% stake for $700M; announced joint development of two VW-branded EVs using XPeng's SEPA 2.0 platform
- **February 2024**: Expanded cooperation to E/E architecture technology
- **April 2024**: Further strategic cooperation announced
- **January 2025**: XPeng and VW China charging networks merged
- **August 2025**: Extended to include ICE and PHEV platforms — surprising since XPeng's platform was designed for BEVs
