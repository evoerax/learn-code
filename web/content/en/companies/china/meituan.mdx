# Meituan

Meituan (美团) is a Chinese multinational technology company headquartered in Beijing, founded in **2010** by **Wang Xing** (王兴). What began as a group-buying website (Meituan.com) during the Groupon-copycat boom in China evolved into the world's largest food delivery platform, a dominant online ticket booking service (movies, travel), and a major competitor to Alibaba's Ele.me in the Chinese on-demand services market. Through the landmark **2015 merger with Dianping** (点评), Meituan became a super-app combining food delivery (Meituan Waimai), restaurant reviews (Dianping), hotel and travel booking (Meituan Travel), and flash sales (Meituan Select). With **over 500 million active users**, a dominant 70%+ share of China's food delivery market, and FY2024 revenue of **¥190 billion (~$26.7 billion)**, Meituan is a defining platform of urban Chinese daily life — rivaled only by Alibaba's Ele.me in food delivery and by Pinduoduo in social commerce.

## Timeline

### 2010–2013: Group Buying Origins and the "千团大战"

- **2010 (March)**: Wang Xing founded **Meituan.com** as a group-buying website, copying the Groupon model. Within months, hundreds of clones emerged in China's "千团大战" (thousand-group-buying-war), with an estimated 5,000+ group-buying sites competing simultaneously.
- **2010**: Meituan differentiated from competitors by being among the first to offer **full refunds** for deals that didn't meet minimum buyer thresholds — a transparency move that built consumer trust at a time when many Groupon clones were defrauding users.
- **2011**: Meituan raised **$120 million** in a round led by Sequoia Capital China, becoming one of the best-funded group-buying sites. The capital enabled aggressive national expansion as competitors were burning through cash.
- **2011**: By mid-2011, Meituan was one of the top 5 group-buying sites in China. The market began consolidating rapidly as most of the 5,000+ sites ran out of capital.
- **2012**: Meituan expanded from group buying into **online ticket booking** (movies, events, travel) — a logical extension of its merchant relationships and consumer base.
- **2013**: Meituan launched **Meituan Waimai** (美团外卖) — entering the food delivery market, which would become its most important business unit.

### 2014–2016: Food Delivery Wars and Dianping Merger

- **2014**: Meituan Waimai grew rapidly, competing head-to-head with **Ele.me** (饿了么). The two companies, along with Baidu Waimai (百度外卖, later sold to Ele.me), engaged in an aggressive subsidy war — both paying restaurants and couriers to attract users, burning through billions of dollars.
- **2015 (October)**: Meituan and **Dianping** (大众点评), the dominant restaurant review platform, announced a **merger of equals** in a deal valued at approximately $15 billion. The combined entity became **Meituan-Dianping**, led by Wang Xing as CEO. This was one of China's largest internet mergers and created a super-app with dominant positions in food delivery, restaurant reviews, and travel booking.
- **2015**: The merger triggered antitrust scrutiny from Chinese regulators but was ultimately approved. The combined company controlled ~80% of the online food delivery market.
- **2016**: Meituan-Dianping raised **$3.3 billion** at a $18 billion valuation in a round led by Tencent, DST Global, and others — one of the largest private funding rounds in Chinese internet history. Tencent's involvement formalized a strategic partnership including WeChat integration.

### 2017–2019: Platform Expansion and Antitrust

- **2017**: Meituan launched **Meituan Travel** (formerly Meituan Hotel), competing with Ctrip and Expedia for hotel bookings and travel services. Within 2 years, Meituan became China's #3 online travel agent by room nights booked.
- **2017**: Meituan launched **Little White Whale** (小象生鲜) — a fresh food supermarket chain with in-store dining and 30-minute delivery, competing with Alibaba's Hema (盒马鲜生) and JD's 7Fresh.
- **2018 (September)**: Meituan-Dianping completed its **HKEX IPO** at HK$60.60 per share, raising **$4.2 billion**. The company was valued at approximately $53 billion at IPO. The IPO roadshow coincided with China's tech sector regulatory crackdown, making the pricing challenging.
- **2018**: Meituan launched **Meituan Select** (美团优选) — a community group-buying service targeting lower-tier cities, competing with Pinduoduo's Duoduo Maicai (多多买菜). This was part of Meituan's aggressive expansion into everyday grocery and essentials delivery.
- **2019**: Meituan launched **Meituan Delivery** (美团配送), an open logistics platform offering delivery services to third-party merchants, competing with JD's logistics-as-a-service model.

### 2020–2022: COVID and Profitability Crisis

- **2020 (January-February)**: COVID-19 lockdowns dramatically increased food delivery demand — Meituan Waimai saw massive volume surges as consumers were unable to dine out. However, Meituan also faced costs for contactless delivery protocols and courier safety measures.
- **2020**: Meituan raised **$1.5 billion** in a strategic placement, with Tencent maintaining its ~18% stake and **Pony Ma (Ma Huateng) personally joining Meituan's board** — signaling Tencent's deepened commitment to the platform.
- **2020**: Meituan launched **Meituan Instashopping** (即时配送) — 30-minute delivery of supermarket and convenience store items in major cities, competing with JD's Jingxi and Alibaba's Taobao Instant Delivery.
- **2021**: Meituan posted its **largest-ever annual loss of ¥23.5 billion** (~$3.5 billion) — driven by aggressive subsidies in food delivery, the community group-buying war with Pinduoduo, and regulatory headwinds. The company's New Retail (fresh food, community group-buying) investments were particularly costly.
- **2021**: Chinese regulators imposed **antitrust fines on Meituan** for exclusivity arrangements with merchants (similar to Alibaba's 2021 fine). Meituan was fined ¥3.4 billion and ordered to stop certain exclusive arrangements.
- **2022**: Meituan began **"Quality over Quantity"** strategic shift — cutting subsidies, reducing losses in community group-buying (Meituan Select was scaled back significantly), and focusing on core food delivery profitability.

### 2023–2025: First Profit and AI Integration

- **2023 (December)**: Meituan reported its **first full-year annual profit** — net income of ¥13.9 billion (~$1.9 billion) for FY2023, driven by improved margins in food delivery (reduced subsidy spending) and strong performance in the Meituan Travel hotel booking business. This was a major milestone 13 years after founding.
- **2023**: Meituan Waimai maintained **70%+ market share** in food delivery, with Ele.me (Alibaba-backed) as the primary competitor. Average daily orders exceeded **55 million** orders.
- **2024**: Meituan launched **Meituan AI** — integrating LLM-powered features into its app: smart restaurant recommendations, conversational ordering, courier route optimization. The company also launched **Meituan Drone Delivery** (无人机配送) pilot programs in Shenzhen and Shanghai.
- **2024**: **Meituan Travel** became China's #2 online travel platform (after Ctrip/Trip.com) with strong growth in domestic hotel bookings and short-haul travel. The company reported ¥39 billion in Meituan Waimai GMV for Q4 2024 alone.
- **2024**: Meituan discontinued **Meituan Select** (community group-buying) in most cities, writing off billions in losses from the experiment — the lesson that community group-buying requires different supply chain and logistics capabilities than Meituan's core model.
- **2025**: Meituan launched **Meituan Fast-Moving** (美团快驴) — B2B restaurant supply sourcing platform, competing with Hadoop's Meishijie and JD's enterprise procurement. Also launched **24-hour delivery** service in tier-1 cities for pharmaceutical and convenience store products.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Food Delivery (Meituan Waimai)
- 70%+ market share in China's food delivery; 55+ million daily orders; 7 million+ active merchants; proprietary courier network (1 million+ registered riders)
- **Meituan Waimai**: core restaurant food delivery platform
- **Meituan Delivery**: open delivery logistics platform for third-party merchants

### In-store Services
- **Dianping (大众点评)**: restaurant reviews and discovery platform with 200+ million reviews; dominant in China for restaurant discovery
- **Meituan Instore**: in-store services including beauty, haircuts, massage, medical aesthetic — a marketplace connecting consumers with local service merchants

### Travel and Hotel (Meituan Travel)
- #3 online travel agent in China by room nights; covers hotels, air tickets, vacation packages
- Competes with Ctrip/Trip.com in domestic travel; strength in budget and mid-tier hotel segments

### New Retail and Others
- **Little White Elephant (小象超市)**: fresh food supermarket with 30-minute delivery — competes with Hema and 7Fresh
- **Meituan Bike (美团单车)**: bike and e-bike sharing — acquired Mobike in 2018 for $2.7 billion, creating China's largest bike-share operator

## Corporate Governance

- **Founder & CEO**: Wang Xing (王兴) — one of China's most celebrated tech entrepreneurs; previously co-founded校内网 (Xiaonei.com,校内SNS) and fanfou.com (Twitter-like microblog); known for his intellectual approach and ability to compete in crowded markets
- **President**: Wang Hui (王慧文) — longtime Meituan executive, led New Retail and food delivery divisions; stepped back 2022
- **Headquarters**: Beijing, China
- **Listed**: HKEX (3690.HK, since 2018)
- **Employees**: ~200,000 (2024, including 1M+ registered couriers not on W-2)
- **Key shareholders**: Tencent ~18%, founder Wang Xing ~8%, other institutional investors
