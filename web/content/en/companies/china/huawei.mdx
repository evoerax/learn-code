# Huawei

Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd. (华为技术有限公司) is a Chinese multinational technology company headquartered in Shenzhen, founded in **1987** by **Ren Zhengfei** (任正非), a former engineer in the People's Liberation Army. Huawei is the world's largest telecommunications equipment manufacturer and the third-largest smartphone maker globally (as of 2024), with a diversified business spanning **5G network infrastructure**, enterprise networking, cloud computing, semiconductors, consumer electronics, and smart automotive solutions. Despite US export controls that have severely impacted its smartphone business since 2019, Huawei staged one of the most dramatic corporate comebacks in history with the **Mate 60 Pro** in August 2023 — powered by a 7nm processor manufactured by China's SMIC, demonstrating that China could produce advanced chips domestically despite US sanctions. Huawei reported **2024 revenue of approximately ¥860 billion (~$120 billion)**, with its device (consumer) business recovering strongly and enterprise cloud businesses expanding rapidly.

## Timeline

### 1987–1995: Foundation and Early Telecommunications

- **1987**: Huawei founded by **Ren Zhengfei** (born 1944, PLA engineering corps background) in Shenzhen with **¥21,000 (~$5,000)**. The company initially operated as a **reseller of phone switch gear** imported from Hong Kong, serving small businesses and remote areas that major state-owned enterprises ignored. Ren's vision: "Huawei has a solitary goal of achieving commercialization of our products."
- **1990**: Huawei began developing its own **phone switches** after realizing imported equipment was overpriced and under-supported. The company hired 100+ young engineers to reverse-engineer and improve telecommunications equipment. This "grassroots R&D" model became a defining Huawei characteristic.
- **1993**: Huawei launched the **C&C08 digital switch** — its first domestically developed product. Priced at roughly one-third of imported competitors (Nortel, Ericsson, Lucent), the C&C08 won contracts from rural Chinese telecom operators. By 1995, the C&C08 had 1 million lines installed.

### 1996–2003: International Expansion and Corporate Transformation

- **1996**: Huawei began **international expansion**, targeting markets in Hong Kong, then Africa and Latin America. The strategy: offer high quality at lower prices in markets where Western competitors were absent or overpriced.
- **1997**: Won first international contract in **Cambodia** for phone switch equipment. Expanded to Vietnam, Laos, and other Southeast Asian markets.
- **1999**: Established **joint research labs** with several Russian telecom operators. Opened R&D center in **Bengaluru, India** (later became one of Huawei's largest overseas R&D centers).
- **2000**: Revenue reached **¥10 billion (~$1.2 billion)** — becoming China's largest telecommunications equipment manufacturer. Opened first European office in Sweden.
- **2001**: Established **Huawei Technologies (USA)** in Dallas, Texas. Continued US expansion despite growing political scrutiny.
- **2003 (January)**: **Cisco Systems filed a landmark lawsuit against Huawei** in the US District Court, alleging that Huawei copied Cisco's IOS router source code and documentation. The lawsuit was settled in 2004 with Huawei acknowledging it would modify certain products, and Cisco receiving no monetary damages. The case made Huawei a household name in the US and highlighted IP theft concerns that would persist for two decades.
- **2003**: Huawei established its **handsets business** (initially as a sub-brand), producing low-end CDMA phones for the Chinese market. This would evolve into the smartphone business that eventually challenged Apple and Samsung.

### 2004–2010: Handsets, Enterprise Business, and Global 3G

- **2005**: Won first major European contract — **BT Group** (UK) selected Huawei as a supplier for its 21st Century Network modernization, valued at £10 billion over 5 years. This was controversial: BT faced political pressure but proceeded, establishing Huawei as a legitimate enterprise vendor in Western Europe.
- **2007**: Launched its first branded mobile phone, the **Huawei U8800 (Ideos)**, running Android — one of the first mid-range Android smartphones globally. The Ideos became unexpectedly popular in emerging markets.
- **2008**: Awarded **5G network trial contracts** in Europe as carriers began planning next-generation networks.
- **2010**: Entered the **Fortune Global 500** for the first time (ranked 397), with revenue exceeding $28 billion. Also launched the **Huawei Cloud** data center services.

### 2011–2018: Consumer Business Boom and the Smartphone Rise

- **2011**: Restructured into three business groups: **Carrier BG** (infrastructure), **Enterprise BG**, and **Consumer BG** (devices). This marked the formal elevation of the consumer business as a core growth driver.
- **2013**: Launched the **Ascend P6** — an ultra-thin (6.18mm) Android smartphone that became Huawei's first globally recognized premium device, selling over 10 million units. The P6 established Huawei as a serious competitor to Samsung and Apple in premium Android.
- **2014**: Launched the **Mate 7** — a large-screen (6-inch) phablet with a fingerprint sensor, battery life, and premium build. The Mate 7 became a cultural phenomenon in China, with waiting lists and grey market premiums. It established the Mate series as Huawei's flagship business phone.
- **2015**: Launched the **P9** — co-engineered with **Leica** for dual-camera technology. The P9's monochrome + color dual-camera system was widely praised and influenced the entire smartphone industry's camera strategy. By this point, Huawei had passed Xiaomi to become China's #1 smartphone brand.
- **2016**: Released the **Mate 9** and **P10** series, solidifying Huawei's premium positioning. The Mate 9 Porsche Design edition sold out immediately at €1,395.
- **2017**: Huawei overtook **Oppo and Vivo** to become China's #1 smartphone brand again. Globally, Huawei became the **third-largest smartphone maker** (after Samsung and Apple).
- **2018**: Released the **P20 Pro** — with a groundbreaking 40MP triple camera (main + monochrome + 3x telephoto) that dominated DXOMARK ratings for over a year. Huawei overtook **Apple as the world's #2 smartphone maker by volume** in Q2 2018 — the first Chinese brand to reach this position. Released **Mate 20 Pro** with in-display fingerprint and 3D face unlock. Revenue reached **¥721.2 billion (~$105 billion)**.

### 2019–2022: US Sanctions and Strategic Crisis

- **2019 (May)**: The **US Department of Commerce added Huawei to the Entity List** via an Executive Order, prohibiting US companies from supplying Huawei with technology without government approval. This cut off Google's Android services (GMS — Google Mobile Services) from new Huawei phones. The impact was severe: international sales collapsed as Huawei phones without Google Play Store, Maps, and YouTube were unattractive to consumers outside China.
- **2019 (August)**: Huawei released **HarmonyOS (鸿蒙操作系统)** — a distributed operating system designed from the ground up to work across smartphones, smart TVs, wearables, and IoT devices, reducing dependence on Android. HarmonyOS was positioned as an alternative to Android if sanctions cut off Android entirely.
- **2020 (May)**: US tightened sanctions, restricting **TSMC** from manufacturing chips for Huawei (including the Kirin 9000 series). This was existential: TSMC produced Huawei's most advanced chips. The Mate 40 Pro (with Kirin 9000) became the last flagship phone with full Google-less Android.
- **2020 (August)**: SMIC began limited production of some chips for Huawei, but could not match TSMC's advanced node capabilities.
- **2020**: Huawei sold **Honor** brand (budget smartphone sub-brand) to a consortium of 30+ Chinese agents and distributors for approximately ¥100 billion — isolating the sanctions-hit brand from the main Huawei entity to allow Honor to obtain US technology supplies independently.
- **2021**: Released the **P50 Pro** (with 4G-only Snapdragon due to chip shortages) — a flagship phone unable to compete internationally due to lack of 5G and Google services. Huawei dropped from #1 in China smartphones to #4.

### 2023–2025: The Comeback — SMIC 7nm and Recovery

- **2023 (August 29)**: Huawei surprised the world by releasing the **Mate 60 Pro** — containing the **Kirin 9000s** processor manufactured by **SMIC using a 7nm process node** — the most advanced chip ever produced in China without EUV lithography machines. The phone sold out instantly in China. US politicians called it a "slap in the face" of US sanctions — proof that export controls had not stopped China's semiconductor advancement. The Kirin 9000s was estimated to be one or two generations behind the leading edge but was a profound engineering achievement.
- **2023**: Huawei regained the **#1 smartphone maker position in China** (reclaiming from Apple/Divine) with the Mate 60 Pro driving a 50%+ revival in domestic sales.
- **2024**: Released the **Pura 70 Ultra** with a groundbreaking retractable camera lens system. Released the **Mate XT Ultimate Design** — the world's first commercially available **tri-fold smartphone** (two hinges, three panels), priced at ¥23,999 ($3,300). Also launched **HarmonyOS NEXT** (the "pure" HarmonyOS without Android compatibility layer).
- **2024**: Huawei became **China's largest enterprise cloud provider** (ahead of Alibaba Cloud) with its Huawei Cloud business growing 100%+ YoY. Launched the **Ascend 910C** AI chip — a competitor to NVIDIA's H100, reportedly being deployed by Chinese AI companies as an alternative despite US export controls.
- **2025 (Q1)**: Huawei announced **HarmonyOS PC** — the first personal computer running HarmonyOS, targeting the Chinese market. Revenue for FY2024 reached approximately **¥860 billion (~$120 billion)** with strong recovery in consumer devices and enterprise/cloud segments.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Carrier (Telecom Infrastructure)
- **5G RAN (Radio Access Network)**: Industry-leading 5G base stations, Massive MIMO antenna systems
- **5G Core**: Cloud-native 5G core network solutions
- **Optical networks (FTTH, OTN)**: Fiber optic transport equipment
- **IP networks**: routers and switches for telecom operators
- **Global services**: network planning, optimization, managed services

### Enterprise Networking
- **CloudCampus**: enterprise Wi-Fi and switching
- **CloudFabric**: data center networking (competing with Cisco)
- **HiCampus**: smart campus solutions

### Consumer Devices
- **Mate series**: Premium flagship smartphones (Mate 60 Pro, Mate XT tri-fold)
- **Pura series**: Camera-focused flagship (Pura 70 Ultra with retractable lens)
- **Nova series**: Mid-range smartphones
- **MateBook laptops**: HarmonyOS PC (2025), Windows laptops
- **Wearable devices**: Watch GT, FreeBuds earphones, Eyewear
- **Smart home**: Huawei Sound speakers, smart displays, HiLink IoT ecosystem

### Cloud & AI
- **Huawei Cloud**: IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS services; #1 enterprise cloud in China (2024)
- **Ascend AI chips**: Ascend 310 (inference) and Ascend 910 (training) — Huawei's AI semiconductor lineup
- **HI (Huawei Inside)**: Smart automotive solutions for automakers (Huawei's role as tier-1 automotive supplier)

### Automotive Solutions
- **HI (Huawei Inside)**: Full-stack smart driving solution (AITO问界 M5/M7/M9, Avatr, JAC) — Huawei provides the entire smart cockpit, autonomous driving, and electrical architecture to traditional automakers who manufacture the vehicles

## Corporate Governance

- **Founder & CEO**: Ren Zhengfei (任正非) — PLA engineering background; built Huawei from ¥21,000 to $120B+ revenue; famously reclusive but occasionally gives internal speeches
- **Rotating Chairman/CEO structure**: Huawei uses a rotating CEO system (each serving 6-month terms) among senior executives
- **Employee ownership**: Huawei is 100% employee-owned — Ren Zhengfei holds approximately 0.75% of shares directly, with the remaining 99.25% held by a union committee on behalf of employees. This unusual structure has been both a strength (long-term orientation) and a source of controversy.
- **Headquarters**: Shenzhen, Guangdong, China
- **Employees**: ~195,000 globally (2024)
- **R&D**: Huawei invests ~20%+ of revenue in R&D annually; total R&D spend in 2022 was ~€23 billion — among the highest in the world
