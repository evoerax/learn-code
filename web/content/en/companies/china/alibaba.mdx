# Alibaba

Alibaba Group Holding Limited (NYSE: BABA; 9988.HK) is China's largest e-commerce company and one of the world's largest cloud computing providers, founded by Jack Ma in 1999 in Hangzhou. With a mission to "make it easy to do business anywhere," Alibaba pioneered China's online retail ecosystem and expanded into cloud computing (Alibaba Cloud), digital payments (Alipay via Ant Group), logistics (Cainiao), entertainment (Youku, streaming), and AI. In fiscal year 2025, Alibaba reported revenue of approximately CNY 941.1 billion (~$82B). The company operates platforms serving over 1 billion consumers in China and hundreds of millions globally, though it has faced significant regulatory challenges since 2020 that reshaped its competitive position and growth trajectory.

## Timeline

### 1999–2003: Founding and Early Struggles

- **September 1999**: **Jack Ma**, together with 17 co-founders (the "18 heroes"), establishes **Alibaba.com** in a small apartment in Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province. Initial funding: $500,000 from Goldman Sachs and $20,000 from Jack Ma himself. The vision: connect Chinese small and medium enterprises (SMEs) with global buyers.
- **1999–2000**: Alibaba faces intense competition from GlobalSources.com and Tradezone.com. The company nearly runs out of cash during the dot-com bust. Jack Ma famously refused a $25M investment from SoftBank's Masayoshi Son in 2000 (though SoftBank later invested $20M in 2001).
- **2000**: Jack Ma gives famous "Olympic torch" speech to employees. SoftBank invests $20M for a 34% stake — the lifeline that saved Alibaba during the dot-com bust.
- **2003**: Jack Ma and CFO Joe Cai secretly develop **Taobao** (C2C platform) in just two months — built specifically to compete with eBay in China. eBay had acquired EachNet (40% market leader) and was considered invincible.
- **2003**: Alibaba launches **Alipay** as an escrow service for online transactions — solving the trust problem in Chinese e-commerce where credit card penetration was low.

### 2004–2010: Taobao's Victory and Growth

- **2004**: eBay withdraws from China (shut down EachNet). Taobao, with its free model and local support, won the C2C battle decisively. eBay's strategy of not customizing for China was its fatal flaw.
- **2005**: Yahoo invests **$1 billion for 40% of Alibaba** — giving Alibaba capital and Yahoo's China portal (Yahoo! China) and search technology.
- **2006**: Taobao launches **Taobao Mall (Tmall)** — a B2C platform for branded goods, later becoming Alibaba's most profitable segment.
- **2008**: Alibaba Cloud (Aliyun) is established (**September 2009**) — making Alibaba an early entrant in cloud computing in China.
- **2009**: Alibaba launches the first **Singles' Day (11.11)** shopping festival — 27 brands participated, generating CNY 52M (~$7.6M) in sales. The event would become the world's largest online shopping day.
- **2010**: Taobao Mall spun off as **Tmall.com**. Alibaba launches **AliExpress** — enabling international consumers to buy from Chinese merchants. Singles' Day 2010: CNY 936M ($145M) in 24 hours.

### 2011–2014: Restructuring and Record IPO

- **2011**: Alibaba undergoes major restructuring. **Alipay** is separated from Alibaba and transferred to Jack Ma-controlled **Ant Group** — a controversial decision that later required Alibaba to compensate Yahoo and SoftBank shareholders. Yahoo's stake diluted.
- **2012**: Alibaba achieves **1 trillion RMB ($160B) in GMV** — surpassing Amazon and eBay combined. SoftBank and Yahoo stakes partially reduced ahead of IPO.
- **2013**: Alibaba launches **Cainiao Smart Logistics Network** (joint venture with logistics partners). Alibaba invested in **AutoNavi** (mapping/navigation).
- **2014**: **September 19, 2014** — Alibaba IPO on NYSE at $68/share, raising **$25 billion** — the largest IPO in history at the time (surpassing Visa 2010). Valuation: $168B at float. Jack Ma famously rang the opening bell in a superhero costume ("Jack Ma, the warrior").
- **2014**: Alibaba launches **Alibaba Cloud** internationally. Acquires **UCWeb** (mobile browser, $4.7B) and **ChinaVision** (film/TV production, $800M — later became Alibaba Pictures).

### 2015–2019: New Retail and Ant Group Expansion

- **2015**: Jack Ma launches **"New Retail"** strategy — integrating online and offline commerce. Alibaba invests in **Sun Art Retail** (hypermarkets) and **Suning** (electronics retailer). Alibaba acquires **Ele.me** (food delivery, $1.3B).
- **2016**: Alibaba's Singles' Day hits **$17.8B** in GMV — more than Black Friday and Cyber Monday combined. Alibaba acquires ** Lazada** (Southeast Asian e-commerce, $1B).
- **2017**: Alibaba's revenue crossed **RMB 150 billion**. Alibaba launches **Damai Academy** (AI research). Alibaba Cloud becomes Asia Pacific's #1 cloud provider.
- **2018**: Alibaba reaches **$100B revenue milestone**. Jack Ma announces planned retirement (September 10, 2019 — his birthday). Daniel Zhang (CEO of Tmall) named successor. Alibaba's Singles' Day hits **$30.8B** in GMV.
- **2019**: **September 10, 2019** — Jack Ma steps down as Executive Chairman; **Daniel Zhang** (formerly CEO) becomes Chairman; **Eddie Wu Yongming** becomes CEO. Singles' Day 2019: **$38.4B** GMV in 24 hours — world's largest shopping event.

### 2020–2022: Ant Group IPO Crisis and Regulatory Crackdown

- **2020**: Alibaba achieves 1 trillion RMB in a single quarter for the first time. **October 2020** — Ant Group filed for **Shanghai + Hong Kong dual IPO** — targeting $34.5B raise at $315B valuation. The IPO was halted by Chinese regulators **2 days before listing** (November 3, 2020) — stunning the global financial markets. New regulatory guidelines for fintech were issued.
- **2020**: Chinese regulators launch **anti-monopoly investigation** against Alibaba (focused on exclusive dealing practices). Jack Ma publicly criticized Chinese regulators in a speech (October 24, 2020) — widely seen as triggering the regulatory crackdown.
- **2021**: **April 2021** — China State Administration for Market Regulation fined Alibaba **RMB 18.2 billion ($2.8B)** for requiring merchants to use Alibaba exclusively — the largest anti-monopoly fine in Chinese history. Alibaba was forced to accept merchants on competing platforms.
- **2021**: Alibaba appointed **Eddie Wu Yongming** as new CEO (December). Daniel Zhang stepped down as both CEO and Chairman. A corporate restructuring plan ("1+6+N") announced in March 2023.
- **2022**: Alibaba's market cap dropped from $850B (late 2020) to ~$200B. Ant Group underwent a restructuring plan mandated by regulators. Alibaba's revenue growth slowed significantly.

### 2023–2025: Recovery, AI Pivot, and Restructuring

- **2023**: Alibaba announced major restructuring — **"1+6+N"** organization: one holding company (Alibaba Group) and six business groups (Taobao Tmall Commerce, Alibaba Cloud, Cainiao, Global Digital Commerce, Local Services, and Digital Media & Entertainment), with ambition for independent fundraising and IPOs for each.
- **2023**: Alibaba Cloud launches **Qwen** family of AI models — a competitive alternative to GPT-4. Qwen was later open-sourced and gained significant traction among Chinese developers.
- **2023**: **September 2023** — Alibaba Cloud and others suspended partnerships after Chinese government cited the Log4Shell vulnerability disclosure as a "national security" concern.
- **2024**: Alibaba's **Singles' Day** (11.11) gross merchandise value (GMV) exceeded **CNY 500 billion ($69B)**. Alibaba launched **Wanxiang** (AI chip initiative) and invested heavily in AI infrastructure.
- **2024**: Alibaba Cloud's AI expansion — **Qwen 2.5** models, partnerships with automotive (BMW, Audi) and enterprise clients. Alibaba Cloud had 29 regions and 87 availability zones globally.
- **2025**: Alibaba demonstrates strong recovery — FY2025 revenue approximately **CNY 941.1 billion (~$82B)**. Eddie Wu Yongming, once the tech lead behind Alibaba's early platforms, became a central figure in AI strategy. Alibaba's market cap rebounds to ~$250-280B range. Cainiao logistics network expanded globally.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Taobao Tmall Commerce (China Commerce)
- **Taobao** — China's largest C2C marketplace (900M+ users)
- **Tmall** — leading B2C platform for branded goods
- **Alibaba.com** — B2B marketplace connecting Chinese SMEs with global buyers
- **AliExpress** — cross-border B2C (consumer-facing international)
- **Lazada** — Southeast Asia e-commerce platform

### Alibaba Cloud (Cloud Intelligence)
- **Elastic Compute Service (ECS)** — cloud computing
- **Object Storage Service (OSS)** — cloud storage
- **ApsaraDB (RDS)** — managed databases
- **MaxCompute** — big data processing
- **PAI** — machine learning platform
- **Qwen** — LLMs and AI models (open-source)
- **Yitian 710** — in-house ARM-based AI chip (data center)
- 29 regions, 87 availability zones globally
- Asia Pacific's #1 cloud provider

### Cainiao Smart Logistics
- Cainiao Tracking (logistics tracking)
- Cainiao Warehouse (fulfillment)
- Express delivery partnerships with SF Express, ZTO, YTO, etc.
- **Cainiao Network** serves 1+ billion annual包裹 deliveries

### Ant Group (associated company)
- **Alipay** — China's largest payment platform (900M+ users)
- **Ant Fortune** — wealth management
- **MYbank** — internet bank
- **Zhima Credit** — credit scoring

### Digital Media and Entertainment
- **Youku** — video streaming (优酷)
- **Alibaba Pictures** — film/TV production
- **Alibaba Music** — music streaming
- **UCWeb** — mobile browser

### Other Investments
- **AutoNavi** (高德) — mapping and navigation
- **Ele.me** — food delivery
- **Sun Art Retail** — hypermarkets
- **Lazada** — Southeast Asia

## Corporate Structure

- **Executive Chairman**: Joseph Tsai (co-founder, 2019–present)
- **CEO**: Eddie Wu Yongming (吴咏铭, co-founder, 2021–present)
- **Key co-founders**: Jack Ma (permanent honorary chairman), Joe Cai (CFO emeritus)
- **Major shareholders**: SoftBank (~14%), Alibaba Partnership (management, ~10%), Vanguard (~5%)
- Headquarters: **Alibaba Campus**, Hangzhou, Zhejiang, China
- Dual-listed: NYSE (BABA) and HKEX (9988)
- **"1+6+N" reorganization** (2023): Six business groups that can raise independent capital and IPO
