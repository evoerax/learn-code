# Baidu

Baidu, Inc. (百度) is China's dominant Internet search engine and a leading artificial intelligence company, founded in 2000 by Robin Li Yanhong and Eric Zhao. Headquartered in Beijing's Haidian District (the "Chinese Silicon Valley"), Baidu holds approximately 65-70% of China's search engine market and serves over 1 billion registered users. The company's name derives from a Song Dynasty poem — "Bidding for the hundreds (or countless) desires" — reflecting its mission to connect people to information. Baidu developed ERNIE (Enhanced Representation through Knowledge Integration), one of the world's most capable large language models, and operates Apollo, the world's largest open-source autonomous driving platform. With $18.6 billion in FY2024 revenue and significant investments in cloud, AI chips (Kunlun), and intelligent driving, Baidu is often called "China's Google" but has evolved into a broader AI and cloud-first company.

## Timeline

### 2000–2004: Founding and Search Dominance

- **January 2000**: Baidu founded in Beijing by Robin Li Yanhong (CEO) and Cheng Yijin (COO), initially as a Chinese-language search engine for portals like Sina and Sohu — the company operated as a B2B search provider before building its own product.
- **2000**: Sequoia Capital China invested $10 million in Baidu — one of Sequoia's first major Chinese investments.
- **June 2001**: Baidu.com launched as independent search engine with "Baidu Search" — direct-to-consumer model replacing B2B portal partnerships. Immediately differentiated from Google with features optimized for Chinese language and character sets.
- **2002**: "闪电魂" (Lightning Plan) — Baidu optimized indexing speed to deliver search results faster than Google in China. Rolled out MP3 search, image search, and localized features that Google lacked.
- **2003**: Baidu introduced "Knows" (zhidao.baidu.com) — a Q&A platform predating Quora; became enormously popular and drove significant traffic.
- **December 2003**: Baidu overtook Google to become China's #1 search engine — a position it has held continuously since.
- **2004**: Post-SARS period — Baidu's zhidao platform and mp3 search drove explosive user growth among Chinese Internet users.

### 2005–2010: NASDAQ IPO, Google's Exit, and Diversification

- **August 2005**: NASDAQ IPO at $27/share, raising $109 million. Stock surged 354% on first day of trading — one of the most successful Chinese tech IPOs at the time.
- **2006**: Wikipedia-style encyclopedia Baidu Baike launched (baike.baidu.com) — became one of the world's largest collaborative encyclopedias with 30M+ articles.
- **2006**: Google entered China with google.cn, agreeing to censorship. Began a period of direct competition.
- **2008**: Google's market share in China dropped to ~20% amid controversy over censorship. Baidu held ~60%.
- **2009**: Baidu's "Three S" strategy (Search,SNS, Shared) — early recognition that social and user-generated content were the future.
- **2010**: Google withdrew from China (redirecting to google.com.hk) following disputes over censorship and cyberattacks. Baidu inherited Google's market share, reaching ~80% of China's search market — effectively a monopoly position that lasted a decade.

### 2011–2016: Mobile Era and AI Awakening

- **2011**: Deep learning lab established — Baidu was one of the earliest Chinese companies to establish a dedicated AI research division (Baidu Research).
- **2013**: Robin Li proposed "Mobile First" strategy — company shifted from PC to mobile as smartphone penetration accelerated in China.
- **2014**: $1.7 billion invested in mobile and O2O (Online-to-Offline) services including Nuomi (group buying), iQiyi (video — now a major streaming platform).
- **2015**: Transportation business DuDi (later An Zong) launched — early experiments with ride-hailing.
- **2015**: Image search and voice search launched — first major AI-powered consumer product.
- **January 2016**: Baidu's artificial intelligence system "Xiaodu" (Duер) wins human competition in facial recognition accuracy — first AI milestone.

### 2017–2020: AI Strategy and Apollo

- **April 2017**: Apollo announced at Beijing Auto Show — open-source autonomous driving platform partnering with 50+ automakers and suppliers. Robin Li drove a Baidu autonomous vehicle on Beijing highways, causing regulatory discussions.
- **July 2017**: China's State Council issued AI development plan — Baidu designated as one of three national AI champions (alongside Tencent and Alibaba).
- **2018**: Tencent and Alibaba made significant investments in AI while Baidu consolidated its strength in search and autonomous driving.
- **2019**: ERNIE 1.0 launched — one of the world's first large-scale pre-trained language models, combining language understanding with knowledge graphs.
- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic — Baidu's AI temperature measurement and contact tracing used extensively in China. Baidu's search and map services became critical public health tools.

### 2021–2023: ERNIE 4.0 and Cloud Growth

- **March 2023**: ERNIE Bot (文心一言) launched — China's first major generative AI product, competing with ChatGPT. 33 enterprise partners at launch; 650,000+ requests in first day.
- **August 2023**: ERNIE 4.0 launched — multimodal model with significantly improved reasoning, code generation, and Chinese language mastery. Considered China's most capable LLM.
- **2023**: Baidu Cloud revenue grew 12% year-on-year — AI solutions driving enterprise adoption. iQiyi streaming service had 125M+ subscribers.

### 2024–2025: AI Monetization and Apollo Go

- **2024**: Apollo Go robotaxi service expanded to multiple Chinese cities — Wuhan became flagship operation with 500+ robotaxis, offering 24/7 driverless rides. Ranked #1 globally by trips completed.
- **2024**: ERNIE 4.0 Turbo launched — faster inference, lower cost. Integrated into Baidu's search homepage for all users.
- **2024**: Baidu reported $18.6 billion FY2024 revenue (RMB 133.5 billion), with AI cloud revenue growing 14% — its fastest-growing segment.
- **2025**: Apollo Go launched in Hong Kong — first deployment outside mainland China. Beijing approved Baidu to operate fully driverless (no safety driver) commercial robotaxis in designated zones.
- **2025**: Baidu's AI cloud infrastructure expanded to serve enterprise clients globally. ERNIE model API calls exceeded 3 trillion per day across Baidu's platform.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Core Search
- **Baidu Search** — China's largest search engine with 70%+ market share; 1B+ daily queries; AI-powered results with ERNIE integration
- **Baidu App** — super app with 600M+ MAU combining search, news feed, video (Haoshou), and mini-programs
- **Haoshou** (好看视频) — short video platform competing with Douyin/Kuaishou
- **Baidu Tieba** — China's largest online forum community (2003 launch) with 1.5B+ posts across 15M+ topics
- **Baidu Zhidao** — Q&A platform with hundreds of millions of answers

### AI and Cloud
- **ERNIE Bot** (文心一言) — Baidu's generative AI chatbot, 4th generation; integrated into search, cloud products, and enterprise solutions
- **ERNIE SDK** — API platform for enterprise developers to build AI applications
- **Baidu Cloud** — AI-enhanced public cloud with IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS; ~9% China cloud market share
- **Kunlun AI Chip** — Baidu's in-house AI accelerator chips (Kunlun 1 and 2) used for training and inference
- **Baidu AI Cloud** — smart city, smart manufacturing, and enterprise AI solutions

### Autonomous Driving (Apollo)
- **Apollo Go** — commercial robotaxi service; largest globally by trip volume; operating in Wuhan, Beijing, Shenzhen, Hong Kong
- **Apollo** — open-source autonomous driving platform (50M+ code lines, 400+ partners including Ford, Volkswagen, Hyundai, BYD)
- **Valet Parking** — automated parking solutions integrated with Chery, BYD, and other OEMs
- **ROBO-01** — JV with Jidu Auto (joint venture with Geely) for premium AI electric vehicle

### Entertainment and Other
- **iQiyi** — video streaming platform (Netflix-like) with 125M+ subscribers; Netflix-like original content model; 30M+ paying subscribers
- **iQIYI** — original productions including "The Lost Tomb," " Nirvana in Fire 2" — some of China's most-watched dramas
- **Tencent Video** competitor for Chinese premium video content

## Corporate Structure

- **Chairman & CEO**: Robin Li Yanhong (co-founder, 8.4% share, ~51% voting control via dual-class shares)
- **COO**: Dou Shen (previously in charge of AI Platform and Navigation)
- **CFO**: Rosalind Zhou (Jing Zhou)
- **Headquarters**: Baidu Campus, Haidian District, Beijing

- **Employees**: ~38,000 (2024)
- **Dual-class share structure**: Class A shares (1 vote), Class B shares (10 votes) — Robin Li controls ~51% of voting power

## Strategic Position

Baidu's strategic challenge is balancing its dominant but maturing search business against aggressive AI investment. While ERNIE Bot has established Baidu as China's LLM leader, monetization remains nascent. The Apollo robotaxi business represents a potential $10B+ opportunity if regulatory approvals accelerate. Baidu's ownership of iQiyi provides a valuable entertainment asset that competes directly with Tencent Video. The Kunlun chip division is strategically important for reducing dependency on NVIDIA exports amid US-China tech tensions.
