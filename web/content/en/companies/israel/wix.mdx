# Wix

Wix.com Ltd. (NASDAQ: WIX) is an Israeli cloud-based web development platform headquartered in Tel Aviv, founded in **2006** by **Avishai Abrahami**, **Nadav Abrahami** (brothers), and **Giora Kaplan**. What began as a solution to a simple problem — the founders wanted to build websites without writing code — has grown into a platform used by over **260 million registered users** worldwide, enabling individuals and small businesses to create professional-quality websites through a drag-and-drop interface without any coding knowledge. Wix operates on a **freemium model**, offering a free tier with Wix-branded subdomain and premium plans that remove branding, add custom domains, e-commerce capabilities, and advanced features. With **$1.76 billion in revenue** and operations across 22 global offices, Wix is one of Israel's most successful consumer SaaS companies, competing with Squarespace, GoDaddy, and WordPress.com in the website building market, while also pioneering AI-powered website creation tools.

## Timeline

### 2006–2012: Founding and Flash-Based Platform

- **2006**: **Avishai Abrahami**, **Nadav Abrahami**, and **Giora Kaplan** found **Wix** in Tel Aviv, Israel. The founders set out to democratize web design, believing that building a professional website should not require coding skills. Initial development focused on creating an intuitive visual website editor.
- **2007**: Launches **open beta** using **Adobe Flash** as the underlying technology — a bold choice that allowed rich animations and interactive elements but tied Wix to a plugin that would later become obsolete. The Flash-based editor allowed users to drag and drop elements visually, creating websites in real time.
- **2008–2010**: Rapid growth driven by the viral nature of Flash-based animations and the growing demand for web presence among small businesses. Wix's editor wins multiple design awards, establishing brand recognition in the web design community.
- **2010**: Reaches **1 million registered users** — a significant milestone demonstrating the mass-market demand for no-code website building tools.
- **2011**: Opens first international office in Dublin, Ireland — beginning Wix's transition from Israeli startup to global platform company.

### 2012–2016: HTML5 Transition and Public Markets

- **2012**: Launches **HTML5 site builder**, replacing Flash as the underlying technology. This was a critical strategic pivot — Flash was being abandoned by the broader tech industry due to mobile incompatibility, security vulnerabilities, and the rise of HTML5 as the web standard. The transition required rebuilding the entire editor from scratch while migrating millions of existing users. The transition was executed successfully and became a case study in platform technology transitions.
- **2013 (November)**: **Goes public on NASDAQ**, raising approximately **$127 million** at a $520 million valuation. The IPO provided capital for global expansion and product development.
- **2014**: Acquires **Appixia** — a mobile commerce platform, adding m-commerce capabilities to the Wix ecosystem. Also acquires **OpenRest** — strengthening backend and API capabilities.
- **2015**: Acquires **Moment.me** — a platform for creating mobile experiences and event pages, expanding Wix's use cases for social and event-driven websites.
- **2016 (February)**: Launches **Wix ADI** (Artificial Design Intelligence) — the world's first AI-powered website builder. ADI asks users a series of questions about their business and automatically generates a customized website design, dramatically reducing the time required to create a site from hours to minutes. This was a moonshot bet on AI-driven design that preceded the broader AI revolution in creative tools by several years.

### 2017–2021: Platform Expansion and E-Commerce

- **2017 (February)**: Acquires **DeviantArt** — the world's largest art community with over 50 million members — for approximately **$36 million**. The acquisition gave Wix access to a massive creative community and provided DeviantArt with a new platform after years of ownership changes. The integration was challenging, and DeviantArt later suffered a significant data breach (2018) affecting 8.5 million accounts.
- **2017**: Also acquires **Flok** — a mobile loyalty and customer engagement platform for businesses.
- **2018**: Reaches **150 million registered users** and **1.5 million premium subscribers** — demonstrating the conversion funnel from free to paid is working at scale.
- **2019**: Launches **Wix Payments** — an integrated payment processing solution, removing the need for third-party payment gateways and taking a cut of transactions directly. This positions Wix as a commerce platform, not just a website builder.
- **2020**: Acquires **Inkfrog** — a platform for creating and managing Amazon product listings, expanding Wix into the Amazon seller ecosystem.
- **2021**: Launches **Wix eCommerce** as a fully integrated online store solution — including inventory management, checkout, shipping label printing, and abandoned cart recovery. This directly competes with Shopify's core offering for small merchants.
- **2021**: Acquires **Rise.ai** — a loyalty and rewards platform for e-commerce, deepening the commerce feature set.
- **2021**: Acquires **Modalyst** — a dropshipping marketplace connecting Wix merchants with global suppliers, enabling automated product sourcing and fulfillment.

### 2022–2025: AI Web Builder, Studio, and acquisitions

- **2022**: Launches **Wix Editor** improvements with AI-powered layout suggestions and automated image enhancement — using computer vision to optimize photo quality across websites.
- **2023**: Launches **Wix Studio** — a dedicated platform for web design agencies and freelancers managing multiple client websites. Studio provides advanced collaboration tools, white-label options, and workflow automation that competing consumer-focused builders lack.
- **2023**: Announces and launches **AI Website Builder** — a next-generation tool using large language models and generative AI to create complete websites from natural language descriptions. Users describe their business and the AI generates a complete site with relevant content, images, and layout.
- **2024**: Launches **AI Chatbot Builder** — enabling Wix merchants to deploy AI-powered customer service chatbots on their websites, competing with Intercom, Drift, and other conversational AI platforms.
- **2024**: Launches **Wix Harmony** — an AI website builder that creates sites from a single prompt, integrating the AI editor, chatbot, and automated content generation into a unified product.
- **2024**: Revenue reaches **$1.76 billion** with **$138 million net income** — demonstrating that the AI investments are paying off without sacrificing profitability.
- **2025**: Acquires **Hour One** — a company specializing in AI-powered video generation using photorealistic virtual human presenters. This deepens Wix's AI video capabilities for website and e-commerce use cases.
- **2025**: Acquires **Base44** — an AI-native application development platform that may accelerate Wix's ability to add AI-driven features across its product suite.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Website Building (Core Platform)
- **Wix Editor**: The original drag-and-drop website building tool — visual editor allowing users to place elements (text, images, video, forms, menus) without code. Supports responsive design for mobile.
- **Wix ADI** (Artificial Design Intelligence): AI-powered website builder that creates customized sites based on user answers to questions. Positioned as the fastest path from idea to live website.
- **Wix Velo**: Low-code development platform (formerly Corvid) allowing developers to add custom JavaScript, access Wix APIs, and build custom backend logic — bridging the gap between no-code and developer customization.

### E-Commerce
- **Wix Stores**: Complete online store functionality — product catalog, inventory management, variants, pricing, and digital products
- **Wix Payments**: Integrated payment processing for 40+ countries, supporting credit cards, PayPal, and local payment methods (SEPA, iDEAL, Bancontact, etc.)
- **Wix Shipping**: Discounted shipping labels and tracking integration with major carriers
- **Wix POS**: Point-of-sale system for physical retail locations
- **Wix eCommerce Automated Tools**: Abandoned cart recovery, product recommendations, SEO automation, and inventory sync across channels

### AI Products
- **AI Website Builder**: Natural language → complete website generation
- **AI Chatbot Builder**: No-code chatbot creation for customer service, FAQ handling, and sales support
- **AI Text Creator**: Auto-generated copy for website pages, product descriptions, and marketing
- **AI Image Enhancer**: Auto-optimization and background removal for product photos

### Business Management
- **Wix Hotels / Wix Restaurants / Wix Bookings**: Vertical-specific templates and booking/payment systems for service businesses
- **Wix Blog**: Integrated blogging platform with SEO optimization
- **Wix SEO Hub**: Search engine optimization tools including meta tags, sitemaps, and redirect management

### Enterprise and Agency
- **Wix Studio**: Multi-site management platform for agencies — client management, white-label portals, centralized billing, and workflow automation for handling dozens or hundreds of client websites from a single dashboard

### Acquisitions in the Ecosystem
- **DeviantArt** ($36M, 2017): Art community platform — provides creative assets and access to a passionate artist community
- **Inkfrog** (2020): Amazon listing management
- **Rise.ai** (2021): Loyalty and rewards for e-commerce
- **Modalyst** (2021): Dropshipping marketplace
- **Hour One** (2025): AI video generation with virtual human presenters
- **Base44** (2025): AI-native app development

## Key Learnings from the Wix Story

- **Flash to HTML5 transition (2012)**: One of the most successful technology platform transitions in consumer SaaS history. Wix correctly identified Flash's impending obsolescence years before it became critical and invested heavily in rebuilding the core product. The transition was disruptive but executed without losing the majority of users — a testament to the company's engineering capabilities.
- **AI-first strategy (2016 ADI launch)**: Wix's 2016 launch of ADI (Artificial Design Intelligence) was years ahead of the broader AI boom. While the initial product was relatively narrow, it established Wix's engineering culture and infrastructure for AI-driven automation that would later become the foundation for the AI website generator (2023) and AI chatbot builder (2024).
- **DeviantArt acquisition misfire**: The $36M DeviantArt acquisition (2017) was strategically sound but operationally challenging. DeviantArt suffered a data breach affecting 8.5M accounts in 2018, and the cultural integration of a passionate creative community into a commercial platform was difficult. Wix eventually discontinued some DeviantArt features, and the acquisition is viewed as only partially successful.
- **Agency platform opportunity**: Wix Studio (2023) addresses a previously underserved segment — professional web design agencies managing multiple client accounts. This represents a natural upsell from individual users to teams and agencies, similar to how Mailchimp expanded from individuals to teams.
