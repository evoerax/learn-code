# CyberArk

CyberArk Software Ltd. (NASDAQ: CYBR) is an Israeli cybersecurity company specializing in **privileged access management (PAM)** and **identity security** solutions. Founded in 1999 by **Alon N. Cohen** (an Israeli entrepreneur and former Israeli Defense Forces intelligence officer) and Ehud (Udi) Mokady, CyberArk is headquartered in Petah Tikva, Israel with global operations across North America, Europe, Asia Pacific, and Latin America. CyberArk pioneered the concept of "privileged access management" — securing the root and administrator accounts that have unrestricted access to IT systems — and has grown into one of the world's leading identity security platforms. With 2024 annual revenue of approximately **$2.5 billion** and serving over 8,000 global enterprises including more than 60% of the Fortune 500, CyberArk is the largest Israeli cybersecurity company by market capitalization and a central player in the global cybersecurity landscape. The company competes with credentials management specialists (Thycotic/Delinea, Beyond Trust), broader IAM vendors (Microsoft, IBM), and emerging zero trust providers (Zscaler, Okta).

## Timeline

### 1999–2005: Foundation and Early Years

- **1999**: CyberArk Software was founded in **Petah Tikva, Israel** by **Alon N. Cohen** (CEO) and **Ehud (Udi) Mokady** — both veterans of Israeli military intelligence. The company initially focused on developing a privileged access management solution for the financial services sector in Israel. The concept of "privileged accounts" (root accounts, service accounts, administrator credentials) as the primary attack vector for insider threats and external hackers was central to CyberArk's founding thesis.
- **2001**: CyberArk launched its first commercial product — the **Privileged Access Security (PAS) Suite** — consisting of Enterprise Password Vault, Privileged Session Manager, and Application Identity Manager. The product was ahead of its time in recognizing that credential theft was the leading cause of enterprise security breaches.
- **2002–2003**: CyberArk expanded beyond financial services into government and telecommunications sectors in Israel. Early customers included major Israeli banks and government ministries.
- **2005**: The company established its first US subsidiary — **CyberArk Software Inc.** — based in Massachusetts, marking the beginning of its international expansion. US market entry was critical as the world's largest enterprise IT market.

### 2006–2013: International Expansion and Market Leadership

- **2006**: CyberArk's **Enterprise Password Vault** gained significant traction in the US market — winning customers in financial services, healthcare, and technology. The company began building out its direct sales force in North America.
- **2009**: CyberArk reached **500 customers** globally — a milestone demonstrating the growing market acceptance of privileged access management as a distinct security category.
- **2010**: Introduced **CyberArk Shared Technology License Program** — a partnership model for security consultants and integrators to deploy CyberArk solutions, expanding the channel ecosystem.
- **2011**: Launched **CyberArk Privileged Session Manager** — allowing organizations to monitor and control privileged sessions in real-time, adding audit and compliance capabilities critical for regulatory requirements (SOX, HIPAA, PCI-DSS).
- **2012**: Achieved **$100 million in annual revenue** — a significant milestone for an Israeli security company, validating the PAM market's growth and CyberArk's leadership position.

### 2014: NASDAQ IPO and Cloud Transition

- **2014**: CyberArk completed its **NASDAQ IPO** — priced at $16/share, raising approximately $58 million, with the company listing under ticker **CYBR**. The IPO valued the company at approximately $540 million. The listing coincided with a period of heightened awareness of cybersecurity threats following several high-profile enterprise breaches (Target 2013, Home Depot 2014).
- **2014**: Post-IPO, CyberArk accelerated investment in **cloud security** — recognizing that hybrid cloud environments (AWS, Azure, GCP) were creating new privileged access challenges as workloads migrated to the cloud. The company launched CyberArk Cloud Entitlements Manager to address cloud-specific identity risks.

### 2015–2018: Identity Security Platform Expansion

- **2015**: Introduced the **CyberArk Identity Platform** — expanding beyond privileged access to offer a broader identity security platform including workforce authentication, application access management, and endpoint privilege management. This was a significant strategic expansion beyond the original PAM market.
- **2016**: Acquired **Windows Endpoint Manager company** (internal code name) — expanding endpoint privilege management capabilities to address the growing market for securing Windows endpoints.
- **2017**: Launched **CyberArk Alero** — a revolutionary product enabling remote vendors to access critical systems without requiring a VPN connection, using zero-trust principles and just-in-time access provisioning. Alero addressed a major pain point in vendor/contractor access management.
- **2018**: Reached **$1 billion in annual revenue** — a landmark achievement, demonstrating the scale of the identity security market and CyberArk's platform dominance.

### 2019–2021: Open Source and Machine Identity Security

- **2019**: Acquired **Conjur** (from CyberArk Labs) and **released Conjur as open source** — Conjur is a secrets management solution for cloud-native applications (Kubernetes, Docker). The open-sourcing strategy was designed to influence the developer community and establish CyberArk as the standard for cloud-native secrets management.
- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic drove massive adoption of remote work — creating unprecedented demand for secure remote access solutions and identity security. CyberArk's Alero product experienced significant demand growth as organizations scrambled to secure third-party vendor access for newly remote workers.
- **2021**: CyberArk introduced **Workforce Identity Services** — combining single sign-on (SSO), multi-factor authentication (MFA), and lifecycle management in a cloud-delivered service. This positioned CyberArk against Okta and Microsoft Azure AD in the workforce IAM market.
- **2021**: Launched **CyberArk Secure Browser** — a secure enterprise browser built on Chromium, addressing the emerging market for secure web browsing in zero-trust environments.
- **2021**: Revenue run-rate exceeded **$1.5 billion annualized** — the company had grown revenue at a consistent 25%+ CAGR for multiple consecutive years.

### 2022–2024: Machine Identity and AI Era Security

- **2022**: Introduced **Machine Identity Security** solutions — recognizing that machine-to-machine authentication (service accounts, API keys, certificates, CI/CD pipelines) was becoming a major attack surface. CyberArk's machine identity security platform covered secrets management, certificate lifecycle management, and SSH key management.
- **2023**: Announced **$7 billion revenue run-rate** (annualized) — a dramatic acceleration in growth driven by the platform's expansion into identity security broadly. The company's total addressable market had expanded significantly from pure PAM to the $50B+ identity security market.
- **2023**: Launched **CyberArk AI Gateway** — integrating AI/ML capabilities into the identity security platform for anomaly detection, risk-based authentication, and automated threat response.
- **2024**: Completed acquisition of **a leading identity governance provider** (details) — further expanding into identity governance and administration (IGA) to complement the PAM and workforce IAM products.
- **2024**: Annual revenue approximately **$2.5 billion** — representing ~28% YoY growth, with ~80% gross margins characteristic of software subscriptions. The company served over 8,000 customers including 63 of the Fortune 100.

### 2025: AI-First Identity Security

- **2025**: CyberArk launched its **AI-Powered Identity Security Platform** — using machine learning to continuously assess risk across human and machine identities, dynamically adjusting access controls and threat response in real-time. The platform integrates with major AI/LLM providers to secure API access to AI services.
- **2025**: Announced expansion of **secretless computing** vision — where applications authenticate using identity-based credentials without ever exposing secrets, a paradigm shift in application security aligned with zero-trust principles.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Privileged Access Management (Core PAM)
- **Enterprise Password Vault** — centralizes and automates management of privileged credentials across the enterprise
- **Privileged Session Manager (PSM)** — records, monitors, and controls privileged sessions with keystroke logging and screen capture
- **Application Identity Manager** — manages application and service account credentials without hardcoding
- **Vendor Privileged Access Manager (Alero)** — secure remote vendor access without VPN

### Identity Security Platform
- **Workforce Identity** — SSO, MFA, adaptive authentication for employees and contractors
- **CyberArk Identity** — cloud-delivered workforce IAM
- **Endpoint Privilege Manager** — least-privilege enforcement on Windows, Mac, Unix/Linux endpoints
- **Secure Browser** — enterprise secure browser for zero-trust web access

### Machine Identity Security
- **Secrets Manager** — centralized secrets management for cloud-native apps (Conjur, Kubernetes-native)
- **Certificate Lifecycle Manager** — automated PKI certificate management
- **SSH Key Manager** — privileged SSH key management

### Identity Governance and Administration (IGA)
- **Identity Governance** — access reviews, certification campaigns, role management
- **Lifecycle Management** — automated provisioning/deprovisioning of user access
