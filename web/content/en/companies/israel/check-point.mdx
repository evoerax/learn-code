# Check Point

Check Point Software Technologies Ltd. is a global leader in cybersecurity, headquartered in Tel Aviv, Israel, with US headquarters in San Carlos, California. Founded in 1993 by **Gil Shwed** (then just 25 years old), Check Point pioneered the modern firewall and network security industry. Shwed, who remains CEO as of 2024 (making him one of the longest-tenured tech CEOs in the world), developed Check Point's foundational **Stateful Inspection** technology while still a student at Hebrew University — a technology that became the foundation of modern enterprise network security. Check Point went public in 1996 and grew to become one of the largest pure-play cybersecurity companies in the world, with 2024 revenue of approximately $2.4 billion and over 7,000 employees globally. The company's products span firewall, threat detection, endpoint security, cloud security, and mobile security — protecting hundreds of thousands of enterprises from cyber attacks.

## Timeline

### 1993–1999: Founding and the Firewall Revolution

- **1993**: **Gil Shwed**, Marius Nacht (co-founder), and Shlomo Kremer (third co-founder, left early) founded **Check Point Software Technologies** in Tel Aviv, Israel. Shwed was just 25 years old and had been developing network security concepts while a computer science student at Hebrew University of Jerusalem. The company's initial product was designed to solve enterprise network security problems in a pre-Internet era.
- **1994**: Check Point released **Firewall-1** — one of the first commercial stateful inspection firewalls. The key innovation was **Stateful Inspection** technology (developed by Shwed), which examined not just individual network packets but also the state of network connections — allowing only authorized traffic that was part of an established, legitimate session. This was a dramatic improvement over simple packet filtering firewalls and became the industry standard for enterprise network security. Firewall-1 was also one of the first products to offer a **graphical user interface (GUI)** for firewall management — a major usability innovation.
- **1994**: Firewall-1 won the **COMDEX Best of Show** award — Check Point's first major industry recognition.
- **1995**: Check Point established **international distribution agreements** with major IT distributors, beginning its global expansion. The company signed partnerships with companies in Europe and Asia.
- **1996**: Check Point had approximately $10 million in revenue and was growing at 100% per year.
- **1996**: Check Point went **public on NASDAQ** (ticker: CHKP) — raising approximately $47 million. The IPO valued the company at approximately $170 million. The strong public market response validated the growing importance of network security.
- **1997**: Check Point released **Check Point VPN-1** — combining firewall and virtual private network (VPN) capabilities in a single product. As enterprises began connecting to the Internet, VPN-1 became essential for secure remote access. VPN-1 was the first product to combine stateful inspection firewall with site-to-site VPN encryption.

### 1999–2006: Dot-Com Boom, Peak, and Nokia Partnership

- **1999**: At the height of the dot-com bubble, Check Point's stock reached **$239 per share** — giving the company a market capitalization of approximately **$20 billion**. At the time, this made Check Point one of the most valuable technology companies in the world (relative to revenue), illustrating the premium investors placed on cybersecurity.
- **2000**: The dot-com crash devastated Check Point's stock — shares fell from $239 to approximately **$20** in a matter of months (a 90% decline). Many dot-com era companies saw similar crashes, but Check Point's decline was particularly severe because its valuation had been so stretched.
- **2000**: Post-crash, Check Point restructured and refocused on profitability. The company shifted from a pure growth story to a sustainable business model. This period shaped Check Point's conservative financial culture — the company has historically maintained high profit margins and avoided large debt.
- **2001**: After the 9/11 terrorist attacks, demand for cybersecurity products increased significantly. Check Point benefited from heightened corporate and government awareness of network security threats.
- **2003**: Check Point released **Check Point NG (Next Generation)** — incorporating application-level gateway functionality and identity-based security into the firewall. This extended Check Point's technology from pure network perimeter security to more granular security controls.
- **2005**: Gil Shwed was named **CEO** (he had been president and CEO since founding; this formalized his role with the full CEO title). He has held the CEO position continuously since 1993 — making him one of the longest-serving tech CEOs in the world.
- **2007**: Check Point entered a major **distribution partnership with Nokia** — the Finnish telecommunications giant that was a major player in network appliances at the time. Nokia's network security appliances ran Check Point software, and the partnership extended Check Point's reach into hardware-based security solutions. Nokia's security appliance business was later sold to private equity.
- **2007**: Check Point released **ZoneAlarm** — a consumer firewall and security product for Windows PCs. ZoneAlarm became one of the most popular consumer security products, competing with Norton/Symantec in the consumer market.

### 2008–2016: acquisitions and Platform 3D

- **2009**: Check Point acquired **Nokia's security appliance business** — bringing hardware-based security appliances in-house and deepening the Nokia partnership.
- **2011**: Check Point acquired **FaceTime Communications** (for approximately $70 million) — a provider of security for real-time communications and social media applications. This gave Check Point visibility into Web 2.0 security threats (social media, instant messaging, video conferencing).
- **2012**: Check Point acquired **Simplicity Financial Technologies** (for approximately $20 million) — an Israeli startup focused on financial technology security. The acquisition reflected Check Point's strategy of building vertical-specific security capabilities.
- **2012**: Check Point's market cap was approximately **$15 billion** — recovering from the dot-com crash but still well below peak.
- **2013**: Check Point released **Check Point R75** — the first major release to incorporate advanced threat protection and anti-bot technologies, responding to the sophisticated APT (Advanced Persistent Threat) attacks that were becoming more common.
- **2014**: Check Point introduced the **Check Point ThreatCloud** — a collaborative threat intelligence network that aggregated data from sensors worldwide to identify emerging threats in real-time. This was one of the first coordinated threat intelligence platforms.
- **2015**: Check Point released **Check Point 15000** series — a new family of high-end security appliances designed for large data centers and service providers.
- **2016**: Check Point launched **Check Point Infinity Architecture** — a consolidated security architecture that protected across network, endpoint, mobile, and cloud. This was the predecessor to the later "Check Point 3D" branding and established Check Point's multi-domain security vision.

### 2017–2020: acquisitions and the SecureAnywhere Pivot

- **2018**: Check Point acquired **Dome9** (for approximately $175 million) — a cloud security posture management (CSPM) company that provided security for multi-cloud environments (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud). This acquisition positioned Check Point in the rapidly growing cloud security market.
- **2018**: Check Point acquired **ForceNock** (for approximately $30 million) — an Israeli startup focused on endpoint detection and response (EDR). This added EDR capabilities to Check Point's endpoint security portfolio.
- **2019**: Check Point acquired **Cymplify** (for approximately $30 million) — an Israeli startup providing security for IoT (Internet of Things) devices and embedded systems.
- **2019**: Check Point acquired **Hadar** (for approximately $30 million) — a startup focused on cloud security analytics. Total acquisitions in this period approached $200 million.
- **2019**: Check Point released **CloudGuard** — a comprehensive cloud security platform covering IaaS, SaaS, and container environments. CloudGuard was one of the first major cloud security platforms from a traditional network security vendor.
- **2020**: COVID-19 pandemic forced enterprises to rapidly adopt remote work. Check Point's **Check Point Remote Access VPN** solutions saw significant demand as organizations secured distributed workforces. The shift to cloud also accelerated Check Point's cloud security business.

### 2021–Present: Infinity Platform and AI Security

- **2021**: Check Point launched **Check Point Horizon** — a security operations platform combining threat prevention, incident response, and security training. Horizon was designed for MSSPs (Managed Security Service Providers) and enterprise SOCs.
- **2021**: Check Point released **Quantum** — a new family of next-generation firewalls built on a new hardware platform (Quantum Titan chips) and software architecture. Quantum firewalls were designed to handle the massive traffic volumes of 5G networks and AI-driven applications.
- **2022**: Check Point introduced **Check Point Quantum Smart Network** — an AI-powered network security platform that used machine learning for real-time threat detection and automatic policy enforcement.
- **2022**: Check Point's revenue was approximately **$2.2 billion**, with approximately $1 billion in free cash flow — demonstrating the company's high-margin, cash-generative business model.
- **2023**: Check Point launched **Infinity Platform** — a comprehensive, AI-powered security architecture that unified network, cloud, endpoint, IoT, and mobile security under a single management console. Infinity Platform used AI/ML for threat detection and included automatic threat prevention capabilities.
- **2023**: Check Point introduced **Check Point AI Na** — an AI-powered threat detection system that used machine learning models trained on Check Point's global threat intelligence data to identify novel attacks in real-time.
- **2024**: Check Point reported **FY2024 revenue of approximately $2.4 billion** with strong free cash flow. The company served over 100,000 organizations globally, including the majority of Fortune 500 companies.
- **2024**: Check Point's threat intelligence network (ThreatCloud) processed over 3 billion security events per day across sensors worldwide.
- **2025**: Check Point continued to invest in AI-driven security, with its AI-powered threat prevention becoming a key differentiator against both legacy competitors (Palo Alto Networks, Fortinet) and newer AI-native security startups.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Network Security
- **Check Point Quantum** — next-generation firewalls (NGFW) for perimeter and data center security, with Quantum Titan chips for 5G and AI workloads
- **Check Point Quantum Security Gateways** — hardware and virtual appliances for enterprise perimeter security
- **Check Point ThreatCloud** — collaborative AI-powered threat intelligence network aggregating data from global sensors

### Cloud Security
- **Check Point CloudGuard** — comprehensive cloud security platform for IaaS (AWS, Azure, GCP), SaaS, and container environments
- **CloudGuard Posture Management (CSPM)** — from Dome9 acquisition; automated cloud security compliance and misconfiguration detection
- **CloudGuard Workload Protection** — security for containers and Kubernetes environments

### Endpoint and Mobile Security
- **Check Point Endpoint Security** — endpoint protection platform (EPP) and endpoint detection and response (EDR)
- **Check Point Harmony** — integrated endpoint, mobile, and remote access security
- **Harmony Mobile** — mobile threat defense for iOS and Android

### Threat Prevention
- **Check Point Infinity Architecture** — unified security across network, cloud, endpoint, IoT, and mobile
- **Anti-Bot, Anti-Ransomware** — advanced threat prevention technologies
- **IPS (Intrusion Prevention System)** — network-based threat detection and blocking
