# Ericsson

Telefonaktiebolaget LM Ericsson (Ericsson) is a Swedish multinational telecommunications equipment and services company headquartered in Stockholm, founded in **1876** by **Lars Magnus Ericsson** (Lars Magnus Ericsson). What began as a repair shop for telegraph equipment grew into one of the world's dominant telecommunications infrastructure vendors, competing with Nokia and Huawei for global market share in radio access networks (RAN), core networks, and managed services. Ericsson played a defining role in the development of mobile communications standards — from the early Nordic Mobile Telephone (NMT) system in the 1980s, to the global adoption of **GSM (2G)** in the 1990s (which Ericsson's equipment powered across Europe, Asia, and Africa), to **WCDMA/3G** and **LTE/4G** leadership, and now **5G** networks globally. With FY2024 revenue of **SEK 265.7 billion (~$24.4 billion)** and operations in over 180 countries, Ericsson is a cornerstone of global telecommunications infrastructure and holds one of the telecommunications industry's most valuable patent portfolios.

## Timeline

### 1876–1945: Telegraphy Origins and Early Telephony

- **1876**: Lars Magnus Ericsson founded the company as a **repair shop for telegraph equipment** in Stockholm, Sweden. Ericsson's early business was fixing and selling telegraph equipment to the Swedish telecommunications authority.
- **1878**: Ericsson began manufacturing **telephone equipment** — including microphones, receivers, and switchboards. Sweden's small domestic market forced Ericsson to export early, building relationships across Scandinavia and Northern Europe.
- **1890s**: Ericsson expanded into telephone exchanges and became a major supplier to Swedish PTT (Post, Telegraph & Telephone) authorities across Scandinavia.
- **1918**: Ericsson merged with **Stockholms Allmänna Telefon AB** (SAT), creating a larger Swedish telecommunications manufacturer with greater scale.
- **1920s-1930s**: During the interwar period, Ericsson exported telephone equipment globally, particularly to Asia (China, Japan) and Latin America. The company survived the Great Depression better than many competitors.

### 1946–1978: Mobile Telephony Pioneer

- **1956**: Ericsson introduced the world's first **fully automatic mobile telephone system** — the **AX** (AutomXtel) system — which allowed subscribers to make calls without operator assistance. This was a pioneering step in mobile communications.
- **1960s**: Ericsson developed **radio telephone systems** for maritime and vehicular use, establishing early expertise in wireless voice transmission.
- **1970s**: Ericsson introduced the **AKD** (Automatic Cross-Connected) digital telephone exchange system, moving beyond analog to digital switching technology.

### 1979–1995: AXE Breakthrough and GSM Leadership

- **1979**: Ericsson launched the **AXE** (Automatic Exchange system) — a digital telephone switching system that became one of the most successful telecommunications switching platforms in history. AXE was sold to over 100 countries and generated massive revenue that funded Ericsson's mobile network R&D.
- **1981**: Ericsson supplied equipment for the world's first commercial **NMT** (Nordic Mobile Telephone) network — the first international mobile telephone system, operating across Sweden, Norway, Denmark, and Finland. NMT was the precursor to modern cellular networks.
- **1982**: Ericsson equipment was selected for the first **NMT 450** network (operating at 450 MHz), expanding mobile coverage to rural areas in Scandinavia.
- **1987**: Ericsson was selected as a major supplier for **GSM** (Global System for Mobile Communications) — the 2G standard that would become the foundation of all digital mobile communications globally. Ericsson's AXE platform was adapted to support GSM switching.
- **1990**: Ericsson held a dominant **40%+ global market share** in mobile telecommunications infrastructure — primarily due to GSM deployment across Europe, which was mandated to use GSM equipment.
- **1991**: Ericsson's first generation of **digital mobile phones** (the Hotline and Cityman lines) entered the market — these were among the first commercial digital mobile handsets.
- **1994**: Ericsson's DNA (Finnish mobile operator) launched the world's first commercial **GSM 1800** network — the first use of the DCS-1800 (1800 MHz) spectrum for GSM.

### 1996–2005: Global Dominance and Handset Struggles

- **1996**: Ericsson was the **world's largest mobile phone manufacturer** by volume — ahead of Nokia, Motorola, and Samsung. Ericsson's phones were known for quality and innovation (the Ericsson T28 was famously thin).
- **1997**: Ericsson's mobile phone division began struggling as Nokia and Samsung launched increasingly competitive products. Ericsson's operating system (EPOC, developed with Psion) was technically superior but lacked developer support compared to Nokia's Symbian.
- **1998**: Ericsson's mobile phone market share collapsed from #1 to #4 within 2 years, devastated by competition from Nokia's iconic 3310 and Samsung's design leadership.
- **2001 (October)**: Ericsson formed the **Sony Ericsson** joint venture — combining Ericsson's mobile phone business with Sony's consumer electronics expertise. Sony Ericsson was headquartered in London, and Ericsson's mobile phone division was transferred to the JV (Ericsson retained 50%).
- **2001**: Post-JV, Ericsson repositioned entirely as a **network infrastructure and services company**. The move was prescient — the network equipment market was more profitable and had higher barriers to entry.
- **2002**: Ericsson's network business boomed as **3G (WCDMA/UMTS)** network deployments accelerated globally. Ericsson's position in GSM made it the natural supplier for 3G upgrades.

### 2006–2014: LTE Leadership and Managed Services

- **2008**: Ericsson was the leading supplier for the first commercial **LTE (4G)** networks — providing core network and RAN equipment to operators in Norway, Sweden, and the US. LTE's IP-based architecture favored Ericsson's IP expertise.
- **2009**: Ericsson's **Sony Ericsson** joint venture struggled; the mobile phone market had shifted to smartphones (iPhone, Android). Sony Ericsson's Symbian-based smartphones (the Satio, Vivaz) failed to gain traction against iPhone and Android.
- **2011 (February)**: Sony acquired Ericsson's **50% stake in Sony Ericsson** for **€1.1 billion** ($1.46 billion), becoming sole owner. The brand was rebranded to **Sony Mobile Communications**. Ericsson exited the mobile phone business entirely.
- **2012**: Ericsson was awarded the **"Best LTE Infrastructure"** market share leadership globally, with LTE contracts covering all major markets including the US, Japan, South Korea, and Europe.
- **2013**: Ericsson's **OSS (Operations Support Systems)** and **Managed Services** business grew rapidly — the company operated networks for 1 billion subscribers globally through its managed services contracts.
- **2014**: Ericsson's CEO Hans Vestberg (CEO 2010-2016) led the company through a major restructuring — focusing on mobile broadband, IP networks, and cloud-native network functions.

### 2015–2020: 5G Push and Huawei Competition

- **2015**: Ericsson was the **global leader in 2G, 3G, and 4G network equipment**, with approximately 40% global market share in RAN. The company employed 116,000 people across 180 countries.
- **2016 (July)**: Ericsson CEO Hans Vestberg was ousted and replaced by **Börje Ekholm** — a move that signaled Ericsson's need to restore profitability after years of declining margins. Vestberg had invested heavily in 5G R&D but neglected near-term profitability.
- **2017**: Under Börje Ekholm, Ericsson returned to profitability through aggressive cost-cutting (18,000+ layoffs), exiting unprofitable markets, and focusing on core RAN business. The company also began its **cloud-native network functions (CNF)** transformation.
- **2017**: Ericsson's **5G NR (New Radio)** equipment was selected by all major US carriers (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) for initial 5G trials. The company held a strong position in 5G standard-essential patents.
- **2019**: Ericsson's **5G commercial contracts** reached 78 — more than any competitor. The company launched its **Ericsson Radio System** for 5G Massive MIMO deployments.
- **2020**: Ericsson's **5G** deployments accelerated globally, including landmark deals with Verizon (US), Deutsche Telekom (Germany), and SK Telecom (South Korea).

### 2021–2025: 5G Global Rollout and Open RAN

- **2021**: Ericsson held approximately **60%+ of global 5G RAN** market share (excluding China, where Huawei and ZTE dominate domestically). The company signed 136 new 5G commercial agreements.
- **2021**: Ericsson's **Patent Licensing** business (from its massive 5G patent portfolio) generated SEK 10 billion+ annually — a high-margin revenue stream.
- **2022**: Ericsson's revenue grew to **SEK 271.5 billion** (~$26 billion) — the highest in 10 years — driven by strong 5G rollout in North America and Europe.
- **2023**: Ericsson launched **Ericsson 5G Core** — a cloud-native, standalone 5G core network stack designed for network slicing and edge computing. The company also expanded its **Enterprise Mobile Networks** (private 5G for enterprises) business.
- **2023**: Ericsson's board and CEO Börje Ekholm faced **shareholder pressure** — the stock declined ~30% from 2022 peak due to concerns about slowing 5G deployment cycles in mature markets and competitive pressure from Samsung and Nokia.
- **2024**: Ericsson reached **60% of global 5G network coverage** powered by its equipment, serving 290+ live 5G networks across 100+ countries.
- **2024**: Ericsson's **Cloud Software and Services** segment grew — addressing demand for cloud-native 5G core and private 5G network solutions for enterprises.
- **2025**: Ericsson announced **Ericsson One 5G** — a unified 5G platform targeting both service providers and enterprises, integrating AI for network optimization and energy efficiency.

## Key Products and Business Segments

### Network Infrastructure
- **Radio Access Network (RAN)**: 5G NR macro and micro cells, Massive MIMO radio units, RAN Compute — global leader with ~50-60% market share in 5G RAN (ex-China)
- **5G Core (Cloud-Native)**: Standalone 5G core with network slicing, edge computing, and cloud-native functions
- **IP Networks**: Router and switch solutions for mobile backhaul and carrier Ethernet

### Digital Services
- **BSS (Business Support Systems)**: charging, billing, and customer management for telecom operators
- **OSS (Operations Support Systems)**: network management and optimization
- **Cloud-native 5G Core**: containerized, microservices-based 5G core network

### Managed Services
- Operates networks for 40+ countries' telecom operators; manages networks serving 1+ billion subscribers
- **Network Design and Optimization**: RF planning and network optimization services

### Patent Licensing
- One of the largest patent portfolios in telecommunications; 60,000+ patents; earns SEK 10+ billion annually from licensing (high margin)
- Critical for 5G standard-essential patents (SEPs) in 3GPP
