# ASML

ASML Holding N.V. (Euronext Amsterdam: ASML; Nasdaq: ASML) is a Dutch multinational semiconductor equipment manufacturing company headquartered in Veldhoven, Netherlands. As the world's sole manufacturer of extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines — the equipment essential for manufacturing the most advanced microchips — ASML occupies one of the most critical and defensible positions in the global semiconductor supply chain. With a market capitalization of approximately $527 billion (January 2026) and 2025 revenue of €32.7 billion, ASML is Europe's largest technology company by market value. Its machines are used by every leading-edge chipmaker including TSMC, Samsung, Intel, and SK Hynix to produce chips at nodes below 7nm. ASML holds approximately 83% of worldwide lithography machine sales (2025), making it one of the most dominant technology suppliers on Earth.

## Timeline

### 1984–1995: Joint Venture Origins and Independence

- **1984**: **ASML** (originally: ASM Lithography) established as a **joint venture** between **Philips** (a major Dutch electronics conglomerate) and **ASM International** (a Dutch semiconductor equipment maker). Philips contributed its chip-making equipment division; ASM International provided wafer-stepper technology. Initial team: ~30 employees from Philips and ASM.
- **1985**: ASML's first product, the **PAS 2000** stepper, was based on Philips' technology. Early years were difficult — Philips had tried and failed to compete in lithography against Nikon, Canon, and GCA.
- **1986**: ASML introduced the **PAS 2500** — its first commercially successful stepper. This established ASML's reputation for reliability.
- **1990**: ASML grew to 1,000 employees. Early success in Asia (particularly with Taiwanese chipmakers including TSMC) helped ASML gain market share against Japanese competitors.
- **1991**: ASML launched the **PAS 5500** series — a major stepper platform that became widely adopted by memory chip manufacturers. The PAS 5500 was critical in establishing ASML as a serious competitor to Nikon and Canon.
- **1994**: ASML achieved **10% global market share** in lithography equipment — breaking the Japanese near-monopoly (Nikon and Canon dominated 80%+).
- **1995**: ASML became **fully independent** from Philips and ASM International, with **dual listing** on Amsterdam and New York (Nasdaq) stock exchanges. This gave ASML access to capital for R&D investment. Philips sold its remaining 30% stake.

### 1996–2007: Global Expansion and Immersion Lithography

- **1998**: ASML surpassed GCA to become the **#2 lithography equipment maker** behind Nikon. ASML focused on precision alignment technology (water immersion) as a competitive differentiator.
- **2000**: ASML acquired **Silicon Valley Group (SVG)** for $1.6 billion — the largest acquisition at that time. SVG's T线步进器（stepper）technology complemented ASML's. This acquisition gave ASML access to the US market and Intel as a major customer. However, integration was difficult and SVG was eventually largely dissolved.
- **2001**: ASML's immersion lithography technology — which places a thin layer of water between the lens and the wafer to increase resolution — was adopted by Taiwanese chipmakers. This dramatically improved chip density. Nikon and Canon initially dismissed immersion lithography as a niche technology but were forced to catch up.
- **2003**: Nikon filed a patent infringement lawsuit against ASML over immersion lithography — the dispute was eventually settled. ASML's immersion technology became the industry standard.
- **2004**: TSMC began **90nm production** using ASML's immersion lithography. The partnership with TSMC deepened, with TSMC becoming ASML's largest customer for many years.
- **2006**: ASML launched the **TWINSCAN NXT** platform — a new generation of immersion lithography systems that became the backbone of advanced chip manufacturing.
- **2007**: ASML launched **TWINSCAN XT:1900i** — capable of 38nm resolution. IBM and Intel adopted ASML tools for leading-edge research. By 2007, ASML had 35% market share, challenging Nikon for the leadership position.

### 2008–2016: EUV Development and Acquisitions

- **2008**: ASML began serious development of **EUV lithography** (extreme ultraviolet, 13.5nm wavelength) — a technology that had been in research since the 1990s but was considered "15 years away" for decades. ASML committed €1 billion+ to EUV R&D.
- **2010**: ASML delivered the first **pre-production EUV system** (NXE:3100) to TSMC for testing. Samsung and Intel also received early EUV systems. ASML's EUV technology was proving viable, though still years from high-volume manufacturing.
- **2012**: ASML entered into a **Customer Co-Investment Program** with Intel, Samsung, and TSMC — each invested ~€1 billion for ~15% equity stakes in ASML's EUV development. This was an unprecedented move: chipmakers directly funding their supplier's R&D. Intel invested €2.5B for ~15% stake. The co-investment program provided ASML with €3.85B to fund EUV development.
- **2012**: ASML acquired **Cymer** for **$2.8 billion** (completed 2013) — Cymer was the leading manufacturer of **light sources** (lasers) used in lithography. This acquisition gave ASML control over the critical light source technology for EUV. Cymer's deep ultraviolet (DUV) light sources were already essential to ASML's machines.
- **2013**: ASML delivered the first **production-ready EUV machine** (NXE:3300) to Samsung. The machine cost ~$120 million each.
- **2015**: ASML announced the **TWINSCAN NXE:3350B** — the first EUV system designed for high-volume manufacturing (HVM). ASML achieved **40% market share** in lithography — officially surpassing Nikon as the global leader.
- **2016**: ASML acquired **Hermes Microvision (HMI)** for **$3.1 billion** — HMI was the leading supplier of **e-beam inspection** tools used to detect defects in chip patterns. This acquisition strengthened ASML's process control portfolio and added a new technology dimension to ASML's offering.

### 2017–2021: EUV Volume Production and Geopolitical Tensions

- **2017**: ASML's EUV systems reached **85 wafers per hour** throughput — making them viable for high-volume production. TSMC announced plans to use EUV in its 7nm and 5nm process nodes.
- **2018**: ASML shipped **18 EUV systems** in 2018 (up from 11 in 2017). Revenue from EUV systems exceeded €1 billion for the first time. US government (under Trump administration) **attempted to block ASML from selling EUV machines to China** — citing national security concerns. The Netherlands initially resisted US pressure.
- **2019**: TSMC began **volume production using EUV** for its 7nm+ nodes. Samsung also ramped EUV. ASML's order book for EUV systems was sold out through 2021+. ASML's backlog reached €4.2 billion.
- **2020**: ASML began **volume production of EUV systems** (TWINSCAN NXE:3400C). Shipped **31 EUV systems** in 2020. Revenue reached €14.78 billion. ASML's market cap crossed €200 billion for the first time.
- **2021**: ASML announced **High NA (Numerical Aperture) EUV** platform (EXE series) — the next generation EUV technology capable of printing even finer features. The first High NA EUV system (EXE:5000) was ordered by Intel. ASML signed a **10-year patent cross-licensing agreement** with TSMC. Dutch government granted ASML export licenses for EUV to China (US strongly objected). Revenue: €18.6 billion. ASML's backlog reached €26 billion.

### 2022–2025: High NA EUV and Geopolitical Escalation

- **2022**: ASML's EUV systems reached **170 wafers per hour** throughput. US imposed **export controls** on ASML's EUV and DUV tools to China (following Netherlands' decision). ASML estimated the restrictions would reduce annual revenue by ~€1–2 billion. Revenue: €21.1 billion. ASML's market cap briefly surpassed €300 billion.
- **2023**: Dutch government announced **additional export restrictions** on ASML's DUV tools to China (September 2023) — requiring licenses for most DUV sales to China. ASML's backlog reached a record **€40 billion** (heavily weighted toward AI chip demand). Revenue: €27.6 billion. ASML hired 5,000 new employees (total 42,000+).
- **2024**: ASML delivered the first **High NA EUV machine (EXE:5000)** to **Intel** (Q4 2024) — the first customer for the most advanced lithography system ever built. TSMC also received a High NA EUV system. ASML's EUV systems achieved **200+ wafers per hour**. ASML's market cap reached €400 billion+. US Commerce Department imposed additional export controls targeting ASML's China shipments. Revenue: €30.7 billion.
- **2025**: ASML revenue reached **€32.7 billion** with operating income of €11.3 billion. Net income: €9.61 billion. Total assets: €50.6 billion. ASML's backlog exceeded **€50 billion**. TSMC received its **first High NA EUV system** (Q4 2024, ramped into 2025). ASML held **83% of worldwide lithography machine sales**. Reports emerged that a Chinese state-backed group (with former ASML engineers) secretly built an **EUV prototype in Shenzhen** — expected to produce working chips by 2028–2030.

## Key Products

### EUV Lithography Systems (TWINSCAN NXE/EXE series)
- **TWINSCAN NXE:3600D** — current generation EUV (High Volume Manufacturing)
  - 13.5nm wavelength EUV light
  - 200+ wafers per hour throughput
  - Costs up to **$200 million** per unit
  - Weighs 180 tons
  - Requires three Boeing 747s to transport
  - Print feature sizes down to **8nm**
- **TWINSCAN EXE:5000** — **High NA EUV** (Numerical Aperture 0.55)
  - Next-generation EUV platform for sub-2nm nodes
  - Costs ~**$370 million** per unit
  - First delivered to Intel (Q4 2024)
  - Enables 2nm and below process technologies

### DUV (Deep Ultraviolet) Lithography
- **TWINSCAN NXT** series — immersion lithography (water)
  - 193nm ArF光源
  - Used for mature nodes (28nm to 7nm)
- **TWINSCAN KrF** — krypton fluoride lasers
  - Used for older nodes

### Process Control and Inspection
- **HMI (e-beam inspection)** — detect pattern defects
- **Holistics** — computational lithography and OPC

### Key Customers
- **TSMC** — largest customer (~30% of revenue), uses EUV for 7nm, 5nm, 3nm, 2nm
- **Samsung** — uses EUV for advanced nodes
- **Intel** — using High NA EUV for Intel 18A and Intel 14A processes
- **SK Hynix** — HBM memory manufacturing
- **Micron** — memory manufacturing
- **GlobalFoundries** — specialized foundries
- **SMIC** — Chinese foundry (restricted from EUV, uses DUV)
