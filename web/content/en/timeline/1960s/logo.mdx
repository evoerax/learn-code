## Origin Story

Logo was born in 1968 at MIT's Artificial Intelligence Laboratory, created by Seymour Papert, Wally Feurzeig, and Cynthia Solomon. Papert, a mathematician and educator, had worked with Jean Piaget and was deeply influenced by constructivist learning theory—the idea that children learn best by building things.

The central insight of Logo was remarkably simple yet powerful: give children a "turtle" they could command to draw pictures on screen. This made abstract mathematical concepts tangible and personal. A child telling a turtle to "forward 100" or "right 90" was, without knowing it, learning coordinate systems, angles, and procedural thinking.

Unlike earlier educational computing attempts that treated children as passive learners, Logo positioned children as creators. Papert believed this "constructionist" approach would produce deeper learning than passive instruction.

## Design Philosophy

Logo's design philosophy centered on **low floor, high ceiling, wide walls**:

- **Low floor**: Simple commands like `FORWARD 50` and `RIGHT 90` were accessible to young children
- **High ceiling**: Advanced programmers could explore recursion, complex geometry, and AI concepts
- **Wide walls**: Logo could be applied to music, robotics, simulations, and games

The turtle graphics system was Logo's most distinctive feature—a virtual (or physical) robot that could move, turn, and draw. Commands like `FD`, `BK`, `RT`, `LT` became the vocabulary of a child's first programming experience.

## Code Example

```logo
; Draw a square using the turtle
TO SQUARE :SIZE
  REPEAT 4 [
    FORWARD :SIZE
    RIGHT 90
  ]
END

; Draw a spiral using recursion
TO SPIRAL :SIZE :ANGLE
  IF :SIZE > 100 [STOP]
  FORWARD :SIZE
  RIGHT :ANGLE
  SPIRAL :SIZE + 5 :ANGLE
END

; A recursive tree
TO TREE :SIZE :LEVEL
  IF :LEVEL = 0 [STOP]
  FORWARD :SIZE
  LEFT 30
  TREE :SIZE * 0.7 :LEVEL - 1
  RIGHT 60
  TREE :SIZE * 0.7 :LEVEL - 1
  LEFT 30
  BACK :SIZE
END

SQUARE 100
SPIRAL 5 90
```
This example shows Logo's strengths: recursive procedures that generate complex patterns from simple rules.

## Influence & Legacy

**Inspired by:** LISP (recursive structure, list processing)

**Influenced:** Scratch, Blockly, Python (turtle module), LEGO Mindstorms

Logo's impact extends far beyond its own use:

1. **Scratch** (MIT 2007) is essentially Logo's spirit reborn with a modern visual block interface
2. **Python's `turtle` module** brings Logo-style graphics to one of the world's most popular languages
3. **LEGO Mindstorms** incorporated Logo-like programming for robots
4. Papert's constructionist theories continue to influence how we teach programming to children
