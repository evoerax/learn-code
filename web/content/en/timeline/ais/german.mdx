# German — Compound Words and Precision

German (Deutsch) achieves the highest information density among Latin-script European languages, with approximately 75-90% of English token cost. Famous for its compound words (Donaudampfschifffahrtsgesellschaftskapitän), German offers both efficiency through compounding and precision through compound technical terms.

## German Token Characteristics

**Compound Word System**

German creates new words by combining existing ones:
- Handschuh (hand + shoe = glove)
- Fingerspitzengefühl (finger tip + feeling = finesse)
- QUICK + SORT = QUICKSort (directly usable)

**Token/Character Ratio**

```
German: "Programmierung" (programming) → 2-3 tokens (12 characters)
English: "programming" → 1 token (11 characters)

German: "künstliche Intelligenz" → 3-4 tokens
English: "artificial intelligence" → 3 tokens
```

German compound words can actually reduce token count when the concept is expressed as a single compound.

<Callout type="info">
  German compound words are tokenized inconsistently: some are single tokens, others are split. This creates variable efficiency depending on the specific compound.
</Callout>

## Best Practices for German Prompts

### Do

- **Use compound words naturally** — "Datenstruktur" (data structure) works well
- **Keep imperatives direct** — "schreib" (write), "korrigier" (fix) are concise
- **Employ technical compounds** — German creates technical terms systematically
- **Use English terms when efficient** — API, SQL, Git are token-efficient

### Avoid

- **Overly long invented compounds** — tokenizers may split unexpected compounds
- **Excessive formality** — "würde Ihnen gerne helfen" wastes tokens
- **Translation artifacts** — don't translate English compounds word-by-word

## Compound Word Strategy

| English Phrase | German Compound | Token Efficiency |
|----------------|---------------|------------------|
| machine learning | maschinelles Lernen | Similar |
| deep learning | tiefes Lernen | Similar |
| quick sort | Schnellsortierung | More efficient |
| binary search | Binäre Suche | More efficient |

<Callout type="warning">
  German compound words that don't exist in training data may be tokenized character-by-character, losing efficiency. Stick to established compounds or use phrasal forms.
</Callout>

## Example Prompts

**Efficient (Recommended):**
```
Schreib Quick Sort in Python
```
Tokens: ~6 | Meaning: Write Quick Sort in Python

**Verbose (Less Efficient):**
```
Könnten Sie mir bitte einen schnellen Sortieralgorithmus in der Programmiersprache Python implementieren? Das wäre sehr freundlich von Ihnen.
```
Tokens: ~32 | Meaning: Same result, 5x tokens

## German in European Tech

German is the dominant language in:

- German-speaking countries (DE, AT, CH): ~100M speakers
- European technical documentation
- Engineering and manufacturing software
- Central European tech companies

<Callout type="info">
  German technical documentation is extensive and well-represented in training data. German speakers have strong access to quality AI assistance.
</Callout>

## Conclusion

German offers 75-90% of English token cost with the highest information density among Latin-script languages. For German speakers:

- Use natural compound words for technical concepts
- Keep prompts concise and direct
- English technical terms integrate well
- Established compounds are more efficient than invented ones
