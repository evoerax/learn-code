# Timeline Overview

> Programming language history is the history of ideas. Each decade brought new problems — and new languages that crystallized new ways of thinking about them.

This timeline covers **100+ programming languages** across **eight decades**, organized by the era in which they were created and the problems they were designed to solve.

## Patterns Across Decades

A few recurring tensions drive language evolution:

**Safety vs. Performance** — From C's trust-the-programmer approach to Rust's ownership system, languages constantly negotiate the tradeoff between control and correctness.

**Simplicity vs. Expressiveness** — BASIC was simple by design; APL was maximally expressive. Most languages try to find a middle ground — and disagree on where it is.

**Static vs. Dynamic Typing** — FORTRAN was statically typed; LISP was dynamic. Both traditions continue to thrive, with languages like TypeScript and mypy trying to blend the benefits.

**General-Purpose vs. Domain-Specific** — SQL, MATLAB, and PostScript solved specific domains brilliantly. C, Python, and Java aimed to solve everything.

Understanding these tensions makes the history much richer than a list of dates and names.
