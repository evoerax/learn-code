# Consensus Mechanisms

Consensus mechanisms are the protocols that allow distributed nodes to agree on the state of the blockchain. They solve the **Byzantine General's Problem** — how to achieve agreement in a network where some nodes may be faulty or malicious.

---

## Why consensus matters

In a centralized system, a single authority decides what is true. In blockchain:

- Thousands of nodes may propose different versions of history
- Some nodes may be offline, buggy, or malicious
- Consensus ensures every honest node agrees on the same state

The choice of consensus affects:
- **Security** — resistance to attacks
- **Speed** — transaction throughput and finality
- **Energy** — resources required to operate
- **Decentralization** — how many validators are needed

---

## Proof of Work (PoW)

Miners compete to solve a computationally intensive puzzle. The first to find a valid hash wins the right to add the next block.

**Pros:**
- Battle-tested security (Bitcoin has never been successfully attacked)
- Simple and well-understood
- High resistance to censorship

**Cons:**
- Massive energy consumption
- Hardware requirements limit participation
- Slower throughput

**Examples:** Bitcoin, Ethereum (pre-2022), Dogecoin, Litecoin

---

## Proof of Stake (PoS)

Validators stake (lock up) cryptocurrency as collateral. The protocol randomly selects a validator to propose the next block, with probability weighted by stake amount.

**Pros:**
- ~99% less energy than PoW
- Lower hardware barriers to participation
- Faster block times and higher throughput

**Cons:**
- "Nothing at stake" security concerns (mitigated by slashing)
- Tendency toward validator centralization
- More complex protocol design

**Examples:** Ethereum (post-2022), Cardano, Solana, Algorand

## Other consensus mechanisms

| Mechanism | Description | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Delegated PoS (DPoS)** | Token holders vote for a small number of delegates to validate | EOS, Tron |
| **Proof of Authority (PoA)** | Approved validators known by identity | VeChain, xDai |
| **Proof of History (PoH)** | Time-stamping mechanism that orders events | Solana |
| **Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT)** | Classic分布式 consensus, tolerating f faulty nodes from 3f+1 | Tendermint, Cosmos SDK |

---

## Finality types

| Type | Description | Time |
|------|-------------|------|
| **Probabilistic** | Transaction becomes more secure as more blocks are added | ~6 blocks for Bitcoin |
| **Instant** | Transaction is final immediately after confirmation | Some PoS chains |
| **Economic** | Reversing a transaction would cost more than the transaction value | Rollups |

---

## Ethereum's consensus evolution

Ethereum transitioned from PoW to PoS ("The Merge") in September 2022:

1. **PoW era (2015-2022)** — Energy intensive mining
2. **The Merge (2022)** — Transition to PoS, ~99.95% energy reduction
3. **The Surge (2023+)** — Rollup-centric scaling roadmap
